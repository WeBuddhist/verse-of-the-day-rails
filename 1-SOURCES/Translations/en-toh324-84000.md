# The Four Stanzas

> **84000 English, stored verbatim as a reference layer** (CC BY-NC-ND — do not excerpt or adapt for output; the shipped verse is our own translation, citing 84000). Tibetan source: `1-SOURCES/Text/bo-toh324.md`. Folio anchors `^F<folio>` align to the Degé Kangyur.

Homage to the Three Jewels! ^F204a

I pay homage to all victorious ones,<br>Those celebrated perfect buddhas,<br>And to their bodies<br>That possess the thirty-two supreme marks. [n1]

Where the perfect buddhas were born,<br>Where they reached awakening,<br>Where they turned the wheel of peace,<br>Where they passed into parinirvāṇa, free from defilement, ^F204b

Where those sugatas [n2] sat,<br>Where they walked and stood,<br>And where they slept like lions—<br>To all those places I bow.

I also bow to all stūpas,<br>With and without relics,<br>Above, in between, below,<br>And in the cardinal and intermediate directions.

These stanzas were uttered<br>By the victorious one Difficult to Follow, [n3]<br>The best of humans,<br>Who dwells in the northeastern direction. [n4]

Those who praise the tathāgatas<br>With these four stanzas<br>Will not go to the lower realms<br>For a thousand lifetimes. [n5]


## Notes (84000)

[n1] Kano’s Sanskrit edition reads sarva­buddhān namasyāmi jinān apratipudgalān/ śarīrāṇi ca sarveṣāṃ saṃbuddhānāṃ yaśasvinām: “I pay homage to all buddhas, the matchless victorious ones, and to the relics [or bodies] of all celebrated perfect buddhas.” This matches exactly the quotation of this stanza in the Tibetan translation of Jetāri’s Bodhi­cittotpāda­samādāna­vidhi (Toh 3968): rgyal ba gang zag bla na med pa yi/ /sangs rgyas rnams ni thams cad dang/ /rdzogs pa’i sangs rgyas grags ldan pa/ /kun gyi sku la phyag ’tshal lo.

[n2] Kano’s Sanskrit edition reads tathāgatāḥ.

[n3] The Tibetan in most witnesses reads bsten dka’ (“Difficult to Follow”), except for Lhasa, which reads bstan dka’ (“Difficult to Teach”). Kano’s edition of the Sanskrit reads duṣprasava (“Difficult to Command”?). Hidas’s edition (p. 32) reads duṣprasaha (“Difficult to Bear”).

[n4] This stanza is missing in Yongle, Lithang, Kangxi, Narthang, and Choné.

[n5] The Sanskrit in Kano’s edition reads kalpa­koṭisahasrebhir na te gacchanti durgatim, which corresponds to the Stok Palace Kangyur: bskal pa bye ba stong du ni/ de ni ngan ’gror ’gro mi ’gyur, and Phukdrak: bskal pa bye ba stong du yang/ de ni ngan ’gror ’gro mi ’gyur. “They will not go to the lower realms for ten billion eons.”
