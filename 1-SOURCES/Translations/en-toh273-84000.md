# The Noble Mahāyāna Sūtra The Twelve Buddhas

> **84000 English, stored verbatim as a reference layer** (CC BY-NC-ND — do not excerpt or adapt for output; the shipped verse is our own translation, citing 84000). Tibetan source: `1-SOURCES/Text/bo-toh273.md`. Folio anchors `^F<folio>` align to the Degé Kangyur.

[n1] Homage to all buddhas and bodhisattvas. ^F69a

Two sets of folio references have been included in this translation due to a discrepancy in volume 88 (rgyud ’bum, na) of the Degé Kangyur between the 1737 par phud printings and the late (post par phud) printings. In the latter case, an extra work, Bodhi­maṇḍasyālaṃkāra­lakṣa­dhāraṇī (Toh 508, byang chub snying po’i rgyan ’bum gyi gzungs), was added as the second text in the volume, thereby displacing the pagination of all the following texts in the same volume by 17 folios. Since the eKangyur follows the later printing, both references have been provided, with the highlighted one linking to the eKangyur viewer.

In the Toh 511 version of the text there is a slight discrepancy in the folio numbering between the 1737 par phud printings and the late (post par phud) printings of the Degé Kangyur. Although the discrepancy is irrelevant here, further details concerning this may be found in note 8 of the Toh 511 version of this text.

Thus did I hear at one time. The Blessed One was residing on Vulture Peak at Rājagṛha with a great saṅgha of one thousand two hundred and fifty monks and a great saṅgha of twelve thousand bodhisattvas that included the bodhisattva Ajita and others.  The Blessed One addressed the bodhisattva great being Maitreya, saying, “Maitreya, to the east of this buddhafield, past as many buddhafields as there are grains of dust in ten inexpressible billions of buddhafields, there is a world system called Full of Pearls. In that buddhafield there is a tathāgata, arhat, completely perfect buddha named King of Jewels Devoid of Desire Whose Supreme Emanation Has Arisen from the Expanse of Phenomena, Who Is Ornamented by Boundless Sunlight from the Top of His Crown Protuberance and by an Aspiration Like Moonlight, Whose Body Is Bedecked and Elegantly Ornamented with Offerings of Excellent Perfumes,  Who Has a Body Like a Lotus Flower Lovely as a Resplendent Blue Beryl Gemstone, the Light of the Good Qualities of Whom Are as Beautiful as a Glorious Pure Sky Free of Dust. He nurtures and teaches the Dharma to the beings who live there. Whoever remembers the name of that victorious one will renounce cyclic existence for a great eon that consists of eons equal to the number of grains of dust in Jambudvīpa. ^F36a

“That tathāgata issued the following prophecy about the bodhisattva great being Incomparable: [n2]

“ ‘As soon as the tathāgata, arhat, perfect and complete buddha King of Jewels Devoid of Desire Whose Supreme Emanation Has Arisen from the Expanse of Phenomena, Who Is Ornamented by Boundless Sunlight from the Top of His Crown Protuberance and by an Aspiration Like Moonlight, Whose Body Is Bedecked and Elegantly Ornamented with Offerings of Excellent Perfumes, Who Has a Body Like a Lotus Flower Lovely as a Resplendent Blue Beryl Gemstone, the Light of the Good Qualities of Whom Are as Beautiful as a Glorious Pure Sky Free of Dust  passes into parinirvāṇa and his teachings fade away, the bodhisattva Incomparable will attain unsurpassed, perfect, completely manifest awakening. He will appear in the world as a tathāgata, arhat, perfect and complete buddha endowed with perfect knowledge and conduct, a sugata, a knower of the world, an unsurpassed guide who tames beings, a teacher of gods and humans known as the blessed buddha Victory Banner King Whose Light Rays Illuminate All the World Realms in the Ten-Directional Unimpeded Circular Maṇḍala, Adorned with Completely Illuminating Sunlight, Endowed with a Space-Like Body Resembling Youthful Varuṇa, [n3] the Light of the Sun, a Moon Flower, [n4] and a Beautiful Golden Lotus.’ ^F27a

“You should also remember the name of the tathāgata, arhat, perfect and complete buddha of the quarter to the east from there called Stainless Light of the Entire Array, [n5] and you should recite it and prostrate to him. You should also remember the name of the tathāgata, arhat, perfect and complete buddha of the southern quarter called Thought Adorned with Eloquence, [n6] and you should direct your attention toward him. [n7] You should also remember the name of the tathāgata of the western quarter called Renowned Victor Crowned with a Clear Moon, and you should prostrate to him. You should also remember the name of the tathāgata of the northern quarter Manifesting an Array of Flowers, and you should prostrate to him. You should also remember the name of the tathāgata of the southeastern quarter called Light Maker, and you should prostrate to him. You should also remember the name of the tathāgata of the southwestern quarter called Renowned Supreme Jewel Crown, and you should prostrate to him. You should also remember the name of the tathāgata of the northwestern quarter called Fearless and All-Seeing, and you should prostrate to him. You should also remember the name of the tathāgata of the northeast quarter called Bristling with Fearless Confidence, [n8] and you should prostrate to him. You should also remember the name of the tathāgata of the nadir called Throat of the Yawning Lion, and you should prostrate to him. You should also remember the name of the tathāgata of the zenith called Fearless King Majestic Golden Radiance, and you should prostrate to him. ^F27b

“Maitreya, sons or daughters of the lineage with sincere faith should recite the names of these twelve tathāgata, arhat, perfect and complete buddhas, and over the course of ten days as they confess all their misdeeds, they should rejoice in all roots of virtue. They should make requests to all the buddhas, supplicate all the buddhas, and dedicate all these roots of virtue by dedication to the expanse of phenomena.

“All their misdeeds will be exhausted and all their karmic obscurations will be purified. ^F37a

“Later, they will obtain the excellent conditions of a buddhafield with a vast array of good qualities, the excellent conditions of the fearlessnesses, the excellent conditions of the marks of an awakened being, the excellent conditions of a saṅgha of bodhisattvas, the excellent conditions of the dhāraṇīs, and the excellent conditions of meditative concentrations.

“In the immediate, they will have the support of the excellent conditions of a pure buddhafield in accordance with their aspirations, and the excellent conditions of a spiritual teacher. Their progress toward unsurpassed complete and perfect awakening will not fall back and will never be lost. As they take rebirth in cyclic existence, they will have the support of the excellent conditions of good looks and wealth, the excellent conditions of family line, the excellent conditions of social standing, the excellent conditions of family traits, [n9] the excellent conditions of appearance, and the excellent conditions of having a retinue of people around them who are of similar disposition. ^F70b

“On this topic, it is said:

“In all lifetimes,<br>No matter where they are reborn,<br>They will be a joyful sight, a bringer of joy,<br>Radiant, have great influence,<br>And have abundant wealth.

“They should recite the mantra words of this dhāraṇī:

tadyathā akhe makhe  samanta­mukhe sautiyukte nirukte prabhe sama­yoge cita­vivarte [n10] ame khame madane vivarte samanta­guṇe [n11] satyārame yukte prayukte hili mili masale ānale came aciṭi aciṭi [n12] coce arahe bahudche [n13] māyugrahe hemavati jyotivati dharmaciti [n14] cyutapaṅke [n15] avikṣepe radhikṣa [n16] me skandha­vibhakte [n17] ^F28a

Homage to all buddhas and bodhisattvas. With the blessing of the Buddha, may these mantra words be accomplished.

“On this topic, it is said:

“The six hundred million sages<br>Who dwell in the ten directions<br>Will pay heed to those<br>Who bear these dhāraṇī verses in mind. ^F37b

“In all their rebirths<br>They will encounter a spiritual teacher,<br>And whatever little virtue they possess<br>Will accomplish all their goals.

“Those who bear these dhāraṇī verses in mind<br>Will see the buddhas seated<br>In the center of lotuses before them<br>And be wise, fortunate, and take miraculous birth.

“Their recollection, intellect, understanding,<br>Devotion, and insight will increase.<br>They will understand all they have studied,<br>And their understanding of all they have studied will not diminish. [n18] ^F71a

“Those who recite the names<br>Of these world protectors<br>Will be free from all lower rebirths<br>And quickly proceed to the higher realms;

“There they will realize<br>The supreme meditative concentrations and dhāraṇīs,<br>And they will attain supreme awakening.<br>Compared to filling a thousand million buddhafields

“With gold and making offerings with it,<br>Those who bear the names<br>Of these tathāgatas in mind<br>Will have a much greater heap of merit.

“They will always remember their past lives.<br>When they practice the conduct of a bodhisattva,<br>They will abandon all unfree states,<br>And the exquisite emergence of buddhas is achieved.

“They will serve the perfect buddhas.<br>They will obtain unsurpassed faith.<br>They will express their faith in the buddhas<br>And make supreme offerings to them. ^F28b

“Delighted by the emergence of buddhas,<br>They shall see them and have faith in these teachers.<br>They will see these lamps that illuminate the world<br>And make excellent offerings to them.

“Those who bear these names in mind<br>Will be free from cyclic existence<br>For innumerable millions of eons<br>And quickly attain awakening.

“If they bear these names in mind<br>For a total of seven days and nights,<br>The extensive vision of whoever sees these guides<br>Will be purified.

“Those who bear the names<br>Of the world protectors in mind<br>Will be worthy of veneration<br>Wherever they might take rebirth.

“Those who bear these names in mind<br>Will not fall into disrepute<br>For unimaginable millions of eons,<br>But will be famous throughout all worlds. ^F38a

“Those who bear these names in mind<br>Will be honored by the gods<br>For many eons<br>And quickly attain awakening.

“Those who bear these names in mind<br>Will not lack faith or lose insight,<br>Will give up deviation and deceit,<br>And will always see the buddhas.

“Those who bear  these names in mind<br>Will always be protected<br>By gods, gandharvas, the nāga lords,<br>Garuḍas, asuras, and guhyakas. ^F71b

“They will have excellent bodily forms and names<br>And will be born into wealthy households.<br>They will be generous to all beings<br>And will be brave, magnanimous, and selfless.

“For many millions of eons,<br>They will always have the pleasant scent<br>Of divine red sandalwood,<br>And their breath will have the scent of perfume.

“Those who bear these names in mind<br>Shall be endowed  with the melodious voice of Brahmā<br>For millions of eons<br>And maintain the status of a Dharma king. ^F29a

“Those who bear these names in mind<br>Will have divine bodies,<br>And great riches will spring from the many-million petals<br>Of the lotus of the seven royal treasures.

“Those who bear these names in mind<br>Will never find themselves<br>Without fathers, mothers, siblings, loved ones,<br>And a multitude of good people.

“If a woman has borne the names<br>Of these great sages in mind,<br>She will be freed from her feminine form<br>And become an intelligent man.

“After attaining the next human rebirth,<br>She will attain unsurpassed awakening,<br>Turn the wheel of the Dharma,<br>And easily attain nirvāṇa. [n19]

“Those who bear these names in mind<br>Cannot be harmed<br>By weapons, poison,<br>Fire, kings, or criminals.

“The thoughts of those<br>Who bear these names in mind<br>Will never be troubled<br>By Māra, mounted on his chariot with his retinue.

“Human beings who bear<br>The names of these buddhas in mind<br>Can course through space<br>Across infinite millions of buddhafields. ^F38b

“After making offerings to the buddhas who teach there,<br>They must fearlessly petition them.<br>The victors will understand their wishes<br>And issue prophecies of their awakenings.

“After they receive prophecies<br>From those buddha lords,<br>They will be exceedingly joyful<br>And have no doubt  in the Victor’s teaching. ^F72a

“Those who bear these names in mind<br>Will hear the profound Dharma<br>From all the world protectors<br>And retain all they have heard.

“Then they will perfect<br>All the perfections and the grounds,<br>The four types of fearlessness, the powers,<br>And the major and minor marks.

“They will go to the field<br>Of one of those buddhas.<br>They will realize unsurpassed, perfect,<br>Complete awakening and become a buddha. ^F29b

“A buddha could describe<br>These and the other qualities<br>Of any man or woman<br>Who bears these names in mind for an entire eon.

“People who heed this teaching<br>And learn about these excellent qualities<br>Should bear the names of these renowned<br>Perfect buddhas in mind.

“Anyone who hears the names<br>Of these buddhas who are glorious by nature,<br>Who are world protectors, supreme among human beings,<br>And celestial kings in the course of an eon,

“And those who hear and learn their names<br>Will be moved by devotion,<br>Will develop great insight into all things,<br>And will become supreme among human beings.

“What wise, insightful person,<br>Having learned the names of these great sages<br>And heeded this teaching,<br>Would not bear them in mind?”

After the Blessed One had spoken, the bodhisattva great being Maitreya, the entire retinue of monks and bodhisattvas, and the whole world with its gods, humans, asuras, and gandharvas were delighted and praised his words.

This text was translated and edited by the Indian preceptors Jinamitra and Dānaśīla along with the chief editor and translator Bandé Yeshé Dé and then finalized. [n20]  It was later revised according to the new language reform and then finalized. ^F39a

[n7] D (Toh 273, Toh 511, Toh 853), S: yid la bya. The phrase “direct your attention toward him” (yid la bya, manaskāryaḥ) is suspect since it breaks with the pattern that is repeated for all the other tathāgatas in this sequence. The Tibetan may possibly preserve a scribal error in the Sanskrit here that read some form of manas + kṛ (yid la bya) instead of the namas + kṛ (phyag bya’o) that appears in all other phrasings of the sequence of the twelve buddhas in this text. Nevertheless, we have translated this instance as yid la bya to accurately reflect the Tibetan.

[n8] D (Toh 273): ’jigs bral bag tsha ba mi mnga’ spu zing mi byed; D (Toh 511, Toh 853): ’jig bral bag tsha mi mnga’ spu zing mi byed.

[n9] D (Toh 273, Toh 511, Toh 853), S: rigs phun sum tshogs pa dang / skye ba phun sun tshogs pa dang / rus phun sum tshogs pa dang. The translation combines these three “excellent conditions” (phun sum tshogs pa) into a single clause that takes rigs (kula) to indicate the actual family line, skyes ba (jāti) to indicate that family line’s social standing, and rus (gotra) to indicate the inherited family traits.

[n11] D (Toh 273): samanta­guṇe; D (Toh 511, Toh 853): samanta­guṇi.

[n12] D (Toh 273): aciṭi aciṭi; D (Toh 511, Toh 853): aciti.

[n13] D (Toh 273): bahudche; D (Toh 511, Toh 853): bahucche.

[n14] D (Toh 273): dharmaciti; D (Toh 511): dharmacite; D (Toh 853): dharmacitte.

[n15] D (Toh 511, Toh 853): cyutapaṅke; D (Toh 273): cyuta­vaṅgke.

[n17] Tentative English translation: tadyathā akhe makhe, radiant one whose panoptic gaze is said to be fused with sunlight, whose thought is devoid of illusion, ame khame, for whom intoxicating illusion fused and performed while delighting in truth is entirely virtuous, hili mili masala ānale came aciṭi aciṭi coce, worthy one, bahudche, golden one bathed in light who bears māyu, who knows the Dharma, who is without impurity, unwavering, and furnished with a great host—deliver this to me!

[n18] D (Toh 511, Toh 853): thos pa thams cad ’dzin mi nyams; thos pa rnams ni yongs mi nyams D 273.

[n19] D (Toh 273, Toh 511): ’dod pa ji bzhin mya ngan ’da’; D (Toh 853): ’dod pa zhi bzhin mya ngan ’da’. This translation follows the reading in D (Toh 273, Toh 511). The Negi dictionary (p. 2620.1) notes that ’dod pa ji bzhin is a translation of the Sanskrit abhipriya, and Edgerton (p. 54.2) provides an example where the term is used as an adjective meaning “quite agreeable.” This translation opts instead to read ’dod pa ji bzhin as a Tibetan translation of the Sanskrit yathākāma. The variant in D (Toh 853) translates “pacify all desire, and attain nirvāṇa,” which does not make sense given the fact that the subject of the verse has already attained awakening.

[n20] The colophon to D (Toh 273) ends here, with no mention of the text being revised.
