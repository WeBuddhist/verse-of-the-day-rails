# The Two Stanza Dhāraṇī

> **84000 English, stored verbatim as a reference layer** (CC BY-NC-ND — do not excerpt or adapt for output; the shipped verse is our own translation, citing 84000). Tibetan source: `1-SOURCES/Text/bo-toh143.md`. Folio anchors `^F<folio>` align to the Degé Kangyur.

Homage to Mañjuśrī Kumārabhūta. [n1] ^F262a

Contempt toward the Buddha and the Dharma,<br>Laziness, contentment with small amounts, [n2]<br>Acting with desire and pride,<br>Regret, and parting due to being uncertain— [n3]

Such are beings’ obscurations.<br>The antidote is the teaching of the supreme vehicle<br>Through which all the faults<br>That cause hindrances are abandoned.

tadyathā | oṃ vajra­prakāra vajra­prakāra [n4] | vajra­cakra daṃṣṭrābhayānake [n5]| amale vimale | nirmale [n6] | culuke culuke [n7]| culu culu | sarva­buddhe svāhā [n8] ||

Whoever practices The Two Stanza Dhāraṇī,<br>With respect to either the words or the meaning, [n9]<br>Is a wise sublime being<br>Who will attain ten benefits—

Complete development of the elements,<br>Perfect joy at the moment of death,<br>Rebirth according to one’s wish,<br>Total recollection of former lives,

Meeting with buddhas,<br>Hearing the supreme vehicle from them,<br>Interest, along with intellect,<br>The two gates, [n10] and swift awakening. [n11]


## Notes (84000)

[n1] In IOL Tib J 63 and IOL Tib J 64, the homage is directed toward the buddhas and bodhisattvas instead of Mañjuśrī: sangs rgyas dang byang cub sems dpa’ thams chad la phyag ’tsal lo.

[n2] According to the commentary by Sundaravyūha (Toh 4002), this refers to being content with small amounts of virtue.

[n3] According to the commentary by Sundaravyūha (Toh 4002), this refers to leaving the Mahāyāna in favor of the Śrāvakayāna because one’s spiritual potential is uncertain. The Sūtrālaṃkāra­bhāṣya glosses this as “parting from the Mahāyāna by uncertain bodhisattvas.” (tatrāniyatabhedo bodhi­satvānām aniyatānāṃ mahāyānād bhedaḥ).

[n4] Kano’s Sanskrit edition (2011): oṃ vajra­prakārā vajra­prakārā.

[n5] Kano’s Sanskrit edition (2011): vajra­cakra­daṃṣṭrā bhayānake.

[n6] Degé Kangyur (Toh 611, Toh 918): nirmale; Kano’s Sanskrit edition (2011): nirmale cale culu.

[n7] Degé Kangyur (Toh 611, Toh 918): omits one culuke.

[n8] IOL Tib J 63 and IOL Tib J 64 omit the dhāraṇī. Tentative English translation: It is thus—oṃ vajra fence, vajra fence (*vajraprākāra), vajra wheel! O fearful fang! Stainless, spotless, and unsullied one (*nirmale)! culuke culuke culu culu. Hail to every buddha!

[n9] IOL Tib J 63 and IOL Tib J 64: tshIg gI don (“meaning of the words”).

[n10] Sundaravyūha’s commentary and the Sūtrālaṃkāra­bhāṣya both explain that the two gates are the gates of samādhi and dhāraṇī.

[n11] The Sanskrit manuscripts add two verses; see Kano 2011, pp. 65–66. Their translation can be found in The Secrets of the Realized Ones (Toh 47), and .
