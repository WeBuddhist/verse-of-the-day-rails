# The Noble Great Vehicle Sūtra The Teaching on the Effulgence of Light

> **84000 English, stored verbatim as a reference layer** (CC BY-NC-ND — do not excerpt or adapt for output; the shipped verse is our own translation, citing 84000). Tibetan source: `1-SOURCES/Text/bo-toh55.md`. Folio anchors `^F<folio>` align to the Degé Kangyur.

Homage to all buddhas and bodhisattvas! [n1] ^F195b

Thus did I hear at one time. The Blessed One was residing in Rājagṛha at Vulture Peak, together with a great saṅgha of five hundred monks who had all attained great independence, eight hundred billion bodhisattva great beings with only one birth remaining and led by Maitreya, and four hundred billion bodhisattva great beings led by Mañjuśrīkumārabhūta.

Present in the retinue at that time was Candraprabhakumārabhūta. [n2] He had been seated in the assembly, but he now rose from his seat, arranged his shawl over one shoulder, [n3] knelt on his right knee, and prostrated at the feet of the Blessed One. With his palms respectfully joined together, he asked, “Blessed One, which activities did the Thus-Gone One previously perform to achieve his definitive light, magnetizing light, motivating light, manifesting light, multicolored light, single-colored light, narrow light, vast light, pure light, utterly pure light, stainless light, utterly stainless light, immaculate light, gradually expanding light, bright light, utterly bright light, boundless light, utterly boundless light,  immeasurable light, utterly immeasurable light, unfathomable light, utterly unfathomable light, swift light, utterly swift light, nonabiding light, groundless light, blazing light, illuminating light, inspiring light, light that leads to perfection, unimpeded light, unwavering light, straight light, light that dwells within the infinite, light displaying the marks of form, light displaying the various marks of form, light displaying the immeasurable marks of form, light displaying the characteristics of blue, yellow, red, and white colors, light displaying the characteristics of violet color, light displaying the characteristics of crystalline color, light displaying the characteristics of the sky’s color, [n4] and other such lights? Each of those various lights is manifested mixed with light rays of five colors, and each of those blue, yellow, red, and white lights are manifested mixed with an infinite number of multicolored light rays.” ^F196a

The Blessed One [n5] replied to Candraprabhakumārabhūta [n6] in verse:

“I have achieved those various lights<br>Since I have abandoned delusion<br>Through the causes and conditions<br>Of inconceivable virtuous actions.

“Furthermore, I dwell on the path of the buddhas<br>Through a variety of practices,<br>And I manifest those mixed lights<br>Through the insight of emptiness and nonaction.

“Although external phenomena<br>Are empty and selfless,<br>Beyond actions and thoughts,<br>They appear with various particular characteristics. ^F196b

“The inner body is also<br>Empty, selfless, and beyond action, [n7]<br>And yet all kinds of sounds<br>Manifest from it.

“In the same way, I fulfill the wishes of beings<br>In accordance with their inclinations,<br>By manifesting lights of countless colors<br>Through the power of nonaction.

“Sometimes a single light ray<br>Produces two colors,<br>Each displaying<br>Higher, middle, and lower beams.

“Sometimes a single light ray<br>Produces four colors,<br>Each displaying<br>Higher, middle, and lower beams.

“Sometimes five colors manifest<br>From a single light ray,<br>Each displaying higher, middle, and lower beams;<br>This is the result of pure actions.

“Sometimes six colors manifest<br>From a single light ray,<br>Each displaying higher, middle, and lower beams;<br>This is the result of skillful means.

“Sometimes seven colors manifest<br>From a single light ray,<br>Each displaying higher, middle, and lower beams;<br>This is the result of virtuous actions.

“Sometimes eight colors manifest<br>From a single light ray,<br>Each displaying higher, middle, and lower beams;<br>This is the result of wholesome qualities.

“Sometimes nine colors manifest<br>From a single light ray,<br>Each displaying higher, middle, and lower beams;<br>This is the result of the accumulations.

“Sometimes ten colors manifest<br>From a single light ray,<br>Each displaying higher, middle, and lower beams;<br>This is the result of generosity.

“Sometimes twenty colors manifest<br>From a single light ray,<br>Each displaying higher, middle, and lower beams;<br>This is the result of having guarded discipline.

“Sometimes thirty colors manifest<br>From a single light ray,<br>Each displaying higher, middle, and lower beams;<br>This is the result of patience.

“Sometimes forty colors manifest<br>From a single light ray,<br>Each displaying higher, middle, and lower beams;<br>This is the result of diligence. ^F197a

“Sometimes a hundred colors manifest<br>From a single light ray,<br>Each displaying higher, middle, and lower beams;<br>This is the result of equanimity.

“Sometimes forty million colors manifest<br>From a single light ray,<br>Each displaying higher, middle, and lower beams;<br>This is the result of reverence toward the Buddha.

“Sometimes fifty million colors manifest<br>From a single light ray,<br>Each displaying higher, middle, and lower beams;<br>This is the result of reverence toward the Dharma. ^F197b

“Sometimes sixty million colors manifest<br>From a single light ray,<br>Each displaying higher, middle, and lower beams;<br>This is the result of reverence toward the Saṅgha.

“Sometimes seventy million colors manifest<br>From a single light ray,<br>Each displaying higher, middle, and lower beams;<br>This is the result of respect for discipline.

“Sometimes eighty million colors manifest<br>From a single light ray,<br>Each displaying higher, middle, and lower beams;<br>This is the result of respect for concentration.

“Sometimes ninety million colors manifest<br>From a single light ray,<br>Each displaying higher, middle, and lower beams;<br>This is the result of affection toward everyone.

“Sometimes a hundred million colors manifest<br>From a single light ray,<br>Each displaying higher, middle, and lower beams;<br>This is the result of carefulness. [n9]

“Furthermore, many light rays radiate<br>From a single pore; [n10]<br>I should now tell you the names<br>Of those various lights.

“I have a light<br>Called illumination of pure clouds;<br>It arose from the accumulation<br>Of boundless roots of virtue.

“In the past, out of sympathy for all beings<br>Afflicted by various forms of diseases,<br>I gave them many kinds of medicine and restored their health;<br>I achieved that light through such causes and conditions.

“I have another light<br>Called pure eye;<br>It arose by offering<br>Lamps to the buddhas.

“I have another light<br>Called pure nose;<br>It arose by offering them<br>Perfumed water.

“I have another light<br>Called pure tongue;<br>It arose by offering them<br>Delicacies.

“I have another light<br>Called pure body;<br>It arose by offering them<br>Clothing.

“I have another light<br>Called pure mind;<br>It arose by continuously<br>Trusting the buddhas. ^F198a

“I have another light<br>Called pure form;<br>It arose by painting<br>The forms of the buddhas.

“I have another light<br>Called pure sound;<br>It arose by continuously<br>Praising the Dharma.

“I have another light<br>Called pure scent;<br>It arose by continuously<br>Revering the Saṅgha.

“I have another light<br>Called pure taste;<br>It arose by practicing generosity<br>In accordance with beings’ wishes.

“I have another light<br>Called pure touch;<br>It arose by offering<br>Perfumed ointments.

“I have another light<br>Called pure phenomena;<br>It arose by embracing<br>All phenomena.

“I have another light<br>Called pure earth;<br>It arose by sweeping the ground<br>For the Buddha and the Saṅgha.

“I have another light<br>Called pure water;<br>It arose by offering<br>Springs and wells.

“I have another light<br>Called pure fire;<br>It arose by carrying fire<br>And offering it. [n11]

“I have another light<br>Called pure air;<br>It arose through the offering<br>Made by holding fans.

“I have another light<br>Called pure aggregates;<br>It arose by offering<br>My body to the buddhas.

“I have another light<br>Called pure element;<br>It arose by continuously<br>Cultivating love.

“I have another light<br>Called pure truth;<br>It arose by permanently<br>Giving up lying.

“I have another light<br>Called pure peace; [n12]<br>It arose by continuously<br>Practicing generosity.

“I have another light<br>Called pure sound;<br>It arose by proclaiming<br>The praises of the buddhas. ^F198b

“I have another light<br>Called pure recollection;<br>It arose by proclaiming<br>The praises of absorption.

“I have another light<br>Called pure eloquence;<br>It arose by proclaiming<br>The praises of retention.

“I have another light<br>Called harmony;<br>It arose by settling<br>Conflicts.

“I have another light<br>Called revealing the meaning;<br>It arose by realizing<br>Emptiness.

“I have another light<br>Called blue color;<br>It arose by offering<br>Blue lotuses.

“I have another light<br>Called yellow color;<br>It arose by offering<br>Magnolias.

“I have another light<br>Called red color;<br>It arose by offering<br>Pearls.

“I have another light<br>Called white color;<br>It arose by offering<br>Golden parasols. [n13]

“I have another light<br>Called supreme qualities;<br>It arose by adorning the buddhas<br>With many types of colors.

“I have another light<br>Called nāga splendor;<br>It arose by offering<br>Nāga flags.

“I have another light<br>Called elephant splendor;<br>It arose by offering<br>Elephant flags.

“I have another light<br>Called king of lions;<br>It arose by offering<br>Lion flags.

“I have another light<br>Called king of bulls;<br>It arose by offering<br>Bull flags.

“I have another light<br>Called pure moon;<br>It arose by sweeping<br>The stūpas of buddhas.

“I have another light<br>Called taming the nāgas;<br>It arose by offering<br>Silken streamers. ^F199a

“I have another light<br>Called taming the yakṣas;<br>It arose through<br>Proper discrimination. [n14]

“I have another light<br>Called understanding women;<br>It arose by abandoning<br>The features of women.

“I have another light<br>Called understanding men;<br>It arose by abandoning<br>The features of men.

“I have another light<br>Called vajra strength;<br>It arose through<br>Pure knowledge of actions.

“I have another light<br>Called revealing emptiness;<br>It arose by teaching<br>Karmic ripening to the world.

“I have another light<br>Called true realization;<br>It arose by abandoning<br>Wrong views.

“I have another light<br>Called revealing the Buddha’s words;<br>It arose by proclaiming<br>The praises of the realm of phenomena.

“I have another light<br>Called abandonment of faults;<br>It arose by proclaiming<br>The praises of devotion. [n15]

“I have another light<br>Called illuminating array;<br>It arose by proclaiming<br>The praises of offering lamps.

“I have another light<br>Called abandonment of craving;<br>It arose by proclaiming<br>The praises of absorption and insight.

“I have another light<br>Called abandonment of habitual patterns;<br>It arose by abandoning<br>The knowledge of the past.

“I have another light<br>Called abandonment of attachment;<br>It arose by praising<br>The unborn wisdom.

“I have another light<br>Called abandonment of transmigration; [n16]<br>It arose by praising<br>The wisdom of the exhaustion of defilements.

“I have another light<br>Called abandonment of dwelling place;<br>It arose by praising<br>The knowledge of suffering. ^F199b

“I have another light<br>Called miraculous display of the Buddha;<br>It arose by proclaiming<br>The praises of miraculous power. [n17]

“I have another light<br>Called revealing the nonexistence of name-and-form;<br>It arose by proclaiming<br>The praises of the bases of miraculous power.

“I have another light<br>Called devotion to virtuous friends;<br>It arose by proclaiming<br>The praises of the nature of realization.

“I have another light<br>Called past limit of the eye;<br>It arose by proclaiming<br>The praises of the past limit of the eye.

“I have another light<br>Called limit of the exhaustion of the eye;<br>It arose by proclaiming<br>The praises of the exhaustion of the eye. [n18]

“I have another light<br>Called limit of existence;<br>It arose by proclaiming<br>The praises of the absence of existence.

“I have another light<br>Called absence of disintegration;<br>It arose by proclaiming<br>The praises of the absence of cessation. [n19]

“I have another light<br>Called absence of limits;<br>It arose by proclaiming<br>The praises of the absence of limits.

“I have another light<br>Called absence of marks;<br>It arose by proclaiming<br>The praises of the unconditioned. [n20]

“I have another light<br>Called unchanging;<br>It arose by proclaiming<br>The praises of the absence of distinction.

“I have another light<br>Called absence of engagement;<br>It arose by proclaiming<br>The praises of the absence of clinging.

“I have another light<br>Called absence of origination;<br>It arose by proclaiming<br>The praises of the unborn.

“I have another light<br>Called absence of birth;<br>It arose by proclaiming<br>The praises of the lack of origination.

“I have a light called undefinable<br>That ripens all beings.<br>I have a light called reality [n21]<br>That shakes ten million buddha realms. ^F200a

“I have a light called taming demons<br>Whose brilliance terrorizes demons.<br>I have a light called banner of merit;<br>Those who remember its name will abandon harm.

“I have a light called powerful banner;<br>Those who remember its name will be free from enmity.<br>I have a light called peaceful banner;<br>Those who remember its name will rid themselves of attachment.

“I have a light called banner of concentration;<br>Those who remember its name will abandon improper conduct.<br>I have a light called illustrious banner;<br>Those who remember its name will become famous. [n22]

“I have a light called delightful banner;<br>Those who remember its name will not suffer from torments.<br>I have a light called pure restraint;<br>Those who remember its name will abandon improper discipline.

“I have a light called banner of supreme perfume;<br>Those who remember its name will be free from impurity.<br>I have a light called profound Dharma;<br>Those who remember its name will be free from doubts.

“I have a light called groundless;<br>Those who remember its name will abandon existence. [n23]<br>I have a light called freedom from concepts; [n24]<br>Those who remember its name will not hold on to anything.

“I have a light called Mount Sumeru;<br>Those who remember its name will be unshakable.<br>I have a light called secret conduct;<br>Those who remember its name will be free from clinging.

“I have a light called liberated conduct;<br>Those who remember its name will be free from bondage.<br>I have a light called excellent control;<br>Those who remember its name will be pliant and gentle.

“I have a light called unmoving;<br>Those who remember its name will abandon attachment and clinging.<br>I have a light called total restraint; [n25]<br>Those who remember its name will observe perfect discipline.

“I have a light called actions of manifold virtues;<br>Those who remember its name will not be sullied.<br>I have a light called numerous benefits;<br>Those who hear its name will rid themselves of negativities.

“I have a light called vision of supreme knowledge;<br>Those who hear its name will not be deluded.<br>I have a light called seeking to benefit;<br>Those who hear its name will be free from anger. ^F200b

“I have a light called pleasing the mind;<br>Those who hear its name will experience happiness.<br>I have a light called absence of torment;<br>Those who remember its name will realize emptiness.

“I have a light called absence of essential nature; [n26]<br>Those who remember its name will transcend concepts.<br>I have a light called absence of reliance;<br>Those who remember its name will be unshakable.

“I have a light called freedom from delusion;<br>Those who remember its name will be free from hesitation.<br>I have a light called baseless;<br>Those who remember its name will rid themselves of the darkness of ignorance.

“I have a light called weariness of the inner body; [n27]<br>Those who remember its name will not take rebirth.<br>I have a light called absence of grasping;<br>Those who remember its name will abandon language. [n28]

“I have a light called absence of delusion;<br>Those who remember its name will be free from verbal expression.<br>I have a light called absence of going;<br>Those who remember its name will foresee the future.

“I have a light called total limit;<br>Those who remember its name will know the past.<br>I have a light called unequaled;<br>Those who remember its name will realize the absence of defilement.

“I have a light called noble attainment;<br>Those who remember its name will know what is supreme.<br>I have a light called stainless;<br>Those who remember its name will abandon clinging.

“I have a light called abandonment of dust;<br>Those who remember its name will be free from darkness.<br>I have a light called absence of craving;<br>Those who remember its name will give up their reliances. [n29]

“I have a light called supreme victory; [n30]<br>Those who remember its name will overcome the proponents of opposing views.<br>I have a light called youthful;<br>Those who remember its name will accomplish the six actions.

“I have a light called supreme nobility;<br>Those who remember its name will gain unimpeded insight.<br>I have a light called swift;<br>Those who remember its name will become the most accomplished monks.

“I have a light called possessing marks;<br>Those who remember its name will realize the profound Dharma.<br>I have a light called absence of marks;<br>Those who remember its name will rid themselves of pride. ^F201a

“I have a light called unborn;<br>Those who remember its name will reach the absence of attainment.<br>I have a light called recollecting the Buddha<br>That is praised by the thus-gone ones.

“By practicing various activities in the presence of many buddhas,<br>I have attained such a light.<br>The light rays emanating from the bodies of buddhas<br>Equal in number all the myriads of particles of dust

“Present in all the countless millions of buddha realms<br>As numerous as all the grains of sand present in the ocean.<br>Each of those light rays, as numerous as all those particles,<br>Is surrounded by many attendants.

“Those light rays manifest as the pure bodies of the thus-gone ones<br>In all the realms where there is no buddha,<br>To teach the utterly profound Dharma<br>And thereby establish beings in the state of forbearance.

“I have a light called Buddha<br>That causes beings to abide on the path of the buddhas.<br>I have a light called Dharma<br>Whose illumination is immaculate and stainless.

“I have a light called Saṅgha<br>That is praised by the thus-gone ones.<br>I have a light called purity<br>That is exalted and hard to find.

“I have a light called lotus [n31]<br>That benefits and ripens sentient beings.<br>I have a light called Brahmā,<br>And others called Śakra—or god—,

“moon, nāga, yakṣa,<br>asura, garuḍa,<br>king, woman,<br>girl, and boy.

“Through their virtuous qualities, those manifold light rays<br>Tame each of the beings born with corresponding fortunes,<br>Ripening all those countless millions of retinues<br>Into awakening.

“I have a light called insight,<br>And others called discipline, love,<br>compassion, joy, lamp,<br>perfumed ointment, and music.

“Those light rays are named<br>According to their respective activity<br>And were perfected by attracting<br>Countless beings. ^F201b

“I have a light called foremost [n32]<br>That is praised by the thus-gone ones<br>And was perfected<br>By continuously revering the teachings of the Buddha.

“From all his pores, the Buddha emits light rays [n33]<br>As numerous as the beings he beholds,<br>And each of those light rays<br>Is surrounded by many attendants.

“As he emits those light rays,<br>The Buddha ripens sentient beings<br>In accordance with<br>Their individual inclinations.

“Whoever rejoices and feels inspired<br>Upon hearing this teaching on those lights<br>Must already have heard this discourse<br>From many buddhas in the past.

“I have a light called exalted [n34]<br>That has eight hundred million attendants;<br>I achieved this light by offering<br>One verse of praise to the buddhas in the past.

“I have a light called absence of sorrow<br>That has eight hundred billion attendants;<br>I achieved this light by recalling<br>A single teaching of a thus-gone one.

“I have a light called utmost purity<br>That has eight hundred million attendants;<br>I achieved this light<br>By mastering a single absorption.

“In the past, a buddha<br>Called Exalted<br>Appeared in the world.<br>His lifespan was immeasurable.

“When he first reached awakening,<br>The assembly that had gathered around him<br>And to whom he taught the Dharma<br>Counted eight hundred billion members.

“At that time, in the lands of the great Jambudvīpa,<br>There was a king called Devoted to Words. [n35]<br>He had five hundred sons who were handsome,<br>Beautiful, and pleasant to behold.

“Their father, the king,<br>Was a majestic and powerful man<br>Who had sincere faith<br>In the Three Jewels. ^F202a

“Out of reverence,<br>The king offered to that blessed one<br>All the most magnificent gardens<br>That were in his possession.

“All the walkways<br>That crossed those gardens<br>Were lined with<br>Eight hundred million trees:

“Magnolia, banyan fig,<br>Kiṁśuka, goolar fig, [n36]<br>Śiriṣa, aśoka,<br>And other such trees.

“Those many trees blossomed during both summer and winter;<br>Their branches, fruits, and flowers shone with brilliant colors.<br>The sweetest fragrances arose from them<br>And perfumed the body of the Thus-Gone One.

“All the monks had a golden color.<br>They were all sitting in front of those trees.<br>They had all attained retention<br>Through great exertion.

“At that time, out of sympathy<br>For the king, his sons, and the numerous assemblies,<br>The Thus-Gone One [n37] taught them<br>The sūtra on the definitive lights.

“When he heard that sūtra,<br>The king, overwhelmed with joy,<br>Proclaimed the praises of the Thus-Gone One<br>In countless verses.

“That great king also offered the Blessed One<br>Eight hundred million magnificent bejeweled parasols,<br>Each of them decorated around its edge<br>By garlands of wish-fulfilling gems. [n38]

“Every one of them had the value<br>Of eight hundred million coins made of pure gold from the Jambu River.<br>Each of those bejeweled parasols was adorned<br>With tassels made of eight hundred million wish-fulfilling gems. [n39]

“Shining with brilliant colors,<br>Those wish-fulfilling gems emitted light continuously,<br>So that there was no distinction<br>Between day and night.

“Each of those light rays<br>Illuminated a hundred leagues,<br>And their light shone brilliantly,<br>Eclipsing the sun and the moon. ^F202b

“The four sides of the precious parasols were ornamented<br>With lions and eight hundred million jewel tassels,<br>As well as eight hundred million garlands<br>Made of precious golden threads.

“Those sublime bejeweled parasols<br>Were also adorned<br>With lattices of precious pearls<br>That intermingled in various ways.

“Those parasols completely covered the gardens,<br>And they were themselves surmounted by countless lotus parasols [n40]<br>Formed by various kinds of flowers<br>Such as jasmine, atimuktaka—also known as mucilinda—

“Udumbara, and blue lotus flowers.<br>All those parasols were beautified with precious lattices,<br>And cloths made of golden threads<br>That formed a covering canopy above.

“Furthermore, eight hundred million bejeweled thrones<br>Built with sandalwood, supported by legs made of pure gold,<br>And decorated with eight hundred million<br>Cushions made of the finest silk [n41] were set forth.

“At that time, all types of sentient beings—<br>Up to those residing at the summit of existence—<br>Had gathered in that place to hear this discourse<br>From the Thus-Gone One.

“When they heard this discourse,<br>Gods, nāgas, yakṣas, the kings of the gandharvas,<br>Mahoragas, and asuras<br>Were overwhelmed with joy.

“They proclaimed the praises of the Thus-Gone One<br>In eight thousand verses, [n42]<br>And all of them made the aspiration<br>To reach great awakening.

“Filled with faith, the gods, the nāgas,<br>The asuras, and all the other beings<br>Showered rains of mandārava flowers<br>And precious pearls as offerings.

“At that time, upon hearing this discourse,<br>All the eight hundred million powerful and majestic great gods<br>Were also overcome with joy, and made the aspiration to reach great awakening,<br>‘In the future, may we also achieve such a light!’ [n43] ^F203a

“At that moment, the Thus-Gone One<br>Knew their thoughts<br>And prophesied their awakening in these words:<br>‘In the future, you will accomplish buddhahood.’ [n44]

“At that time, upon hearing this discourse that reveals the lights in full,<br>Śakra—lord of the gods—Brahmā, and the eight hundred million members of their retinues<br>Also became overwhelmed with joy, gave rise to the mind set on awakening,<br>And received this prophecy: ‘In the future, you will accomplish buddhahood.’ [n45]

“At that time, upon hearing this discourse,<br>All the eight hundred million nāgas who had gathered there<br>Also gave rise to the mind set on awakening<br>And received a prophecy.

“At that time, upon hearing this discourse,<br>All the eight hundred million garuḍa kings who had gathered there<br>Observed the five vows<br>And also received that prophecy.

“At that time, upon hearing this discourse,<br>All the eight hundred million gandharva kings who had gathered there<br>Played a hundred thousand musical instruments, making delightful sounds<br>As offerings to the Blessed One, and also received that prophecy.

“At that time, upon hearing this discourse,<br>All the hundred million yakṣa kings developed intense faith<br>In the pure wisdom of the Buddha,<br>And all of them also received the prophecy of their awakening.

“Candraprabha, you should know that, at that time,<br>You were the king Devoted to Words,<br>Who worshiped that blessed one [n46] with those various offerings;<br>Do not think that this was someone else.

“You have already heard that sūtra in the past,<br>And this is the reason why today,<br>In this place, you once again<br>Request to hear it.

“After I pass into nirvāṇa, [n47]<br>When the wheels of the sacred Dharma are about to cease,<br>Whoever develops faith in these Dharma teachings<br>Will be able to teach this discourse on a vast scale. ^F203b

“Those who teach this discourse in the future<br>Will be the guardians of my Dharma;<br>Like leaders guiding many travelers, [n48]<br>They will be known as guardians of the precious treasure.

“Know that those who hear this discourse in the future [n49]<br>And become interested for a mere moment will be blessed by the Buddha and Mañjuśrī; [n50]<br>They will behold many buddhas, who will entrust secret teachings to them,<br>And their insight will become sharp.

“Beings who are honest, pliant, and gentle,<br>Who continuously strive to serve the buddhas,<br>Who meditate on selflessness, cultivate love, and develop patience<br>Will be interested in this discourse.

“Someone who constantly has a nonvirtuous attitude,<br>Who insatiably hankers after personal gains,<br>And who has no interest in being disengaged<br>Will not be interested in this discourse.

“Someone who worships the thus-gone ones,<br>Who properly understands the profound and sacred Dharma,<br>And who trusts in the genuine wisdom of the buddhas<br>Will be interested in this discourse.

“Someone who is distracted and faithless,<br>Who repeatedly takes the lives of others, who is hard to tame,<br>And who is enslaved by all the objects of desire<br>Will not be interested in this discourse.

“Someone who is always inclined to stay in the wilderness,<br>Who resides alone with a peaceful mind,<br>And who is without attachment to gains and friends<br>Will be interested in this discourse.

“Someone who follows unwholesome friends,<br>Who destroys their own and others’ virtues,<br>And who lets their discipline and absorption become greatly corrupted<br>Will not be interested in this discourse.

“Someone with pure aspirations<br>Who always examines phenomena with insight<br>And is guarded by virtuous friends<br>Will be interested in this discourse. ^F204a

“Someone who is strongly attached to the family household,<br>Who seeks others’ favors by giving flowers and fruits,<br>And who is dishonest and cunning [n51]<br>Will not be interested in this discourse.

“Someone who always recollects the Buddha’s kindness,<br>Who yearns for the highest roots of virtue,<br>And who sincerely [n52] dedicates their acts to awakening<br>Will be interested in this discourse.

“Someone who lusts for women and girls [n53]<br>And always aspires to frolic with women<br>Who are adorned in all kinds of splendid garments<br>Will not be interested in this discourse.

“Someone with a deep mindset who has left all dwellings,<br>Who is not sullied by objects of desire,<br>And who does not deceive others for the sake of food and drink<br>Will be interested in this discourse.

“Someone who says to everyone<br>That it is not a fault to indulge in desires<br>And who denigrates the buddhas of the three times<br>Will not be interested in this discourse.

“Someone with constant and firm trust<br>Who pursues the Dharma persistently<br>And never becomes weary or disrespectful while doing so<br>Will be interested in this discourse.

“Someone who craves women,<br>Who constantly keeps objects of his lust in mind<br>Without cultivating insight or benefiting others extensively,<br>Will not be interested in this discourse.

“Someone who lives in mountains or forests<br>And is purified by cultivating insight<br>Without any desire for the most basic necessities<br>Will be interested in this discourse.

“Someone who is always deluded and fails to understand<br>The past and future limits of the eye<br>Is bound by the demon of ignorance<br>And will therefore not be interested in this discourse.

“Someone who is always free from delusion and understands<br>The past and future limits of the eye<br>Is liberated from demonic bondage<br>And will therefore be interested in this discourse.

“Someone who is always deluded and fails to comprehend<br>The existence and nonexistence of the eye’s limit<br>Is bound by the demon of ignorance<br>And will therefore not be interested in this discourse. ^F204b

“Someone who is always free from delusion and understands<br>The limits of existence and nonexistence of the eye<br>Is liberated from demonic bondage<br>And will therefore be interested in this discourse.

“Someone who is always deluded and does not comprehend<br>The eye’s characteristics of abiding and disintegration [n54]<br>Is bound by the demon of ignorance<br>And will therefore not be interested in this discourse.

“Someone who is always free from delusion and comprehends<br>The eye’s characteristics of abiding and disintegration<br>Is liberated from demonic bondage<br>And will therefore be interested in this discourse.

“Know that the same applies<br>To the essential nature of the ear, the nose, the tongue, the body, the mind,<br>Forms, sounds, scents, textures, mental phenomena,<br>Earth, water, fire, and air.

“It also applies to matter, sentient beings, suffering,<br>The aggregates, the elements, worldly arising, untrue words,<br>Desire, anger, ignorance, pride, craving, pretense,<br>Vanity, miserliness, envy, deceit, aggression, and so forth.

“Someone who is always deluded and does not comprehend<br>The limit of the exhaustion of the eye<br>Falls into the experience of worldly beings<br>And will therefore not be interested in this discourse.

“Someone who is always free from delusion and comprehends<br>The limit of the exhaustion of the eye<br>Is free from the experience of worldly beings<br>And will therefore be interested in this discourse.

“Someone who is always deluded and does not comprehend<br>The disintegration of the eye<br>Falls into the experience of worldly beings<br>And will therefore not be interested in this discourse.

“Someone who is always free from delusion and comprehends<br>The disintegration of the eye<br>Is free from the experience of worldly beings<br>And will therefore be interested in this discourse.

“Someone who is always deluded and does not comprehend<br>The disengagement of the eye<br>Falls into the experience of worldly beings<br>And will therefore not be interested in this discourse.

“Someone who is always free from delusion and comprehends<br>The disengagement of the eye<br>Is free from the experience of worldly beings<br>And will therefore be interested in this discourse.

“Someone who is always deluded and does not comprehend<br>The absence of coming and going of the eye<br>Falls into the experience of worldly beings<br>And will therefore not be interested in this discourse. ^F205a

“Someone who is always free from delusion and comprehends<br>The absence of coming and going of the eye<br>Is free from the experience of worldly beings<br>And will therefore be interested in this discourse.

“Someone who does not realize the selflessness of the eye<br>And is always deluded about the essential nature of its exhaustion<br>Falls into the experience of worldly beings<br>And will therefore not be interested in this discourse.

“Someone who realizes the selflessness of the eye<br>And always understands the essential nature of its exhaustion<br>Is free from the experience of worldly beings<br>And will therefore be interested in this discourse.

“Someone who does not realize the selflessness of the eye<br>And is always deluded about the objects of the practice of patience<br>Falls into the experience of worldly beings<br>And will therefore not be interested in this discourse.

“Someone who realizes the selflessness of the eye<br>And always understands what objects to accept<br>Is free from the experience of worldly beings<br>And will therefore be interested in this discourse.

“Someone who does not realize<br>The essence of the eye’s exhaustion<br>Is unable to accomplish nonabiding discipline<br>And will therefore not be interested in this discourse.

“Someone who realizes<br>The essence of the eye’s exhaustion<br>Is able to accomplish nonabiding discipline<br>And will therefore be interested in this discourse.

“Someone who does not realize<br>The essence of the eye’s exhaustion<br>Is unable to accomplish undefiled discipline<br>And will therefore not be interested in this discourse.

“Someone who realizes<br>The essence of the eye’s exhaustion<br>Is able to accomplish undefiled discipline<br>And will therefore be interested in this discourse.

“Someone who does not realize<br>The essence of the eye’s exhaustion<br>Is unable to accomplish undefiled insight<br>And will therefore not be interested in this discourse.

“Someone who realizes<br>The essence of the eye’s exhaustion<br>Is able to accomplish undefiled insight<br>And will therefore be interested in this discourse. ^F205b

“Someone who does not realize the essence of the eye’s exhaustion<br>Will always be deluded about the eye’s emptiness;<br>That person will not give rise to the all-retaining wisdom<br>And will therefore not be interested in this discourse.

“Someone who realizes the essence of the eye’s exhaustion<br>Will always realize the emptiness of the eye;<br>That person will give rise to the all-retaining wisdom<br>And will therefore be interested in this discourse.

“When someone who realizes the essential nature of the eye’s exhaustion<br>Accomplishes the all-retaining wisdom,<br>They accomplish the unsurpassed wisdom free of attachment<br>And will therefore be interested in this discourse.

“Someone who is not interested in this discourse<br>And always deluded about the essential nature of the eye’s exhaustion<br>Will regress from the state of absorption<br>And thus find it hard to attain unsurpassed wisdom.

“Someone who is strongly interested in this discourse<br>And realizes the essential nature of the eye’s exhaustion<br>Will accomplish the state of absorption [n55]<br>And thus not find it hard to actualize unsurpassed wisdom.

“Someone who realizes the essential nature of the eye’s exhaustion<br>And clearly understands the characteristic of selflessness<br>Will always gain trust and freedom from doubts<br>Upon hearing this Dharma teaching.

“Someone free from laziness who diligently contemplates,<br>Day and night, the essential nature of the eye’s exhaustion<br>Will accomplish retention and eloquence<br>And continuously teach this discourse.

“Someone who contemplates this discourse<br>Will perfect the wisdom of emitting light;<br>That person will clearly reveal the thus-gone ones [n56]<br>And realize the emptiness of the eye.

“The qualities derived from contemplating this discourse<br>Are more exalted than those<br>Derived from worshiping all the buddhas<br>By building a hundred thousand stūpas.

“The qualities derived from hearing this discourse<br>Are more exalted than those<br>Derived from worshiping the thus-gone ones’ relics<br>By playing a hundred thousand musical instruments.

“The merit created by honoring this discourse<br>Is more exalted than the merit<br>Created by honoring all the sentient beings beheld by the Buddha’s eyes<br>For countless millions of eons, as if they were thus-gone ones. ^F206a

“Someone who memorizes a single four-line verse<br>From this discourse and teaches it to others<br>Should be revered<br>As the most compassionate Victor.

“For a hundred thousand eons in the past,<br>When I was circling in the three realms,<br>I served countless buddhas<br>For the sake of this discourse.

“To master this discourse,<br>At times I lit countless hundreds of thousands of lamps—<br>Each of their wicks measuring about a league—<br>And offered those to the great guides.

“I sometimes worshiped the stūpas of thus-gone ones<br>With different types of offerings<br>Such as garlands of magnolias, jasmine, and aśoka flowers,<br>And banners and parasols.

“In the past, when I was circling in saṃsāra, [n57]<br>I practiced charity toward those who asked for favors;<br>I sometimes gave them flowers and fruits, [n58]<br>Sometimes bridges, wells, and springs,

“Sometimes elephants and vehicles, [n59]<br>Sometimes precious steeds and maidens,<br>Sometimes golden thrones and bejeweled tents,<br>And sometimes necklaces and garlands. [n60]

“In that way, for all those<br>Hundreds of thousands of eons,<br>My mind was always free of miserliness<br>For the sake of this discourse. [n61]

“In the past, for the sake of this discourse,<br>I observed completely pure discipline;<br>I constantly cultivated absorption and insight<br>And practiced generosity toward sentient beings.

“In the past, for the sake of this discourse,<br>I never harmed anyone—<br>Even the vicious ones who threatened me—<br>Out of my love for them.

“In the past, for the sake of this discourse,<br>I gave satisfaction<br>To all those who came to ask for favors,<br>By fulfilling all their wishes.

“In the past, for the sake of this discourse,<br>I ornamented mansions<br>With garlands of jewels<br>And offered those to the buddhas.

“In the past, for the sake of this discourse,<br>I worshiped buddhas<br>By offering them a variety of jewels<br>And wish-fulfilling gems. ^F206b

“In the past, for the sake of this discourse,<br>I always remembered the gracious ones<br>And venerated every single verse<br>That I heard from them.

“In the past, for the sake of this discourse,<br>I revered those who were disciplined<br>And always showed respect for them,<br>Even when I was walking.

“When I was circling in saṃsāra in the past,<br>For the sake of this discourse [n62]<br>I always expressed my gratitude<br>To those who extended even the slightest favor.

“In the past, for the sake of this discourse,<br>I did not seek personal gain and I did not deprecate the Dharma.<br>Instead, I developed sympathy<br>For my relatives and friends, and for all beings. [n63]

“In the past, for the sake of this discourse,<br>I became learned and eloquent.<br>I did not cling to phenomena<br>And had no miserliness regarding the household of friends.

“In the past, for the sake of this discourse,<br>Whenever a nonvirtuous thought arose in me,<br>I would never let it develop<br>But immediately confessed it.

“In the past, for the sake of this discourse,<br>When I was born as a prince,<br>I faithfully dedicated and offered to the buddhas<br>All my gems, perfumed ointments, and fragrant powders.

“In the past, for the sake of this discourse,<br>Out of sympathy for prisoners,<br>I took upon myself, as a physical bail,<br>The many sufferings they experienced.

“In the past, for the sake of this discourse,<br>I renounced the five sense pleasures<br>And always took the precepts<br>At the six times of the month. [n64]

“In the past, for the sake of this discourse,<br>I always cultivated patience<br>And never gave rise to desirous miserliness,<br>Even with respect to my wives.

“In the past, for the sake of this discourse,<br>I enriched all destitute beings<br>By offering them wealth,<br>So that they could experience happiness.

“In the past, for the sake of this discourse,<br>I always attracted everyone—<br>Mendicants, brahmins,<br>And all the various other types of beings.

“In the past, for the sake of this discourse,<br>I gave generously to those in need,<br>Always acting as a great benefactor<br>To benefit all beings. ^F207a

“I sometimes fulfilled<br>The wishes of sentient beings<br>By giving them flowers and fruits,<br>Without sending anyone away. [n65]

“When I was practicing generosity in the past,<br>I constantly maintained an impartial attitude.<br>Therefore, with a sense of modesty,<br>I respectfully practiced generosity toward everyone—

“Those endowed with the qualities of discipline,<br>Those who observed improper discipline, those who had no sense of shame,<br>And those who had achieved great miraculous power,<br>As well as all other ordinary beings.

“Since I did not distinguish between high and low<br>When I was practicing generosity in the past,<br>I dedicated myself one pointedly to this discourse<br>Without pursuing afflicted states.

“At times, in some lands,<br>I annihilated all opposing forces<br>And subjugated all the nāgas<br>So that they showered rains in timely ways.

“Candraprabha, you should know that, in the pursuit of this discourse,<br>I have undergone austerities for countless lifetimes; [n66]<br>Even if these were described for a hundred thousand eons,<br>This task would never come to an end.

“Abide by such a conviction and,<br>In the future, during the final period,<br>Give rise to genuine recollection<br>And expound this discourse!

“I clearly see the future<br>With my buddha eyes;<br>I know precisely all the various actions<br>That will occur.

“I also know all the monks who,<br>In the pursuit of unsurpassed wisdom,<br>Will teach this discourse with minds filled with faith,<br>As well as all the directions in which this teaching will take place. [n67]

“I also know all the thoughts and methods of those who,<br>In the future, during the final period,<br>Will be interested in this discourse<br>Upon hearing it.

“I know all the inferior beings<br>Who will not hear this discourse<br>And who will therefore denigrate<br>The teachings of the Dharma.

“I will give a prophecy to all the monks and nuns<br>Who cry out of heartfelt longing<br>When they hear this discourse,<br>And they will behold the supreme victors. ^F207b

“Candraprabha, you should know that,<br>Through my awakened miraculous power,<br>I know the purity and the impurity of beings’ minds,<br>And even all their inclinations. [n68]

“I know all the qualities of sentient beings,<br>Such as those who are always dedicated to peace and isolation,<br>Dwell in absorptions without any attachment to saṃsāra,<br>And have permanently abandoned even its habitual patterns.

“I know which sentient beings will, in the future,<br>Adhere to untrue teachings,<br>And which ones<br>Will be devoted to the Buddha.

“Beings who are attached to worldly pleasures<br>Will not properly grow the seed of the buddhas’ awakening;<br>Due to such causes and conditions,<br>Their practices will deteriorate. [n69]

“Candraprabha, those who realize the seed of awakening<br>Are certain to obtain pure, boundless lights.<br>Thus, regard each of the causes and conditions related to such lights distinctly;<br>You should perceive them all through the power of insight.

“Those who abandon unwholesome friends,<br>Who rely on virtuous friends,<br>And who guard the purity of their discipline<br>Will accomplish the lights of the buddhas.

“Guard your senses,<br>Abandon shamelessness and indecency,<br>Cultivate virtuous qualities,<br>And protect sentient beings!

“Stop being involved in conflicts,<br>Realize emptiness,<br>And develop erudition and eloquence<br>Without hankering for personal gain!

“Regard all personal achievements as being like fecal filth<br>And do not let the purity of your mind<br>Be defiled by the stains of selfish gains;<br>Pursue liberation and you will achieve the unsurpassed goal! ^F208a

“Accomplish the path of the buddhas,<br>Regard the Buddha and reality as being similar,<br>Always set ablaze the great torch of the Dharma,<br>And illuminate all the worlds!

“Let your mind be unshakable<br>Like the king of mountains,<br>And eagerly cultivate patience toward everyone,<br>Even when you are insulted or beaten!

“Act as a genuine virtuous friend<br>For the sake of sentient beings,<br>Abandon all forms of inferior attitude,<br>And always accomplish pure actions!

“Let your mind be stable,<br>Teach the unsurpassed Dharma,<br>And bestow this sacred discourse<br>On the kindhearted!

“Those who pursue unsurpassed wisdom<br>Are not afraid of saṃsāra;<br>Having abandoned negative deeds,<br>They will gain the most supreme benefits. [n70]

“Based on the mind, they see that the mind is empty.<br>Therefore, although they abide within saṃsāra,<br>They see—based on the eye—that the eye is empty,<br>And so they do not cling to the eye.

“Those who understand in that way<br>Can dwell on the eyes without causing affliction;<br>If they see that the eye is empty, they will achieve genuine wisdom,<br>And light rays will therefore manifest.

“If they see that the eye is empty,<br>They will permanently abandon [n72] desire.<br>Due to this absence of desire,<br>Light rays will manifest.

“What is explained here with respect to desire also applies<br>To all the other afflictions up to anger,<br>Ignorance, clinging to “I,” pretense, [n73]<br>Aggression, miserliness, envy,

“Shamelessness, impatience,<br>Haughtiness, arrogance,<br>Pride, deceit,<br>Carelessness, dishonesty, and so forth. ^F208b

“Those who obtain genuine wisdom<br>Realize the eye to be peace.<br>Therefore, light rays will manifest<br>Through this realization.

“Those who have achieved genuine wisdom<br>Are settled in the way of the buddhas,<br>And light rays will manifest<br>On this basis.

“Those who have achieved genuine wisdom<br>Are settled in the methods of the buddhas,<br>And light rays will manifest<br>On this basis.

“I have never heard of or seen<br>Anyone who has permanently abandoned the obscurations<br>And accomplished the manifestation of light rays<br>Without achieving genuine wisdom.

“Those who, after realizing genuine wisdom,<br>Permanently rid themselves of the harm of the obscurations<br>And observe this conduct<br>Will achieve the emanation of light rays.

“Those who pursue the conduct of the victors<br>And worship the thus-gone ones<br>After having trained in this discourse<br>Will achieve genuine wisdom and skillful means. [n74]

“Those who properly understand the limit of the eye’s natural state<br>Will also properly understand the limit of the arising of the eye;<br>Those persons will therefore<br>Achieve the emanation of light rays.

“Those who directly understand the limit of the eye’s natural state<br>Will also directly understand the limit of the arising of the eye;<br>Those persons will therefore<br>Achieve the emanation of light rays.

“Those who properly understand the purity of the eye’s natural state<br>Will properly realize the purity of the ear’s natural state;<br>Those persons will therefore<br>Achieve the emanation of light rays.

“Know that this also applies<br>To the nose, the tongue, the body, the mind,<br>Forms, sounds, scents, tastes,<br>Textures, and mental phenomena.

“They have come into the presence of the Blessed One<br>And respectfully join their palms.<br>For those who have gathered in this assembly,<br>I ask you to declare the causes and conditions of this light. ^F218b

“Since the Thus-Gone One realizes<br>That the eye is empty and selfless,<br>He truly surpasses<br>All householder practitioners.

“The Thus-Gone One knows<br>The lack of difference and the sameness of phenomena.<br>Blessed One, you know this through your insight— [n121]<br>You do not perceive this through your miraculous powers.

“Blessed One, you realize that the eye is selfless,<br>That its essence is empty, and that it does not come from or go anywhere—<br>Your immaculate wisdom is immeasurable.<br>Why do you smile?

“Thus-Gone One, your births are totally exhausted,<br>And you protect all the worlds through your great compassion.<br>Dharma lord, supreme leader of human beings,<br>Please tell us why you smile!

“Countless great bodhisattvas<br>And hosts of majestic gods<br>Are holding sublime parasols in the sky,<br>And this great earth has shaken.

“You bring delight impartially to those<br>Who have accomplished virtuous qualities<br>For a long time under the thus-gone ones of the past.<br>Great compassionate leader, please tell us why you smile!

“Those who have worshiped buddhas in the past<br>Are filled with joy when they hear this teaching.<br>Great guide, supreme leader of human beings,<br>Please tell us why you smile!

“Blessed One, you please beings through your manifold teachings,<br>Your melodious voice resembles the songs of the swan—the king of birds— [n122]<br>And countless delightful words manifest spontaneously from your mouth.<br>Please tell us why you emitted light!

“The praises of your agreeable speech are proclaimed<br>In countless millions of verses,<br>And you are endowed with the supreme voice of the black Indian cuckoo. [n123]<br>Please tell us why you emitted light!

“The deep thunder roar by which you teach the Dharma<br>Resounds throughout countless hundreds of thousands of worlds.<br>Through your compassion you teach using both harsh and sweet words.<br>Why did you emit golden light?

“You understand arising, non-arising, exhaustion, and non-exhaustion,<br>And you realize that the eye is absent and beyond coming and going.<br>Illuminator of the world, supreme ambrosia, [n124]<br>Why did you emit golden light? ^F219a

“You realize that the occurrence of the eye<br>Is always empty and void, beyond coming and going,<br>And groundless, like a mirage or a water bubble.<br>What are the causes and conditions of your smile?

“Know that the same applies<br>To the ear, the nose, the tongue, the body, the mind,<br>Forms, sounds, scents, tastes, textures, and mental phenomena,<br>Up to words, names, and so forth.

“Your body is permanent like vajra and indestructible.<br>It is endowed with a hundred thousand perfect and supreme marks,<br>And you move your body without any fetters.<br>Please tell us—what are the causes and conditions of your smile?

“Your calves are round, [n125] your heels are broad,<br>And like a lion your abdomen does not bulge—<br>Your navel is deep, and your waist is broad.<br>Please tell us—what are the causes and conditions of your smile?

“Your golden body is pure, stainless, and immaculate,<br>And each hair on your body is deep black,<br>Perfumed, and curls to the right.<br>Please tell us—what are the causes and conditions of your smile?

“Your sublime body, perfect and permanently enduring,<br>Is like the king of trees, the banyan fig.<br>It is adorned with all qualities,<br>And your melodious voice pleases your retinues.

“Your shoulders are round, your upper arms are thick and beautiful, [n126]<br>Your crown protuberance is perfect and coiled clockwise,<br>You move throughout countless hundreds of thousands of realms,<br>And you point out to sentient beings the mistaken and the correct paths.

“Your teeth are white and spotless like conch shells, [n127]<br>Your tongue is long and can cover your whole face,<br>Your cheeks resemble the jaws of a lion, and your nose is prominent. [n128]<br>All those qualities arise from your skill and your pure mind.

“The fragrance of blue lotus flowers exudes from your mouth,<br>And your body is pervaded by the scent of sandalwood.<br>Since you have cultivated love extensively in the past,<br>Your pure intent is uninterrupted.

“Thus-Gone One, the soles of your feet are even,<br>And although they touch the ground, they remain unsullied by dust. [n129]<br>Walking like a lion and an elephant,<br>You have transcended the entire world.

“You are beautified by the marks of thousand-spoked wheels,<br>And your sublime light shines brilliantly.<br>Since you benefit beings through your virtue,<br>Whoever sees you is filled with faith. ^F219b

“Each of your fingers emits a hundred thousand light rays<br>That illuminate countless buddha realms.<br>Since you have accomplished so many virtuous practices in the past,<br>You have achieved those various arrays of excellent marks.

“Your form body is perfect and unequaled,<br>The marks that beautify your face are magnificent,<br>And you benefit the world [n130] through your miraculous powers.<br>Please tell us why you smile!

“Your calves are similar to the calves of antelope,<br>And the shape of your body resembles that of a lion. [n131]<br>You appear within the worlds and illuminate them. [n132]<br>Please tell us why you smile!

“Like a stallion, [n133] your genitals are hidden and stainless;<br>The palms of your hands are even and broad,<br>And your arms reach past your knees.<br>Amazing and magnificent lion of human beings, please tell us why you smile!

“Innumerable brilliant, soothing, and brightly colored light rays<br>Radiate from your body. [n134]<br>Your pure mind is uninterrupted,<br>And you reveal countless discourses.

“Such qualities will not be accomplished<br>By those who uphold eternalist or nihilist views.<br>Only those who have abandoned extreme views<br>Will swiftly attain the pure body of the blessed ones. [n135]

“Your speech resounds like divine drums and roaring thunder;<br>It is melodious and clear like the songs of the kalandaka bird [n136]<br>And endowed with a hundred thousand tunes of divine songs.<br>Please tell us why you emitted lights!

“Great Guide, by teaching the Dharma through a single teaching,<br>You interrupt the practices of those who adhere to improper discipline.<br>Whatever melodious words come from the Thus-Gone One’s mouth<br>Are profound and amazing!

“Leader of human beings, the light rays emitted from between your eyebrows<br>Illuminate a hundred thousand buddha realms,<br>Your sublime eyes have a deep black color,<br>And the mark of your crown protuberance cannot be seen by the celestial gods.

“Your forty teeth,<br>White like crystal, [n137]<br>Are the manifestation of your immeasurable virtuous activities.<br>Please tell us why you smile! ^F220a

“Thus-Gone One, your body of perfect qualities<br>Shines with countless excellent and magnificent colors<br>And emits the most amazing pure light rays.<br>Please tell us why you smile!

“Great compassionate one, supreme victor, leader of human beings,<br>You know the pure intentions of sentient beings,<br>And you have achieved supreme, unimpeded eloquence.<br>Please tell us why you smile!

“Since the Thus-Gone One has reached the other shore,<br>He is perfectly endowed with the three knowledges and the six higher perceptions,<br>And he displays innumerable pure light rays.<br>Please tell us why you smile!

“Blessed One, during countless eons in the past,<br>You worshiped a hundred thousand blessed ones,<br>And the fruition of such causes is inexhaustible.<br>Please tell us why you smile!

“Blessed One, since for countless eons in the past<br>You have dwelled within supreme states of absorption,<br>You have realized the limits of the arising and exhaustion of the eye.<br>Please tell us why you smile!

“Great guide, you clearly know<br>The past, the future, and the present—<br>Your immaculate wisdom is unimpeded and inconceivable,<br>Please tell us why you smile!”

At that moment, in front of the numerous retinues, the Blessed One extended his golden hand, placed it on Candraprabhakumārabhūta’s head, and spoke these verses:

“Kumāra, listen carefully!<br>I now entrust to you<br>This teaching on awakening—<br>The sūtra on the manifestation of lights.

“In the future, during the final period<br>Of the degenerate times,<br>When the Dharma is about to vanish,<br>You must reveal it and expound it to sentient beings!

“With my buddha eye, I see and know<br>All those who, in the future, during the final period, [n138]<br>Will be interested in this excellent, profound, and sacred sūtra,<br>As well as all those who will not be interested in it.

“Sentient beings who pursue<br>The path of awakening<br>Should constantly recollect the Buddha<br>And keep this discourse in mind.

“Those whose faith has disappeared,<br>Who are attracted to distraction,<br>And who delight in sleeping both day and night<br>Will not be interested in this discourse. ^F220b

“Even if they become renunciants<br>Under my teachings,<br>Interest in the qualities of nirvāṇa<br>Will not arise in them.

“Those fools will squander the gifts of faith,<br>And even if they hear repeatedly<br>About the defects associated with conditioned phenomena,<br>They will cling to worldly concerns, and fear will not arise in them.

“Those fools<br>Will deprecate the wise ones,<br>And even if they wear the Dharma robes,<br>They will be devoid of even the slightest trace of insight.

“Since they will not know the true words<br>Well-spoken by the Sage,<br>And even if they hear them, they will not believe in them,<br>Do not stay in the company of such fools!

“Even if they hear these magnificent teachings,<br>Joy and devotion will not arise in their minds.<br>I reproach such individuals;<br>Though they have a human body, they waste it in meaningless pursuits.

“Those whose minds become filled with joy and devotion<br>When they hear these profound teachings<br>Have already met buddhas in the past.<br>There is thus no doubt that they will reach unsurpassed awakening.

“Fools who are inferior due to their unwholesome views<br>And sever the life force of insight will forsake awakening;<br>Therefore, abandon such nonvirtuous friends<br>And instead attend to the wise ones and revere them!

“All the innumerable defects in all the worlds,<br>And all sentient beings sinking in the three lower realms,<br>Originate from the causes and conditions of ignorance and delusion;<br>Therefore, those who follow their afflictions will not gain freedom.

“Since they forsake the true Dharma<br>And practice what is contrary to the Dharma,<br>You should abandon<br>Those inferior, deluded beings!

“Like a charioteer [n139] breaking<br>The axle of his steering wheels,<br>Sentient beings who engage in nonvirtuous actions<br>Will be reborn in the hell realms.

“Abusive words always come back to harm<br>Those who pronounce them,<br>Just like a sharp axe that strikes the body<br>Of the one who wields it. ^F221a

“Karmic ripening is experienced without fail,<br>In accordance with the causes and conditions of the actions performed. [n140]<br>Those who do not understand this will attend to worldly sages,<br>Thinking they are virtuous friends.

“Then, thinking that those who meditate on emptiness<br>Realize the unconditioned, they will develop nihilist views;<br>They will say that the body becomes like a broken pot<br>And that the mind also ceases to be.

“They will mostly be drawn<br>To idle gossip, [n141]<br>And, strongly attached to it,<br>They will gain no benefits whatsoever.

“The harm caused by poisonous snakes<br>Will never cause many beings to fall into the lower realms,<br>But when a fool teaches the Dharma he destroys roots of virtue,<br>Which causes countless beings to fall into the hell realms.

“You who have gathered in this assembly should consider<br>That Candraprabhakumāra, who is present here today,<br>Has worshiped and venerated countless blessed buddhas in the past—<br>As many as there are grains of sand in the Ganges.

“Since he has pursued unsurpassed wisdom<br>By meditating in a steadfast manner,<br>His mind does not dwell<br>On any of the realms of existence.

“Since he knows that the limit of the arising of the eye<br>Is always pure,<br>He has abandoned concepts,<br>And he is utterly free from clinging.

“He will teach this unsurpassed sūtra<br>On the manifestation of lights<br>In order to benefit<br>Countless sentient beings.

“Since fools are unable to train in this teaching,<br>They regard genuine spiritual practices as faulty. [n142]<br>Therefore, stay far away from such fools;<br>Do not assist them, and do not train in their teachings!

“Since fools are attracted to dispute,<br>They are unable to earnestly adopt behavior that is free from conflict.<br>Such beings are utterly devoid of an appropriate mindset,<br>So do not serve them or proclaim their praise! ^F221b

“Since fools are lazy and indolent by nature,<br>They constantly engage in physical, verbal, and mental nonvirtuous actions;<br>They therefore completely lack<br>Pure discipline, insight, and erudition.

“They always think about the objects of their desires,<br>And they are drawn to distractions;<br>Regard the families, blood lines,<br>And bodies of such fools as inferior!

“They are filled with aggression, they do not listen to advice,<br>And their faces are repulsive;<br>Even if they are born in virtuous locations,<br>Their bodies will be vile.

“Since they cling to the notion of self, they are deluded about the true meaning.<br>Since they lack intelligence, they are unable to understand the diversity of words.<br>Even if they hear teachings about intrinsic emptiness,<br>They are not interested in them.

“The thus-gone ones, the blessed ones<br>Have long ago abandoned all worldly verbal constructs.<br>Ordinary beings cling and are attached to those,<br>And so, for as long as they live, they fail to gain understanding and realization.

“Though they might proclaim the praises of discipline,<br>They do not practice pure conduct.<br>Though their mouths might teach the Dharma,<br>Their bodies engage in practices that are contrary to it.

“Though they might teach the Vinaya,<br>They always behave in ways that are contrary to it.<br>The Buddha has instructed his disciples<br>To wear the saffron-colored robes.

“Still, even if those who observe improper discipline<br>Put on such robes,<br>It only causes the strength of pride and carelessness<br>To grow in their minds. [n143]

“When they eat the food that is donated to them with faith,<br>It is like swallowing burning iron.<br>In that way, after renouncing their households,<br>They do not experience sense pleasures,

“Nor do they gain the supreme happiness<br>Associated with the Dharma of the buddhas.<br>In this way, they aspire for a variety of experiences<br>And do not abandon the two extremes.

“None of their interests are pure,<br>And even when such fools are in a large retinue,<br>They are like foxes<br>That have entered a pack of lions.

“Even if you give<br>Teachings related to peace,<br>They do not understand or realize<br>The meaning of suchness and emptiness.

“Even if their teachings are praised in large retinues,<br>This only causes their arrogance to increase;<br>Since they do not recollect the great teacher,<br>They lack modesty and humility. [n144] ^F222a

“Even when they take hold of impure objects,<br>They joyfully hold on to them<br>As if they were wish-fulfilling gems,<br>And their minds never let go of them.

“Though such vile beings<br>Might renounce their households,<br>Guard their behavior, put on Dharma robes,<br>And carry alms bowls,

“These are all merely superficial displays,<br>And so they utterly lack true insight.<br>Even if they shave their facial hairs,<br>They do not renounce harmful attitudes.

“Since they follow teachings contrary to virtuous practice<br>And fall into mistaken views,<br>The path leading to disengagement and nirvāṇa<br>Is completely ruined for them. [n145]

“They will not achieve the fruition<br>Resulting from engaging in virtuous practice,<br>And so their ignorance and other afflictions<br>Will not diminish in the slightest.

“While roaming in the cities,<br>They sing the praise of disengagement.<br>Due to their lack of intelligence,<br>Those fools do not understand the genuine path.

“All their aspirations are nonvirtuous;<br>Even if they stay in the wilderness,<br>It is not to accomplish the true causes of nirvāṇa,<br>But out of desire for personal gain.

“Fond of drowsiness and sleep,<br>They are concerned about their physical well-being<br>And constantly long<br>For ways to accomplish that goal.

“Even if they stay in the wilderness for many years,<br>They adhere to mistaken views,<br>And so they stray from the path leading to nirvāṇa,<br>Never obtaining the results of the paths of virtuous practice.

“Since they regress from the genuine view [n146]<br>And transgress pure discipline,<br>They always crave fine clothes to adorn their bodies,<br>And for the happiness of sensual pleasures.

“When they enter cities,<br>They bear the marks of pride<br>And do not guard their physical actions,<br>And so they do not observe pure conduct.

“At times, when they enter cities and roam about,<br>They boast about their characteristics in these words:<br>‘You should know that the caves in the wilderness are my home.<br>My eyes downcast, I glance upward slowly, walking and stopping serenely.’ ^F222b

“Since they do indeed look like worthy ones,<br>All the householders say to one another,<br>‘This monk who stays in the wilderness<br>Is truly a noble being!’

“At other times, when visitors come to see them<br>While they stay in the forest, they brag in words such as these:<br>‘In these jungles I wander day and night’<br>Or ‘This mat of fresh grass is where I sit.’

“At other times, when they go to cities and enter homes,<br>They eagerly ask with deception, ‘Are you well?’<br>Before engaging in frivolous mundane conversations<br>Related to various topics such as kings and thieves.

“At other times, driven by attachment,<br>They boast about their own qualities<br>In various ways<br>In the presence of women.

“They say, ‘I am a great field of merit for the entire world;<br>I have renounced my kingdom and become a renunciant,<br>And now I meditate on the path.<br>My queen and my children [n147] have been reborn in the higher realms.’

“Upon hearing those words,<br>The women are filled with immense respect;<br>They collect clothes, food, and beverages, which they offer with the words,<br>‘Venerable, look upon us with compassion—please come to us directly!’ [n148]

“Since such people are attached to delicious meals<br>And do not know their defects,<br>They are at the mercy of Māra,<br>Like turtles caught in nets.

“Even while staying in the wilderness,<br>They are conceited about their achievements,<br>And they continue to despise and deprecate<br>Those monks who are diligent.

“Since they repeatedly give rise to attachment<br>Out of the delusion related to gain and fame,<br>And since they constantly engage in deceitful actions to sustain themselves,<br>They repeatedly increase their physical and verbal unwholesome actions.

“Even if benefactors make offerings to them out of faith,<br>Their merit will decrease through the power of their laziness.<br>Since those fools always long for nonvirtue,<br>They will never reach the acceptance that accords with emptiness. ^F223a

“For those who completely abandon those faults<br>On the basis of my teachings<br>And practice with zeal and conscientiousness,<br>It will not be difficult to hear [n149] this teaching.

“For those who are unshaken by worldly concerns<br>Such as gain, loss, praise, criticism,<br>Suffering, and happiness,<br>It will not be difficult to receive this teaching.

“For those who perceive the body as filth,<br>The aggregates as wounds,<br>And food as poison for the wounds,<br>It will not be difficult to receive this teaching.

“For those who do not become haughty<br>But put on the attire of modesty<br>Even when they receive fine clothes,<br>It will not be difficult to receive this teaching.

“For those who are not conceited about their appearance and strength,<br>Who are not attached to food<br>But merely eat to sustain their lives,<br>It will not be difficult to receive this teaching.

“For those who know that the objects of craving are empty<br>And therefore neither indulge in nor reject pleasures<br>But always act with the understanding of emptiness and disengagement,<br>It will not be difficult to receive this teaching.

“For those living alone in the mountains—more than a league away—<br>Who, while absorbed in equipoise,<br>Realize the selflessness of conditioned phenomena,<br>It will not be difficult to receive this teaching.

“For those who accomplish the path of the pure eye<br>By realizing the characteristics of the limits<br>Of the exhaustion, arising, and cognition of the eye,<br>It will not be difficult to receive this teaching.

“The same also applies to the ear, the nose,<br>The tongue, the body, the mind,<br>Forms, sounds, scents, tastes, and textures,<br>Up to words and names.

“You should know that,<br>In the future,<br>All the countless monks will be<br>Either diligent or lazy in those ways.

“You should know that, in those ways,<br>They will either be virtuous or nonvirtuous friends,<br>Accomplished or unaccomplished,<br>Trusting or untrusting.

“You should know that, in those ways, they will be<br>Either virtuous or nonvirtuous friends<br>Who accomplish or do not accomplish<br>The path of the eye’s exhaustion. [n150]

“You should know that, in those ways,<br>Some will be strongly interested in awakening,<br>Only to lose interest later,<br>After one, two, or three months have passed.

“Still, even if the interest<br>Born upon hearing this teaching<br>Declines in some,<br>They will revive their faith later on. ^F223b

“Some will not abandon their carelessness<br>When they hear this teaching;<br>Instead, they will think,<br>‘This discourse is not the Buddha’s words!’

“After hearing this teaching,<br>Some will be caught by Māra;<br>Even if faith has initially arisen in them,<br>They will abandon it later on.

“Those who are not interested in the Dharma will pursue falsehood, fame, and gain;<br>Although they might sing the praises of the lights<br>Of dhāraṇīs, absorption, and complete comprehension<br>As they roam through cities and towns, they will not gain true realization.

“Those who are bound by the five sense pleasures<br>And pursue worldly activities<br>Will involve themselves in concepts and statements, [n151]<br>And so denigrate the training in emptiness.

“Devoid of mindfulness and insight,<br>Their whole life will be wasted in meaningless pursuits;<br>The fools who abandon the Dharma of emptiness obstruct their discipline, [n152]<br>And so fall into the Hell of Unceasing Torment, never to be reborn in the higher realms.

“Someone who hears this discourse<br>And memorizes a single four-line verse from it<br>Is more exalted than someone who builds<br>A thousand stūpas in every single moment.

“Someone who hears this discourse<br>And memorizes [n153] a single four-line verse from it<br>Is more exalted than someone who offers<br>A hundred thousand flower garlands to the stūpas of the buddhas.

“Someone who contemplates this discourse<br>For just a single moment<br>Is more exalted than someone who builds<br>As many bejeweled stūpas as there are grains of sand in the Ganges.

“Someone who contemplates this discourse<br>For just a single moment<br>Is more exalted than someone who venerates<br>Ten million buddha realms by tossing flowers.

“Someone who contemplates this discourse<br>For just a single moment<br>Is more exalted than someone who offers<br>Ten million Dharma robes to the buddhas.

“The buddha eye is pure and superior—<br>There is nothing that it does not know and see.<br>Those who are dedicated to this discourse<br>Will achieve the buddha eye.

“Countless eons ago,<br>The Buddha Dīpaṅkara appeared.<br>I was at that time the child of a brahmin, [n154]<br>And I worshiped him by holding up flowers.

“He prophesied that I would become a buddha<br>Named Śākyamuni,<br>And that I would teach this discourse<br>While sitting at the seat of awakening.

“You were a young boy at that time.<br>When you heard this prophecy,<br>You became overjoyed and filled with faith.<br>With your palms joined, you made this aspiration: ^F224a

“ ‘When this brahmin child becomes a perfect buddha,<br>May I become his assistant.<br>After he passes into nirvāṇa,<br>May I uphold and guard his collection of teachings!’

“When the Buddha Dīpaṅkara<br>Taught this discourse on the manifestation of lights,<br>Both the brahmin son and the young boy<br>Listened to it carefully and kept it in mind.

“In the past, I once offered blue lotus flowers to that buddha.<br>You were present at that time, and you made the aspiration<br>To uphold this discourse during the final period of my teachings<br>And to propagate it on a vast scale.

“Those who, upon hearing this teaching,<br>Do not give rise to aversion but hold it,<br>Keep it in mind, read it, and recite it<br>Are sublime beings!

“In the future, you should uphold<br>This teaching that is difficult to hear,<br>Teach it extensively, and reveal its meaning<br>To sentient beings!

“In the past,<br>When the true Dharma was about to disappear,<br>I also taught this discourse on the manifestation of lights<br>Extensively to sentient beings.

“Because fools do not accomplish it with determination,<br>They deprecate it.<br>Still, when I hear their words,<br>I do not become angry.

“Instead, I always cultivate patience<br>And benefit the world.<br>Since the strength of my patience is perfect,<br>I am adorned with the major and minor marks.

“Kumāra, you should regard the excellent<br>And perfect body of the Buddha,<br>As well as his pure and sublime golden color,<br>As the outcome of the strength of his patience.

“Countless realms shake<br>When my feet touch the earth,<br>Yet no sentient being loses balance;<br>All this is the outcome of the strength of my patience.

“The hundred thousand light rays<br>Emitted from each pore of my hair<br>Illuminate all directions;<br>All this is the outcome of the strength of my supreme patience.

“Creatures with frightening appearances such as rākṣasas,<br>As well as ravishing and majestic beings, [n155]<br>All show reverence to me; [n156]<br>All this is the outcome of the strength of my supreme patience.

“All the members of my retinues<br>Have developed gentle minds,<br>And they revere the Thus-Gone One;<br>All this is the outcome of the strength of my supreme patience.

“All the praises of the Buddha’s qualities<br>Proclaimed within my great assemblies<br>With a hundred thousand musical tunes,<br>All this is the outcome of the strength of my supreme patience. ^F224b

“A hundred thousand malevolent<br>And frightening nāga kings<br>Become pleased when they see the Buddha;<br>All this is the outcome of the strength of my supreme patience.

“Beings such as rākṣasas and yakṣas<br>Come to me carrying hundreds of thousands<br>Of flower garlands to worship me;<br>All this is the outcome of the strength of my supreme patience.

“Thus-gone ones residing<br>In countless hundreds of thousands of realms<br>Are praising my qualities;<br>All this is the outcome of the strength of my supreme patience.

“Hundreds of thousands of mahoragas<br>And kaṭapūtanas gather together [n157]<br>And worship me by tossing flowers;<br>All this is the outcome of the strength of my supreme patience.

“Sixty billion beings<br>Including the yakṣa kings<br>Come to worship me;<br>All this is the outcome of the strength of my supreme patience.

“The kings of the nāgas<br>Such as Manavaka<br>Also offer me red pearls;<br>All this is the outcome of the strength of my supreme patience.

“Tumburu [n158] and a hundred thousand other devas of music [n159]<br>Gather together<br>And worship me with music;<br>All this is the outcome of the strength of my supreme patience.

“Hundreds of thousands of mahoragas [n160]<br>And kaṭapūtanas [n161] gather together<br>And worship me with offerings of flowers and perfumes;<br>All this is the outcome of the strength of my supreme patience.

“The nāga king Gandhāra<br>Reveals half of his body<br>And joins his palms respectfully toward me;<br>All this is the outcome of the strength of my supreme patience.

“A trillion beings<br>Such as Anaṅga and Rāhula<br>Worship me with minds filled with faith;<br>All this is the outcome of the strength of my supreme patience.

“Kumāra, you should see<br>That whoever is touched by the Buddha’s light rays<br>Becomes freed from suffering and obtains happiness;<br>All this is the outcome of the strength of my supreme patience.

“Samudra and a hundred thousand<br>Gods of the wind fill the sky<br>And worship me by showering perfumed rains;<br>All this is the outcome of the strength of my supreme patience.

“A hundred thousand divine retinues<br>Worship me by tossing divine flowers,<br>Abandon their cravings, and attend to the buddhas;<br>All this is the outcome of the strength of my supreme patience.

“You also advertise the Buddha’s miraculous power,<br>Generosity, and discipline,<br>Causing everyone to hear about them;<br>Regard this as the outcome of the strength of my supreme patience. [n162]
