# The Noble Great Vehicle Sūtra Siṃha’s Questions

> **84000 English, stored verbatim as a reference layer** (CC BY-NC-ND — do not excerpt or adapt for output; the shipped verse is our own translation, citing 84000). Tibetan source: `1-SOURCES/Text/bo-toh81.md`. Folio anchors `^F<folio>` align to the Degé Kangyur.

Homage to all buddhas and bodhisattvas! ^F27a

Thus did I hear at one time. The Blessed One was dwelling in Rājagṛha on Vulture Peak, together with a great saṅgha of 1,250 monks and a great saṅgha of bodhisattvas. [n1]

Knowing all and seeing all,<br>The Victorious One is a peerless being. [n2]<br>The son [n3] of King Ajātaśatru<br>Was known as Siṃha. [n4] {1}

Accompanied and attended<br>By five hundred wealthy noblemen, [n5]<br>He departed from Rājagṛha<br>With great royal grandeur. [n6] {2} ^F27b

Carrying golden parasols, [n7]<br>They went before the perfect Buddha.<br>Bowing their heads to the feet of the Buddha,<br>They offered the five hundred parasols. {3}

Offering these to the Great Sage,<br>All of them set out for awakening.<br>By the power of the perfect Buddha,<br>They became fearless. [n8] {4}

Siṃha joined his palms in reverence<br>And asked the perfect Buddha,<br>“Will the Guide [n9] please explain to me<br>The conduct of bodhisattvas? {5}

“How does one become beautiful?<br>How does one take miraculous birth? [n10]<br>How does one remember previous births?<br>Great Sage, please give your instruction.” {6}

Knowing their pure intentions with his wisdom,<br>The Buddha gave his instruction:<br>“I will give a teaching on what you have asked:<br>The conduct of bodhisattvas. [n11] {7}

“One becomes beautiful through patience.<br>One takes miraculous birth by pure conduct. [n12]<br>One recalls past lives by giving the Dharma.<br>Youth, know it to be thus!” [n13] {8}

“How does one manifest<br>The thirty-two marks, the eighty excellent signs,<br>And a body radiant like gold [n14]<br>That delights beings upon seeing?” {9}

“The marks manifest through complete generosity.<br>Likewise, the excellent signs come to be through love.<br>The golden radiance of the victorious ones<br>Manifests by having an attitude of equality toward all beings.” [n15] {10}

“How is retention attained?<br>How does absorption arise?<br>How do statements become acceptable?<br>Great Sage, please give your teaching!” [n16] {11}

“Retention is attained through receptivity to the Dharma.<br>Absorption arises by training the mind.<br>Statements become acceptable<br>When one abandons senseless talk.” [n17] {12}

“How does one become mindful?<br>O Sage, [n18] how does one become intelligent?<br>How does one come to act according to the Dharma? [n19]<br>O Holy Being, [n20] how does one become wise?” [n21] {13} ^F28a

“One comes to possess mindfulness by being undeceiving. [n22]<br>One becomes intelligent by contemplating meaning.<br>One comes to act according to the Dharma [n23] by having reverence.<br>One becomes wise by knowing the characteristics of phenomena.” [n24] {14}

“By what action is a bodhisattva [n25] born<br>In the presence of the guardians of the world? [n26]<br>How do they come to ask questions<br>Directly [n27] before the guardians of the world?” [n28] {15}

“The one who never obstructs<br>The giving of Dharma<br>Will with great swiftness [n29]<br>Come to meet the guardians of the world.” [n30] {16}

“How does one relinquish rebirth in inopportune states?<br>How does one go forth to favorable states?<br>How does one become heedful<br>Wherever one takes birth throughout all of one’s lives?” {17}

“Inopportune births are relinquished through faith. [n31]<br>One goes forth to favorable states through moral discipline.<br>One becomes always heedful<br>By becoming familiar with emptiness.” [n32] {18}

“O Sage, [n33] how do bodhisattvas [n34]<br>Purify their karma?<br>How do they dodge the snares of Māra?<br>How do they avoid becoming bereft of Dharma?” [n35] {19}

“Karma is purified through firm resolution. [n36]<br>Māra is subdued through perseverance.<br>They avoid becoming bereft of the Dharma [n37]<br>By acting according to their words.” {20}

“How does one’s voice become like the pleasing song<br>Of the kalaviṅka bird and like the voice of Brahmā?<br>O Sage, [n38] how does one please beings<br>With their diverse inclinations?” [n39] {21}

“The voice of Brahmā is obtained by speaking truthfully.<br>The voice becomes pleasing by abandoning harsh words. [n40]<br>One pleases beings [n41]<br>By relinquishing senseless talk and slander.” [n42] {22}

“How does one come to have a long life?<br>How does one come to have fewer illnesses?<br>How, O Supreme Guide, [n43]<br>Does a retinue [n44] come to be undivided?” {23}

“One comes to have a long life by not killing.<br>One will have fewer illnesses by causing no harm. [n45]<br>A retinue comes to be undivided<br>When one reconciles disputes.” {24}

“How does one obtain wealth? [n46]<br>How do possessions cease to diminish?<br>How does one become distinguished<br>Wherever one is born?” {25} ^F28b

“Wealth is obtained through nonattachment. [n47]<br>Possessions increase by being without stinginess.<br>One will be distinguished wherever one is born<br>By being without jealousy.” [n48] {26}

“How does one obtain divine sight?<br>How does one obtain divine hearing?<br>And how, O Guide, does one know<br>The dying, transmigration, and rebirth of beings?” [n49] {27}

“Divine sight is obtained by giving lamps.<br>The ear is purified [n50] by giving music.<br>The dying, transmigration, and rebirth of beings are known through wisdom. [n51]<br>Thus do these accomplishments arise.” {28}

“O Guide, how do the defilements of bodhisattvas [n52]<br>Come to be exhausted?<br>How do they recollect previous lives?<br>How do they obtain miraculous powers?” [n53] {29}

“The miraculous powers are obtained by offering vehicles.<br>The recollections are gained by cultivating [n54] mindfulness.<br>They reach the cessation of defilements<br>By fully abandoning the two extremes.” {30}

“How is the buddhafield perfected?<br>How is its saṅgha perfected?<br>How does its light shine perfectly<br>Such that the ten directions are illuminated?” [n55] {31} [n56]

“The buddhafield is perfected through aspiration.<br>The saṅgha is perfected by magnetizing disciples. [n57]<br>The ten directions are illuminated brightly<br>By offering jeweled canopies.” {32}

“By what quality [n58] does one attain<br>The embodiment [n59] of all qualities,<br>So that a bodhisattva [n60] will be dear to beings<br>Wherever they are born?” [n61] {33}

“By surrendering the mind to the purpose of awakening<br>In order to liberate all beings, [n62]<br>They attain the embodiment of all qualities<br>And will thus be held dear.” {34}

“In what instruction should one train,<br>And how should one cultivate the mind,<br>To become an omniscient teacher<br>Like you, Great Sage?” {35} [n63]

“If one trains in being impartial<br>To all beings without exception,<br>And if the mind is cultivated in that impartiality,<br>One will become an all-surpassing victorious one.” {36}

“Throughout all one’s lives,<br>How will the mind of awakening<br>Never be abandoned even in a dream,<br>Let alone while awake?” [n64] {37} [n65] ^F29a

“In whatever region one dwells,<br>Whether a village or a city,<br>By correctly guiding others toward awakening,<br>The mind of awakening is not abandoned.” {38}

“Wherever one is born, [n66]<br>How can one acquire great strength<br>And always be elevated with power<br>So that even ten million māras can do no harm?” [n67] {39}

“In this life, if one offers the five essences [n68]<br>And gives courage to those who are afraid,<br>Then wherever one is born [n69]<br>One will also have great strength,<br>Always be elevated with power,<br>And be unharmed even by ten million māras.” [n70] {40} [n71]

Having heard this teaching<br>On the conduct of bodhisattvas [n72]<br>Directly from the Guardian of the World,<br>Siṃha [n73] was overjoyed. [n74] {41} [n75]

He and his companions<br>Spoke the following words: [n76]<br>“We will train<br>In this teaching on conduct.” [n77] {42}

Then the Buddha smiled,<br>And the trichiliocosm quaked. [n78]<br>Ajita then asked the Buddha<br>The following question:<br>“Why did you smile?<br>Great Sage, [n79] please explain this!” [n80] {43} [n81]

And so to Ajita<br>The Buddha replied,<br>“Ajita, please listen to the reason<br>That I smiled. [n82] {44}

“Previously, [n83] these boys [n84] assembled before<br>Eight hundred million buddhas and a quadrillion more. [n85]<br>For the purpose of awakening,<br>They made offerings to those guides of humans. [n86] {45}

“Since the time of [n87] Dīpaṅkara—<br>The perfect buddha, [n88] supreme among people [n89]—<br>I have been ripening<br>These brahmin boys. [n90] {46}

“When the splendid [n91] eon called<br>Great Illumination [n92] arises,<br>In that single eon<br>They will all become guides. [n93] {47} [n94]

“They will become guides for the world<br>Named Jñānaketudhvaja, [n95]<br>And their array will be<br>Just like that of Amitābha. {48}

“From the time of Maitreya<br>Through three hundred full eons,<br>They will always<br>Meet with buddhas. [n96] {49} ^F29b

“Moreover, whoever hears but the name<br>Under which they attain awakening<br>Shall, without exception, become incomparable<br>And omniscient beings in the world. [n97] {50}

“Whoever, on hearing this prophecy, [n98]<br>Becomes inspired<br>Will have merit unparalleled<br>Throughout these three worlds. {51}

“Consider someone who for six thousand eons<br>Filled up all the buddhafields [n99]<br>With jewels and offered them<br>For the purpose of awakening. [n100] {52}

“The tally of that person’s merit<br>Could never match or compare to<br>Hearing this Dharma presentation [n101]<br>And setting out for the sake of awakening.” [n102] {53}

When this Dharma had been expounded,<br>By the power of the Protector of the World, [n103]<br>The gods brought down a rain of flowers,<br>And the trichiliocosm quaked. {54}

Siṃha, along with his companions, [n104]<br>Obtained the five superknowledges and,<br>Having made offerings for one week,<br>Took ordination in the teaching. [n105] {55}

All of them obtained absorption<br>And inexhaustible [n106] retention.<br>Wishing to accomplish [n107] the welfare of beings,<br>They brought them to [n108] the Dharma of the buddhas. [n109] {56}

When this Dharma presentation was taught, eight hundred million beings set out for supreme awakening. [n110] When the Blessed One had spoken, Siṃha, along with his companions and the world with its gods, humans, asuras, and gandharvas, rejoiced and praised the words of the Blessed One. [n111]

The Indian preceptors Dānaśīla and Munivarman and the chief editor-translator Bandé Yeshé Dé translated, edited, and finalized this according to the new lexical register.


## Notes (84000)

[n1] The opening in the Sanskrit manuscript is somewhat unclear, as the traditional opening itself immediately enters into verse, and the description of the Buddha’s retinue is omitted. Vinītā suggests it is likely that this opening became corrupted through the processes of versification. See Vinītā 2010, vol. I,2, p. 456. Vinītā’s conjectural reconstruction for this opening is as follows: “Thus have I heard. At one time the Blessed One left Rājagṛha, / the victor, the unparalleled one, would roam about at the Vulture’s Peak” (evaṃ mayā śrutam ekasmin samaye bhagavān rājagṛhāt samuddhitaḥ | vyavahared gṛdhrakūṭe jino hy apratipudgalaḥ ||). “And together with a great saṅgha of bodhisattvas” is missing in Taishō 310.

[n2] These two lines are included in the first verse of the Potala Sanskrit manuscript, where Vinītā has conjecturally reconstructed the phrase jino hy apratipudgalaḥ (“the victor, the unparalleled one”). Because of this, verses 1–4 of the Tibetan are out of line with the Sanskrit but realign with verse 5 in both editions due to the omission in the Sanskrit of the second half of verse 4 (see ). These two lines are absent in Taishō 310.

[n3] Taishō 310 reads “the beloved son” (所愛之子).

[n4] It is curious that the anonymously translated Chinese version (Taishō 344) names the prince 和休 (he xiu). Old Chinese phonology (Karlgren 1957) give this as g’ăb χia̯b or similar, whereas Tang dynasty reconstructions (Stimson 1976) would suggest *huɑ*xiou, which approximates the Indic siṃha only if the sequence of syllables is reversed. Assuming the possibility that 和休 is metathesis, 休和 may denote an attempt to transcribe the Prakrit form sīha (Woolner 1912, p. 213), which also means “Lion.”

[n5] The N, H, and S editions of the Kangyur differ from D and the Sanskrit, as they all have “noblewomen,” or literally “daughters of merchants” (tshong dpon bu mo); however, all the Kangyur editions later on in verse 47 refer to Siṃha’s attendants as “these brahmin boys” (bram ze’i khye’u). Usually, the term khye’u refers to boys, so we have translated it accordingly. Taishō 310 reads “five hundred friends” (與其同友五百人俱。).

[n6] This line is absent in Taishō 310.

[n12] Taishō 310 reads that the cause for miraculous birth is generosity (Ch. 施, correlating to Skt. dāna). The line in Taishō 310 reads, “One becomes lotus-born through giving” (施蓮花化生).

[n14] This third line of the verse is absent in Taishō 310.

[n16] This fourth line of this verse is absent in Taishō 310.

[n48] For the last two lines of this verse the Sanskrit reads, “Without envy, one is distinguished. Being humble, one always becomes the best” (anīrṣayā maheśatvaṃ nirmānāgragataḥ sadā ||). [n49] Verses 27 through 30 constitute the six superknowledges (ṣaḍabhijñā), although for the third among these, which is represented in the last two lines of this verse, the Sanskrit reads differently: “How, O Guide, does one come to know / The arising thoughts of beings?” (cittotpattiṃ ca sattvānāṃ jānīte kena nāyaka). Taishō 310’s parallel verse supports the Sanskrit, although the two sets of questions appear in separate locations in the text and in the opposite order. The two older Chinese translations support the Tibetan, representing the third superknowledge as knowing the death and transmigration of beings, rather than their thoughts.
