# The Noble Great Vehicle Sūtra The Questions of Ratnajālin

> **84000 English, stored verbatim as a reference layer** (CC BY-NC-ND — do not excerpt or adapt for output; the shipped verse is our own translation, citing 84000). Tibetan source: `1-SOURCES/Text/bo-toh163.md`. Folio anchors `^F<folio>` align to the Degé Kangyur.

Homage to all buddhas and bodhisattvas! ^F144a

Thus did I hear at one time. The Blessed One was dwelling in Vaiśālī in a mansion located by the Monkey Pond, together with a great assembly of monks. In the assembly were four hundred twenty million bodhisattvas. Some of the bodhisattvas, such as Maitreya, were held back by just one birth, while others were held back by two, three, four, five, ten, thirty, or forty births, with some bodhisattvas even held back by one thousand births. Also in the assembly were six hundred million worthy ones and nine hundred ninety million gods including the gods in the desire realm and form realm, the four great kings, Śakra who rules the gods, Brahmā who rules the Sahā world, the god Great Splendor, the god Candraprabha, the god Sūryaprabha, the god Pinnacle of Renown, and the god Joyful. In attendance were also the nāga kings Anavatapta, Sāgara, Vāsukin, Manasvin, Nanda, Upananda, Mucilinda, and Mahāmucilinda, as well as the asura sovereign Rāhu and the retinues of all the other asura sovereigns.

At that time, in the city of Vaiśālī, there was a Licchavi named Limitless Strength, who was the son of Siṃha the captain. Limitless Strength, son of Siṃha, himself had a son, a young Licchavi boy known as Ratnajālin. Since this child had previously served many buddhas,  he had come to possess the lamp that shines with the immense light of the Dharma way. One time, when he was around eight years old, he had a dream as he slept. In his dream, the god Santuṣita descended from the Heaven of Joy and spoke to him, encouraging Ratnajālin with this melodious song: ^F144b

“The essential being, the Victor, the Buddha,<br>Abides, illuminating the entire trichiliocosm.<br>With vast accumulations of merit, he appears in golden hue.<br>The appearance of this great being is beautiful in all respects.

“He is endowed with the light of ten million suns<br>And shines like a thousand or even ten million<br>Luminous full moons—<br>As many as there are grains of sand in the Ganges.

“Shining like many millions of suns,<br>He shines like the supreme disk in the east,<br>Those in the south and the west,<br>And the supreme disk in the north.

“The light rays that stream forth<br>From each pore of this shining protector of the world<br>Will, each and every one,<br>Make all full moons appear gloomy.

“From each pore in the skin of those who are omniscient,<br>Light streams forth.<br>The light that shines from him illuminates as many worlds<br>As there are grains of sand in sixty-six Ganges rivers.”

In the morning, the Licchavi boy Ratnajālin told his father Limitless Strength, “Father, last night the god Santuṣita arrived from the Heaven of Joy and encouraged me with a melodious song. Father, I am telling you this so that we can begin the practice of worshiping the Thus-Gone One.”

At this point the Licchavi boy Ratnajālin sang these verses to his father Limitless Strength:

“The captain of all beings, the perfect Buddha,<br>The great being inconceivably learned in the ways of the world—<br>Please, may you worship this protector,<br>For such a guide rarely appears in the world. ^F145a

“Just like the uḍumbara flower,<br>He has a beautiful color and delicious scent and fragrance.<br>He is stainless and without afflictions.<br>Such a perfect flower is hard to find.

“Father, thus I supplicate you:<br>Right now, may you joyfully agree<br>To worship the luminous protector of the world<br>Who perceives the ultimate!”

The Licchavi Limitless Strength was delighted and addressed the Licchavi Siṃha:

“O Captain, follow my son’s advice<br>To worship the Victorious One!”

To worship the Guide, the Lion of the Śākyas,<br>And to delight the wonderful youth,<br>Six hundred million householders set to work.<br>Śakra with his thousand divine eyes,

The powerful gods Brahmā and Īśvara<br>And Susārthavāha, son of Māra,<br>Together with ten million earthly gods,<br>All came to the delight of Vaiśālī.

Here they produced<br>Billions of thrones made of gold and silver<br>And ornamented with the seven precious substances.<br>Each throne had billions of precious items.

Each side of the thrones was adorned with jewels<br>And had a fine victory banner of the seven precious substances attached.<br>The size of the thrones was several leagues.<br>The victory banners were half a league high.

On either side of the victory banners<br>Was a platform higher than a league<br>Made exclusively of a single substance—<br>Precious jewels rising more than a league in height.

In between the thrones divine banners were hung<br>More than a league long.<br>The banners featured nets of golden tassels<br>Adorned with strings of bells.

Beneath the thrones were well-fastened columns,<br>More than one billion of them.<br>All of these perfect thrones<br>Were firmly placed, as if bolted to the ground.

The surface of the thrones was made of gold—<br>Golden and with silver and beryl,<br>Crystal, coral, and emerald too.<br>Also, at the bottom and the base ^F145b

Were placed natural pearl parasols,<br>Numbering in the billions<br>And colored red with deep red sandalwood.<br>Having produced the thrones

And the heavenly ornaments, [n1] clear as water, on the thrones, [n2]<br>Golden nets were placed on them<br>And adorned with gold,<br>Silver, beryl, and crystal.

Having produced the ornaments of coral and emerald,<br>Affixed to those were sweet-sounding bells<br>Resounding with the voice of the Guide.<br>Fumigants were placed there for veneration.

In the sky above Vaiśālī<br>Was a canopy sewn of golden thread.<br>From each and every golden thread<br>Hung billions of tassels of the finest jewels

And billions of ornamental garlands of pearl<br>Strung with golden thread.<br>All the Licchavis who lived in the city of Vaiśālī<br>Were joyful, regardless of where they went.

They covered the ground of the city of Vaiśālī<br>With pieces of cloth the length of seven men<br>Printed with lattices of red sandalwood<br>And made of well-spun fabrics.

On the right side, seventy billion instruments<br>Of thousands of different varieties rang forth.<br>On the left side, a further seventy billion<br>Were also engaged in venerating the Lion of the Śākyas.

Having set forth such a great array,<br>The Licchavi boy named Ratnajālin<br>Joyfully announced to his parents,<br>“I am going before the Buddha.”

This learned one left the city<br>And went to where the great victorious lord was staying.<br>The young man bowed his head at the feet of this ocean of virtues<br>And stood to one side.

Then the Licchavi boy Ratnajālin left the city of Vaiśālī and went to where the Blessed One was staying. He bowed to the feet of the Blessed One and stood to one side. With him standing there to the side, the Blessed One  delivered a Dharma discourse to the Licchavi boy Ratnajālin that delighted, captivated, and uplifted him, and made him utterly delighted. ^F146a

The Licchavi boy Ratnajālin then stood up, draped his shawl over one shoulder, and knelt on his right knee. With his palms together he bowed toward the Blessed One and asked, “Would the Blessed One and his monastic saṅgha agree to take tomorrow’s midday meal at my home?”

Out of love for the Licchavi boy Ratnajālin, the Blessed One showed his acceptance of the offer by remaining silent. The Licchavi boy Ratnajālin understood that the Blessed One had accepted through his silence, and so he circumambulated the Blessed One three times before taking his leave.

The Licchavi boy Ratnajālin then entered the city of Vaiśālī along with Śakra who rules the gods, Brahmā who rules the Sahā world, and the god Great Splendor. With enthusiasm and delight, they began to prepare abundant varieties of food. When the night had passed, they set out cushions and decorated Vaiśālī. They then returned to the Blessed One, bowed to him, and stood to one side. The Licchavi boy Ratnajālin then said to the Blessed One, “Blessed One, it is time for the midday meal. If it please you, your meal is ready. The time has come.”

Accordingly, on that morning the Blessed One donned his lower garments and Dharma robes. Bearing his alms bowl, with his infinite saṅgha of monks he rose into the sky to the height of seven men  and traveled to the city of Vaiśālī. As soon as he moved, the transformative power of the Thus-Gone One caused all the worlds of the great trichiliocosm to shake six times as they quivered, trembled, and quaked; wobbled, rocked, and swayed; vibrated, shuddered, and reeled; clattered, rattled, and clanged; and tremored, shook, and convulsed. Thus they shook six times with eighteen omens. He also displayed trillions of miracles as he traveled to the city of Vaiśālī. At that time, trillions of gods scattered and tossed blue, pink, red, and white lotuses, flowers made of precious divine substances, the powdered aloewood of the gods, powdered gems, gold from the Jambu river, and powdered divine gems upon the Blessed One. Trillions of divine instruments resounded. Unfathomable divine ornaments adorned him. In the sky above, a steady rain of red sandalwood powder fell to the depth of a chariot axle. ^F146b

The Blessed One then arrived at the Licchavi boy Ratnajālin’s home and sat upon the seat that had been prepared for him along with the saṅghas of fully ordained monks and nuns and the assemblies of novice monks and nuns. Seeing that he had arrived, the Licchavi boy Ratnajālin personally served the Blessed One with an abundance of the finest food, drink, and sweets, to please him and serve him everything he wished for. When the Licchavi boy Ratnajālin noticed that the Blessed One had finished eating, he picked up the alms bowl, offered water, and covered the Blessed One’s hands. He then dressed the Blessed One with a priceless set of the three Dharma robes. He also dressed the saṅghas of fully ordained monks and nuns and the assemblies of novice monks and nuns with sets of the three Dharma robes. Why? He did this because he was moved by the power of the Blessed One. ^F147a

The Licchavi boy Ratnajālin then asked the Blessed One, “If the Blessed One were to grant me the opportunity to request a teaching, I would like to ask the Blessed Thus-Gone One a few questions.”

The Blessed One responded to the Licchavi boy Ratnajālin, “Young man, you may ask the Thus-Gone One anything you are concerned with. Then I shall delight you with teachings that answer your questions.”

The Licchavi boy Ratnajālin then asked the Blessed One, “Blessed One, in other worlds, are there blessed buddhas that through their previous aspirations have names that, when merely heard, help noble sons and daughters who follow the Bodhisattva Vehicle to end all doubt and hesitation, to become irreversible and fully awaken to unsurpassed and perfect buddhahood, to gain recollection of past lives, to never have a stingy attitude, to be surrounded by the bodhisattva assembly and to be without the assembly of hearers, to acquire countless trillions of virtues, to satisfy all beings by pleasing all the buddhas, to terrify the māras, to purify all buddha realms, to cleanse the stains in the minds of all beings, to inspire all beings to the virtuous Dharma, to be protected by the gods, to be considered by the bodhisattvas, to be blessed by the thus-gone ones, to never be separated from the blessed buddhas, to perfect bodhisattva conduct by possessing the finest qualities, to gain the entirety of the 84,000 verbal expressions, to attain the voice of Brahmā, and to gain a voice that is in conformity with all worlds?” ^F147b

The Blessed One answered the Licchavi boy Ratnajālin, “Excellent, excellent, young man. Your thought to ask the Thus-Gone One about such subjects reflects excellent eloquence and fine analysis. Thus, listen well, young man, and bear what I say in mind. Then I will teach you.”

“Blessed One, I shall do just that.”

The bodhisattva Ratnajālin listened as the Blessed One had instructed, and so the Blessed One said, “Young man, to the east of here there is a world called Pearled. Young man, consider this: There are as many peaks of existence as there are grains of sand in the Ganges. And all those too are filled with the smallest possible particles. If a person then came along and carried away one single such particle and placed it in a spot past as many trillions of buddha realms as there are smallest particles in all those world systems under the peaks of existence—then, young man, by repeating this in the same way for a long time, would that great mass of particles eventually run out  and reach its limit? Now, young man, tell me, would it be easy to consider, count, assess, or determine the places in the universe where that person placed or did not place those particles?” ^F148a

“Blessed One, it would not be easy. Blessed One, for that reason, anyone who gets the right sense of this analogy that you have expressed would gain a great degree of understanding.”

The Blessed One said, “Young man, if all the worlds in which that person placed a particle, or alternatively the worlds in which that person did not place a particle—ranging from the mass of water underlying these worlds up to their peaks—were filled with minute particles, and if a second person came along and removed a single particle from them at a time and placed it in a spot past as many trillions of buddha realms as there were minute particles in those worlds, then, young man, by repeating this for a long time, eventually that great mass of particles would become dispersed. Young man, by this way of proceeding, the latter person would travel past nine million nine hundred thousand incalculable numbers of worlds and thus arrive past countless trillions of buddha realms.

“That is where the world called Pearled is located. There the thus-gone one, the worthy one, the perfect Buddha Majestic King with the Splendorous Voice of Learning Adorned by Precious Moonlight lives, abides, and teaches the Dharma. Young man, those noble sons or daughters who abide in the Bodhisattva Vehicle, who have no doubt regarding the name of the thus-gone, worthy, perfect Buddha Majestic King with the Splendorous Voice of Learning Adorned by Precious Moonlight,  and who trust in my vision, will attain the dominion of a universal monarch after their lives are over. A buddha will appear within their dominions. When they see that thus-gone one, they will perform boundless veneration, practice pure conduct, and master the five types of miraculous abilities. Immediately upon beholding that thus-gone one, they will achieve the hundred-swirled dhāraṇī. They will serve as many buddhas as there are grains of sand in the Ganges. They will turn away from saṃsāra for an equivalent number of eons. Their minds will be undeluded, and they will fully awaken to unsurpassed and perfect buddhahood. Their bodies will become solid like that of Nārāyaṇa. Residing in a single place, they will possess the four necessities. [n3] Their bodies will take on golden complexions. They will be adorned with the thirty-two major marks of a great being. They will gain the melodious voice of Brahmā. They will eliminate all unfree states and attain an abundance of leisure.” ^F148b

In order to elaborate on the meaning of what he had taught, the Blessed One then spoke in verse:

“Those who hear the name of this world protector<br>And entertain no doubt,<br>Trusting in what I see,<br>Shall become worthy of veneration.

“They will attain the eternal dominion<br>Of universal monarchs and behold buddhas.<br>Having beheld them, they will perform<br>Boundless and inconceivable veneration.

“They will always practice pure conduct.<br>They will realize all forms of miraculous abilities.<br>Once they see the Buddha, they will achieve<br>The hundred-swirled dhāraṇī.

“They will behold as many buddhas<br>As there are sands in the Ganges.<br>They will abandon saṃsāra<br>For that many billions of eons.

“Those who hear the name of the world protector<br>Spoken to the assemblies and saṅghas<br>Will become undeluded,<br>And it will be a cause for awakening to buddhahood. ^F149a

“They will always be like Nārāyaṇa.<br>Residing in a single place, they will possess the four necessities. [n4]<br>Their bodies will be golden colored<br>And display the thirty-two marks.

“Those who hear his name<br>And prostrate with their five points<br>Will permanently gain the voice of Brahmā<br>And abandon the unfree states.

“Those who hear the name of this world protector<br>And entertain no doubt<br>Will not shy away from awakening.<br>They shall never have any hesitation.

“Those who remember the victor’s name<br>For a full seven days and nights<br>Will purify the infinite seeing eyes<br>Praised by the buddhas.

“They shall refine the eyes of flesh<br>Into the eyes of a god.<br>They will perceive as many buddhas<br>As there are grains of sand in the Ganges.

“They will venerate each of them<br>And receive teachings from each as well.<br>Seeing all the buddhas with their own eyes,<br>They will bring comfort to all humanity.

“Those who hear the name of this thus-gone one<br>Have already offered unfathomable service<br>To the buddhas,<br>All of whom they will recollect.

“If his name is relayed<br>Before any being at all,<br>When those other beings hear this name,<br>They too will definitely proceed to awakening.

“By generating faith in this buddha,<br>The unfree states are dispelled,<br>While by engaging in debate and quarreling,<br>One will be born into the unfree states.

“Once beings have heard<br>The name of this great sage,<br>They shall no longer be interested<br>In debate and quarreling.

“The actions that lead to the Hell of Ultimate Torment,<br>Which could last for eons,<br>As well as the actions that produce a simple headache—<br>All such actions will not ripen for these beings.

“They shall not be burned by fire,<br>Nor drown in water.<br>Even when they draw the ire of the king,<br>It shall be quickly assuaged. ^F149b

“Those who remember this sage’s name<br>Shall be spared many illnesses<br>Including blindness, defective sight,<br>Paralysis, hunchback, and decrepitude.

“Evil and malevolent<br>Yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras,<br>Kumbhāṇḍas, rākṣasas, and humans<br>Shall be unable to harm them.

“Those who remember this sage’s name<br>Shall be favored and preferred<br>By all the bhūtas, gandharvas, yakṣas,<br>Nāgas, and gods.

“Those who hear this protector’s name<br>Shall swiftly attain vast accumulations,<br>Sublime voices, and absorption.<br>Their myriad virtues will be renowned.

“Having heard this world protector’s name<br>Without doubting it,<br>They will hew to awakening<br>Throughout all their subsequent lives.

“Those who hear this great sage’s name<br>Shall uphold and preach<br>The way of the well-spoken Dharma<br>Taught by the buddhas who have gone beyond anguish.

“They shall uphold the teachings<br>Well spoken by all the buddhas.<br>They shall manifest the supreme array<br>And serve all the buddhas.

“Once they pass away,<br>They will swiftly behold myriad buddhas<br>And uphold the teachings<br>Spoken by the buddhas.

“Those who hear this protector’s name<br>Shall earnestly train and establish<br>Millions of beings on the path,<br>Bringing them all to awakening.

“Those who trust what I have seen<br>Shall uphold the teachings<br>Of all the buddhas, the protectors,<br>And additionally venerate the buddhas.

“Those who have faith in this buddha’s wisdom,<br>Trusting in what I have seen,<br>Shall be liberated from many fetters.<br>They will cross the abyss and become fearless.

“Whosoever abandons the sublime Dharma<br>Is among Māra’s ranks. [n5]

“The body hairs of every being<br>Who migrates among the five types of beings<br>Could be consolidated<br>Within one hair on the teacher’s kneecap.

“From a single pore<br>Of the protector, the guardian of the world,<br>As many rays of light as there are<br>Grains of sand in the Ganges stream forth. ^F150a

“Those who trust in the infinite light<br>Of the world protector<br>Shall in one life behold many buddhas—<br>Millions of buddhas.

“Those who hear the teaching<br>Of this discourse by the Dharma king<br>And never doubt it<br>Shall become like me.

“Those who hear the teaching<br>Of this discourse in Vaiśālī<br>Shall themselves preach it to others<br>At a later time.

“Those who preach this widely<br>Shall come to pervade as many worlds<br>As there are grains of sand in the Ganges<br>With a single burst of light.

“One may fill with gold<br>All the buddha realms,<br>The numbers of which defy counting, [n6]<br>And offer it to the world protectors.

“Yet, in comparison, those who hear this name<br>And who are truly delighted by it<br>And utter their homage to the buddhas<br>Obtain inconceivably more merit.

“The mass of merit gained<br>By repeatedly paying homage<br>To all the world protectors<br>Cannot be expressed by any measure.

“Those who hear the world protector’s name<br>And do not hesitate about it<br>Will have their bodies filled with light<br>And become like precious gems.

“After they have passed beyond,<br>Those who remember the name of the sage<br>Will have the sweet scent of divine red sandalwood<br>Waft from their mouth.

“Those who hear the name<br>Of the Buddha, the protector, the essential being,<br>Will have light stream from their pores,<br>Beautifying their bodies.

“In all of their ensuing lives<br>They will become deeply learned.<br>They will retain their unfathomable erudition,<br>Just like Ānanda does for the Buddha.

“When they behold the one who illuminates the world,<br>They will attain boundless happiness.<br>As they engage in bodhisattva conduct,<br>They will recall all their lives.

“Even when they see the victors’ heart sons,<br>They will attain boundless happiness.<br>They will make extensive offerings<br>With incense, flowers, and ointments. ^F150b

“This does not include those who do not remember<br>The name of this world protector when it is spoken,<br>Or those who are on Māra’s side,<br>Or those who have previously been extremists.

“As I have taught this Dharma way,<br>Those who have heard it now<br>Shall also receive this discourse in their hands<br>In a future life.

“Those who hear the explanation<br>Of this sublime discourse<br>Have previously venerated and served,<br>And they shall receive it in their hands again.

“Those who make aspirations<br>Shall, in a future life,<br>Receive into their hands<br>This discourse spoken by the Dharma King.

“Having faith in this discourse<br>Brings even more merit<br>Than honoring countless buddhas<br>With a one-pointed mind.

“They forever will have beautiful bodies and lots of merit<br>And be fearless and wealthy.<br>They will be mindful<br>And ornamented with the major marks.

“Thus, having heard<br>The teaching of this discourse,<br>In the future retain it, teach it,<br>And explain it to others!

“Those who have honored hundreds of thousands<br>Of buddhas, universal monarchs,<br>Shall also in the future<br>Have vision free from doubt.

“Those who have performed service<br>For countless world protectors in the past<br>Will trust what they hear and see<br>Of this world protector.

“Those who hear his name<br>Will actualize the form of a buddha.<br>They will guard the teachings<br>And the sublime Dharma of all the buddhas.

“However, those who abandon the teachings of awakening<br>Shall become like sheep—mute and with poor eyesight,<br>Or even with no eyes at all<br>For millions of eons.

“Compared to destroying countless caityas,<br>With one’s own hands,<br>It is a far viler deed<br>To abandon this discourse.

“Thus when, at a future time,<br>You attain that which I am preaching,<br>Do not harbor any doubt<br>About the unsurpassed wisdom of the buddhas. ^F151a

“Once you have heard this discourse,<br>Perform respectful veneration<br>With flowers, incense, streamers,<br>Parasols, and powders!”

Then, with his soft and supple hands,<br>The Thus-Gone One<br>Repeatedly caressed<br>The Licchavi boy’s head.

After this, the gods<br>Made a loud cry<br>And scattered pleasing divine flowers,<br>Filling the realm.

The gods, having remained in the sky,<br>Then sang countless songs.<br>Millions of brahmā gods<br>Cast down a rain of red sandalwood powder.

When Ratnajālin heard this discourse<br>Spoken by the Thus-Gone One,<br>He was pleased and delighted,<br>And spoke these words to the Thus-Gone One:

“In the future,<br>I will become a monk<br>And fearlessly preach this discourse<br>In towns and villages.

“I will act according to the Buddha’s teaching,<br>Which the perfect Buddha has spoken so gloriously.<br>Traveling millions of leagues<br>I will preach the way.

“I will serve all the gods, nāgas,<br>And kinnaras,<br>And other non-human beings,<br>Satisfying them with the gift of Dharma.”

When this unsurpassed discourse<br>Was taught by the Buddha,<br>The assembly of asuras celebrated<br>And scattered countless flowers.

A single hair of the Dharma King,<br>The Lion of the Śākyas,<br>Who is the supreme being in all buddha realms,<br>Illuminated all the realms.

Then, the tongue of the Teacher,<br>The great being, the essential being,<br>Emerged and covered millions of realms<br>As numerous as the grains of sand in the Ganges.

As the Thus-Gone One covered millions of realms<br>Throughout the ten directions of the universe,<br>The youth Ratnajālin,<br>As well as other heroes and Licchavis

Numbering six hundred million,<br>Arrived saying, “Please teach us!”<br>The Teacher then introduced<br>The Dharma to them all. ^F151b

The buddhas gift every form of happiness,<br>And their eyes are unimpeded.<br>The countless thus-gone ones<br>Have abandoned doubt about awakened qualities.

The buddhas’ wisdom is unimpeded—<br>It perceives the three times.<br>Given that they have dominion over all phenomena,<br>There is nothing unknown to the buddhas.

The Guide of Humans preaches<br>That the aggregates are nonexistent.<br>The four elements are as if hollow,<br>And nowhere are there sounds to be attached to.

The essence of the aggregates<br>Of form, feeling, perception,<br>Thoughts, formations, and consciousness<br>Should be known to be nonexistent.

Just as the Buddha has taught,<br>If one distinguishes the aggregates,<br>One will swiftly attain the dhāraṇīs<br>And become undeluded.

All phenomena are void—<br>All of them are empty and without self.<br>Of this there can be no debate or delusion.<br>This is what the Thus-Gone One has taught.

“Young man, located in the south, past twelve buddha realms reckoned as twice the number of smallest particles in the previous analogy, there is a world called Adorned by the Moon. There resides the thus-gone, worthy, perfect Buddha King of Knowledge of Floral Light Displays. He is alive and well and teaches the Dharma. Young man, when those noble sons or daughters who abide in the Bodhisattva Vehicle hear the name of the blessed one, the thus-gone one King of Knowledge of Floral Light Displays—as long as they do not doubt this but trust in my vision—they will achieve the absorption of emitting light rays once they have passed away. They will become knowledgeable in ten thousand countless trillions of methods to gain absorption.  They will become knowledgeable in sixty countless trillions of dhāraṇī methods—from the oceanic dhāraṇī to the precious treasury dhāraṇī. They will be undeluded in this regard until reaching final awakening. When they die, ten million buddhas will come from the east and reveal themselves, and the same will occur from all other directions. They will retain the Dharma that these blessed buddhas teach. Until they reach awakening they will not lose this Dharma way. Having worked to turn away from cyclic existence for five hundred eons starting from the time they began as bodhisattvas, they will reach unsurpassed and perfect awakening.” ^F152a

The Blessed One then expressed this in verse:

“Hearing the name of this protector,<br>The lord of humans, the world protector,<br>They will eliminate five hundred eons<br>Of cyclic existence.

“When death comes to those noble sons and daughters,<br>Millions of buddhas will appear to them.<br>Having seen them, paying homage with their faculties,<br>They will listen to the Dharma and retain it.

“When they hear that thus-gone one’s name<br>And engage in sacred practice,<br>They will not waste the Dharma taught by them—<br>Even for millions of eons.

“By proclaiming the name of this buddha,<br>They will attain seventy thousand<br>Absorptions known as precious,<br>And they will proclaim millions of virtues.

“Those who apprehend the name of this sage<br>Will come to abide<br>In countless millions<br>Plus sixty million absorptions.

“Thus, when heroes of equilibrium<br>Engage in bodhisattva conduct,<br>They establish many thousands of beings<br>In supreme awakening.

“Until reaching perfect awakening,<br>They will not engage in desirous conduct.<br>The beings to whom they teach the name of this buddha<br>Will also turn away from desire.

“Those who hear the name of this buddha<br>And teach it to all beings<br>Will never again enter unfree states;<br>It will not be hard for them to find leisure. ^F152b

“By pleasing the excellent [n7] buddhas<br>That appear,<br>They will abandon all the unfree states [n8]<br>That I have described. [n9]

“Young man, if you were to understand<br>What the guide knows,<br>Once you knew the name of this teacher,<br>You would spread it throughout the ten directions.

“Thus, young man,<br>If you share this name with others,<br>You will acquire genuine awakening<br>And become a protector of the world.

“Those who hear this buddha’s name,<br>Remember it without distraction,<br>And share it with others,<br>Will guard their awakening.

“Those who hear this discourse<br>And teach it to others<br>Will guard these teachings<br>Of the well-spoken Dharma way.”

“Young man, located in the west, past worlds numbering three times the number of smallest particles in the previous analogy, there is a world called Citraratna. There the thus-gone, worthy, perfect Buddha King of Blossoming Sal Tree Flowers lives. He is alive and well and teaches the Dharma. Young man, when those noble sons or daughters who abide in the Great Vehicle hear the name of the thus-gone one King of Blossoming Sal Tree Flowers—as long as they do not doubt this but trust in my vision—they will acquire five qualities once they have passed away. What are these five? When their bodies are destroyed, buddhas will appear and they will attain the position of a universal monarch, the five forms of miraculous abilities, the recall of Dharma teachings, the thousand-swirled dhāraṇī, and the thirty-two marks of a great being until they attain awakening. They will achieve these five qualities.”

The Blessed One then expressed the meaning of this in verse: ^F153a

“Those who hear the name of this leader of men, this protector,<br>And are faithful, without hesitation,<br>Will acquire these five qualities,<br>Which shall not be lost until they attain awakening.

“They will gain a golden-colored appearance,<br>The supreme knowledge of a universal monarch,<br>Merit rivaling that of Nārāyaṇa,<br>And the unceasing turning of the wheel of the sacred Dharma.

“They will be fortunate and have a thousand sons<br>Who are heroic and courageous, capable of defeating their opponents.<br>Adorned with marks, their bodies will have perfect limbs.<br>Therefore, their power will resemble that of a supreme ruler.

“They will have many thousands of queens<br>Who are fine and beautiful, with golden complexions.<br>Going late to bed and rising early,<br>They will never cause their supreme rulers any sorrow.

“The vassals, ministers, and major householders<br>Living in his realm<br>Will never cause the king any grief when they meet.<br>Their merit will even be greater than that.

“Even the crafty gods will find them difficult to match.<br>All the yakṣas and nāgas living in the human world<br>And all the other beings living in his realm<br>They will establish at the seat of awakening.

“Their dominion will expand and the harvests grow bountiful.<br>Their realm will be happy and buddhas will appear.<br>They will perform sacred worship of them.<br>The land of the king will be free from malice.

“Even those scholars who hear the perfect name of this buddha,<br>Yet doubt it or become displeased with it,<br>Will benefit from the perfect Dharma<br>In the sublime discourse that I have spoken today.

“However, this is not the case for those who abandoned the Dharma<br>Of previous victors, or those in the grip of demons,<br>Or those who in the past despised<br>The essential beings, the perfect victors.

“Young man, those who haven’t witnessed the delights<br>Of the royal palace and this realm of the Licchavis<br>And who haven’t seen me<br>Will harbor doubts when they hear the name of this buddha. ^F153b

“Young man, located in the north, past sixty buddha realms reckoned as thrice the number of smallest particles in the previous analogy, there is a world called Covered with a Jewel Net. There the thus-gone, worthy, perfect Buddha Pure Moon Disk lives. He is alive and well and teaches the Dharma. Young man, when those noble sons or daughters who abide in the Great Vehicle hear the name of the thus-gone one Pure Moon Disk—as long as they do not doubt this but trust in my vision—they will obtain the absorption called source of jewels and behold as many buddhas as there are grains of sand in the Ganges once they have passed away. For as many millions of eons, they will turn away from cyclic existence. Such beginning bodhisattvas will reach unsurpassed and perfect awakening. They will also attain the absorption called pleasant melody. They will not turn away from unsurpassed and perfect awakening.

“Should women hear the name of the blessed thus-gone one Pure Moon Disk without feeling any hesitation or doubt and with trust in my vision, they will abandon their female faculties and gain male faculties once they have passed away. They will establish many beings in unsurpassed and perfect awakening. They will achieve the absorption called pleasant melody. They will not turn back from unsurpassed and perfect awakening.”

The Blessed One then expressed this in verse:

“Those who hear the name of this sublime human<br>And do not doubt it<br>Will, after as many eons as there are grains of sand in the Ganges,<br>Abandon rebirth and change to a state free from anguish. ^F154a

“They will behold and venerate<br>Buddhas as numerous as the sands of the Ganges.<br>All those buddhas they will venerate<br>In a single life, as the victors pass beyond anguish.

“Those who hear the name of this leader of men<br>As they engage in the perfect actions of sacred awakening<br>And remember it without ever doubting it<br>Will achieve a similar exalted state.

“Those who hear the name of this supreme human<br>And do not doubt it at all<br>Shall obtain self-existing wisdom, buddhahood itself,<br>And skillfully attain the absorption called pleasant voice.

“As they engage in the perfect actions of sacred awakening,<br>They will achieve the faculties of a pure individual.<br>They will be beautiful and endowed with all qualities—<br>Such distinctions they will attain.

“They will shine in all directions<br>And proceed to countless millions of realms.<br>As they behold the great sage engaging with the world, [n10]<br>They will escape anguish and later discover awakening.

“Having heard the name<br>Of that great lord, that thus-gone one,<br>They will attain such distinctions as perfect miraculous powers,<br>And also the desire to attain awakening.

“Having developed interest in immaculate awakening,<br>They will share the name of the victor with others.<br>They will obtain virtues similar to his—<br>As brilliant as the full moon.

“Such learned persons will establish<br>Fathomless multitudes of beings in supreme awakening.<br>Like the chariot of the moon in the sky,<br>They will be beautiful and shine brightly.

“Those who remember the name of this victor<br>Will have bodies that are just as beautiful.<br>Yet some scholars who hear the name of this victor<br>Might doubt it, finding displeasure in it.

“As they engage in the perfect actions of sacred awakening,<br>These teachings will be helpful for them.<br>Due to the supreme great sage who engages with the world,<br>Such scholars will not come to ruin. ^F154b

“Young man, located below us, past ninety-nine times the number of particles in the previous analogy, there is a world called Lord of the Supreme Banner. There resides the thus-gone, worthy, perfect Buddha King Who Is Peaceful and Splendorous in His Learning and Rich in Melody. He is alive and well and teaches the Dharma. Young man, when those noble sons or daughters who abide in the Great Vehicle hear the name of the thus-gone one King Who is Peaceful and Splendorous in His Learning and Rich in Melody—as long as they do not doubt this but trust in my vision—they will achieve the absorption called utterly illuminating once they have passed away. At the time of death, they will see trillions of buddhas. In all directions, as in the east, they will see trillions of buddhas. They will retain all the Dharma teachings given by these blessed buddhas. Until reaching awakening, they will be blessed by trillions of buddhas. Such beginning bodhisattvas, having turned from cyclic existence for ninety-nine trillion eons, will reach unsurpassed and perfect awakening.”

The Blessed One then expressed the meaning of this in verse:

“Those in this world who hear [n11] the supreme name<br>Of this buddha with an ocean of virtues<br>Will swiftly perform unsurpassed veneration<br>Of one hundred billion buddhas.

“Once they have venerated these limitless beings free from stains,<br>They will engage in the splendorous activity<br>Of the magnificent buddhas in all existences.<br>Later, they will also reach the perfect insight of awakening.

“Those who, in a single life, venerate as many guides<br>As there are grains of sand in the Ganges<br>Attain the utterly illuminating absorption<br>And subsequently reach awakening beyond anguish. ^F155a

“Having obtained immaculate awakening, great awakening,<br>They will turn the sublime wheel of Dharma,<br>Whereby they will establish beings in the supreme awakening of buddhahood,<br>Transcending anguish just as rainfall quenches flames.

“The guide of humans, the charioteer of humans,<br>Is the single field of merit, the single mount of merit.<br>If people simply develop faith toward that buddha,<br>They will instantly discard all unfree states.

“Those who recite the name of this well-gone one<br>Will obtain human bodies and gain leisure.<br>Then these wise persons will create merit<br>In the limitless ocean wherein the thus-gone ones dwell.

“Those who chant the name of this buddha<br>A mere one hundred thousand times will attain concordant acceptance<br>And the attainment of the divine eye, just as I did. [n12]

“When I saw the Buddha Dīpaṃkara I scattered flowers<br>And thereby attained acceptance.<br>They will acquire that sort of acceptance.

“Only those who are extremists,<br>Or who suffer from jealousy<br>Would not want teachings<br>Upon hearing the name of this illuminating sage who protects the world.

“Those who hear such a discourse taught<br>Directly from the guide, the Buddha,<br>Will remember it in the future<br>And teach it to others.

“Young man, located above this buddha realm, as in the previous analogy, past as many realms as there are grains of sand in the Ganges, there is a world called Wide Open. There the thus-gone, worthy, perfect Buddha Stable in Immeasurable Diligence lives. He is alive and well and teaches the Dharma. Young man, when those noble sons or daughters who abide in the Great Vehicle hear the name of the thus-gone one Stable in Immeasurable Diligence—as long as they do not doubt this  but trust in my vision—their diligence will never decrease once they have passed away. Never again will they indulge in desire. Never again will they be stained by the suffering of a mother. Never again will they be stained by the suffering of a father, relative, and friend. Never will they be stained by the suffering of a sister. Never again will they be stained by the suffering of a companion, ally, clansman, or kinsman. Never will their bodies not be adorned by the thirty-two marks of a great being. Never again will their attachment, aggression, and ignorance increase. Never again will any diseases proliferate. Never again will any anguish grow. Instead, they will gain limitless happiness. On the way to awakening, they will gain countless trillions of limitless virtues.” ^F155b

The Blessed One then expressed the meaning of this in verse:

“Those who hear the name of this world protector<br>And do not doubt it<br>Will never have to witness<br>The suffering of parents and many relatives.

“Above us abides the supreme human,<br>Who illuminates the world.<br>He illuminates the three realms of existence<br>And teaches beings the Dharma.

“Those who hear the name of this perfect buddha<br>And teach it to others<br>Will be perennially adorned<br>With the thirty-two marks.

“Those who remember the name of this sage<br>Will not have their insight impaired.<br>When they are engaged in the practices of awakening,<br>They will venerate the bodhisattvas as well.

“By teaching this buddha’s name,<br>When later they practice for awakening,<br>These insightful ones shall constantly venerate<br>Inconceivable millions of buddhas.

“Those who hear the name of this perfect buddha<br>And then reveal it to others<br>Will obtain a mass of merit<br>The measure of which is impossible to describe. ^F156a

“Those who hear this name<br>Shall never come to doubt—<br>Even for the longest time in the future—<br>The unsurpassed wisdom of buddhahood.

“Those who see this perfect buddha<br>And render service and honor unto him<br>Shall receive this discourse<br>In their hands at a future time.

“Those who see me surrounded by bodhisattvas,<br>As well as Ratnajālin,<br>And hear this discourse<br>Will not come to doubt it.

“They will abandon any doubt<br>In the boundless thus-gone ones and their buddha qualities.<br>They will continue their earlier practices<br>For many billions of eons.

“They will give up their hands and legs<br>And give away their ears, noses, heads,<br>Sons, daughters, and subjects<br>Without any displeasure.

“When those who hear this discourse<br>Come in contact with it in the future,<br>They shall teach it to others,<br>And so become worthy of veneration.

“Let those who desire complete awakening,<br>Which is the domain of all the buddhas,<br>Never doubt the teachings<br>Of the world protector!

“Those who have heard this discourse<br>Must uphold it free from deceit.<br>They should preach this discourse again and again,<br>Wherever they may go.

“If they can perfect the meaning of this discourse,<br>Entirely within three months,<br>They shall attain the wisdom of recall<br>That recollects all teachings.”

Once the Young Licchavi Ratnajālin had heard these well-spoken teachings of the Blessed One, he draped the Blessed One in priceless fabrics ornamented with gold. Then the Blessed One performed a miracle such that from every pore of the Blessed One’s body, light streamed forth, illuminating all the countless, infinite worlds in the east. All the beings born in those buddha realms beheld this buddha realm, and all the beings gathered in the Thus-Gone One’s  assembly in this buddha realm also beheld this buddha realm. At that moment as many blessed buddhas, including their fourfold assemblies, were illuminated as ninety-nine times the number of grains of sand in the Ganges. Such was the miracle that he performed. ^F156b

The Blessed One then asked the young Licchavi Ratnajālin, “Young man, when you look above, do you see all the blessed buddhas, who are so unfathomably, innumerably, limitlessly, immeasurably, and indescribably many?”

“Blessed One, I see them. Well-Gone One, I see them.”

“Noble son, the names of these thus-gone ones vastly outnumber the names of the gods and humans gathered in the worlds of the great trichiliocosm. Each of them has an inconceivable and boundless agglomeration of names. This also applies to the thus-gone one Majestic King with the Elegant Peaceful Voice in the east, and likewise to the thus-gone one Pure Moon Disk, the thus-gone one King of Knowledge of Floral Light Displays, the thus-gone one King of Blossoming Sal Tree Flowers, and the thus-gone one Majestic King with the Splendorous Voice of Learning Adorned by Precious Moonlight. Just as it is with the name of a single thus-gone one, so it is with that of any thus-gone one. Do you see that?”

“Blessed One, I see it. Well-Gone One, I see it. So it is in the south, in the west, in the north, below, and above—in each of the ten directions there are countless names of the individual thus-gone ones and myriad different names of the thus-gone ones.” ^F157a

“Young man, do you see how the blessed buddhas vastly exceed those who abide in the realm of a universal monarch?”

“Blessed One, I see it. Well-Gone One, I see it.”

The young Licchavi Ratnajālin became pleased, delighted, joyful, and happy. At that moment when he was happy and delighted the earth quaked. A great burst of light appeared that benefitted all beings, brought them happiness, pleased their minds, and aroused roots of virtue in them. Trillions of gods stationed in the sky cast down powdered gold from the divine Jambu River. Trillions of brahmā gods tossed, scattered, and sprinkled divine red sandalwood powder upon the Blessed One. Trillions of gods tossed, scattered, and sprinkled divine blue, pink, red, and white lotuses, as well as smaller and larger māndārava flowers. Trillions of gods offered thousands of jeweled parasols. Trillions of gods wept as they bowed their heads to the Blessed One’s feet, recalling trillions of their past lives. It was the blessings of the Blessed Buddha that allowed these trillions of miracles to occur.

About this, it was said,

Billions of gods<br>Stationed in the periphery<br>Tossed fine red sandalwood powder<br>Upon the perfect Buddha. ^F157b

Tens of billions of śakra gods<br>Stationed in the sky<br>Tossed much powdered gold from the Jambu River<br>Upon the Thus-Gone One.

Ten million brahmā gods arrived<br>And tossed powdered red sandalwood<br>Upon the perfect Buddha<br>And sang melodious songs.

Seeing the immaculate light,<br>The gods arrayed themselves in the sky.<br>They sang countless songs<br>And waved fabrics in the sky.

The gods arrayed in the sky,<br>Holding thousands of lotus flowers,<br>Displayed them to the World Illuminator<br>And venerated him with banners and so forth.

With minds filled with joy,<br>They honored the unparalleled supreme Victor, the Lord of Sages.<br>They praised him and scattered upon him<br>Garlands of precious jewels and pearls.

A trillion people<br>Venerated the unparalleled supreme Victor.<br>They remembered hundreds of their past lives<br>And bowed their heads to the unparalleled Lord of Sages.

Then, the World Illuminator<br>Beat the Dharma drum;<br>Its sound was heard throughout many billions<br>Of buddha realms.

At that moment,<br>Countless millions of beings arrived from all directions.<br>They were established in the wisdom of buddhahood<br>By the perfect Buddha.

The young man was delighted;<br>He offered a seat of uraga sandalwood<br>And venerated the supreme Buddha<br>With powdered gems.

The light of one precious gem<br>Shone throughout thousands of worlds.<br>The light of that single gem<br>Radiated throughout billions of worlds.

In the same way, light sprang forth<br>From every gem without exception.<br>At that moment, they also<br>Illuminated Vaiśālī.

All of these gems were used<br>To venerate the principle Buddha.<br>A great pile of gems<br>Also was offered to the Great Sage.

Parasols adorned<br>With pearl garlands surrounded him.<br>Each had twenty-five thousand<br>Surrounding layers.

They venerated the Great Sage<br>Through such excellent means.<br>Woven fabrics were offered<br>To all the bodhisattvas. ^F158a

Lion thrones were placed<br>Before emanated trees of awakening.<br>All told, two hundred fifty million<br>Were set out.

For millions of years,<br>Countless bodhisattvas—<br>Hundreds of millions in fact—<br>Arrived there.

They arrived first from the east,<br>And then from the south, west, and north,<br>From above and below,<br>And from the intermediate directions.

The golden-colored light<br>Of the Refuge, the World Protector,<br>Filled as many realms<br>As the eye could see.

Bodhisattvas arrived<br>From all worlds<br>To the place where the Teacher was sitting<br>And the Dharma was shining.

In this fashion, Ratnajālin,<br>The young Licchavi,<br>Performed boundless,<br>Limitless, and inconceivable homage.

Then the great person,<br>The supreme human, the Lion of the Śākyas,<br>Brought comfort to the assembled<br>Gods, nāgas, and humans:

“Standing before me,<br>You have performed such veneration.<br>This veneration has been done with offerings<br>As numerous as the grains of sand in the Ganges.

“Those who hear this discourse,<br>Which has been taught for the sake of Ratnajālin,<br>Shall not turn back from awakening;<br>They will become supreme preachers of buddhahood.

“One cannot describe<br>The measure of the mass of merit<br>That ensues from remembering this discourse<br>By saying, ‘It shall be this much.’

“While one can describe the extent<br>Of the earth, water, fire, and wind<br>Throughout the ten directions,<br>It is impossible to describe this mass of merit.

“When a monk or nun,<br>Or a novice monk or nun,<br>Hears this discourse,<br>All beings including gods, nāgas, and mahoragas

“Will arrive near them<br>And find their splendor overwhelming.<br>While it is possible that fire can turn cold,<br>Their splendor will never diminish. ^F158b

“When someone who remembers this discourse<br>Engages in the conduct of awakening,<br>All beings will attend to them<br>And venerate the mind of awakening.

“A powerful person<br>Might hold up a thousand worlds<br>For millions of eons<br>On the tip of a hair that has been split a billion times.

“Still, compared to that, during the latter days<br>One may teach to others<br>A discourse of the buddhas’ awakening<br>That one has heard in this fashion.

“In that case the great wonder<br>Of the former example<br>Shall not measure even a hundredth of that—<br>As no number, figure, or example could suffice.

“In the latter days,<br>When people hear the unsurpassed teachings<br>Of the lion’s roar in this manner,<br>It will not be contradicted or opposed.

“In the future, when people hear<br>Of those who have seen the World Protector—<br>The shining perfect Buddha—<br>They will act with respect and reverence.

“If all beings<br>In millions of buddha realms<br>Were endowed with the wisdom of buddhahood—<br>As many of them as there are grains of sand in the Ganges—

“One might perform service to them<br>For many hundreds of eons,<br>And offer them as many fine temples<br>As there are worlds in the great trichiliocosm.

“The temples could be made of sandalwood;<br>And each supreme and divine temple<br>Would have ten billion assembly halls. [n13]

“Each of the billion assembly halls<br>Could be made from divine jewels as an offering.<br>One could arrange thrones for everyone,<br>Complete with a billion servants.

“Skillfully, one could take nets of jewels<br>And adorn the thrones.<br>For eons as numerous as the grains of sand in the Ganges,<br>The great sages who wander through the world could then abide there.

“One could offer all that is fit to be offered<br>To each of those buddhas.<br>One could also create caityas for their parinirvāṇas<br>As numerous as the grains of sand in the Ganges.

“One could create a caitya to every single buddha<br>In all the worlds of the great trichiliocosm—<br>Composed of jewels, with thousands of divine doors,<br>Reaching as high as the peak of existence. ^F159a

“One could offer parasols as numerous as the grains of sand in the Ganges<br>To each of these caityas.<br>The parasols offered at that time would be<br>Covered with ornamental garlands of pearl.

“One could also offer millions of streamers<br>As numerous as the grains of sand in the Ganges<br>And billions of banners made of various precious jewels<br>To these caityas as numerous as the grains of sand in the Ganges.

“One could play billions of instruments<br>At each caitya of the omniscient illuminators.<br>Trillions of respectful humans too<br>Would join in making offerings.

“They would offer hundreds of flowers and parasols to the caitya<br>And venerate the great sages in this manner every day<br>For as many eons as there are grains of sand in the Ganges. [n14]

“Compared to all this, hearing just one verse from this discourse<br>While not doubting it, developing interest,<br>And reciting these victors’ names<br>Brings merit that is unsurpassed and supreme.

“Wise ones should listen to the names of absorptions,<br>Maintain flawless discipline, remember these names,<br>And venerate the thus-gone buddhas.<br>Thereby, they will behold as many buddhas as the grains of sand in the Ganges.

“Having directed their minds to the sacred veneration<br>Of the supreme humans, the great sages,<br>They will always be sane, they will teach the Dharma,<br>And for millions of eons they will remain undeluded.

“As the World Protector teaches<br>This supreme king of discourses,<br>Those who hear what the Supreme Victor has previously seen<br>Will obtain happiness in the future.

“They will discard desires, knowing them to have faults,<br>Like a person tossing away a vessel filled with foul-smelling excrement.<br>Such wise beings will delight in emancipation and peace,<br>Be constantly sane, and rely on the protectors.

“Those who saw me teach this<br>In the city of Vaiśālī,<br>Including Ratnajālin, who beheld and venerated me,<br>Shall remember this supreme discourse in the future.

“Those of my children who always follow me,<br>Engage in the supreme acts of awakening and peace,<br>And constantly train beings<br>Shall uphold this supreme discourse in the future. ^F159b

“Those who serve the buddhas, the guides,<br>As they engage in the fathomless and incalculable practices,<br>And likewise engage in perfect worship,<br>Shall uphold this teaching of the Supreme Victor in the future.

“One could have engaged in pure conduct for a million eons<br>Or previously worked for the benefit of infinite worlds;<br>Yet the merit of hearing this discourse in the future<br>Shall not be countable at all.

“Let those intelligent beings who desire to behold<br>These peaceful and immortal buddhas—<br>The unfathomable and limitless buddhas—<br>Uphold the unfathomable greatness of this discourse!

“Let those who wish to behold all these buddhas,<br>Including Amitāyus, Akṣobhya, Vimalaprabhā,<br>and Siṃhaketu,<br>Uphold this teaching of the Supreme Victor in the future!

“Let those who wish to behold these principle illuminators,<br>The buddhas Maitreya and Vimalaprabhā,<br>Mahātman, and Siṃhaketu,<br>Uphold this teaching of the Supreme Victor in the future!

“I have taught this noble and supreme discourse<br>to Ratnajālin.<br>In the future,<br>Do not stay in heedless places!

“When the future times come,<br>Be careful and always keep your discipline flawless.<br>Then, when your time comes to die,<br>You will go to the realm of the victor Infinite Light.

“His realm is known as Jeweled,<br>And is located in a world above us.<br>Following that, when your time comes to die,<br>You will swiftly behold the Buddha Maitreya.”

Once the Blessed One gave this teaching, the young Licchavi Ratnajālin as well as four hundred million bodhisattvas such as Maitreya, and also six hundred million worthy ones and nine hundred ninety million gods rejoiced and praised what the Blessed One had said.
