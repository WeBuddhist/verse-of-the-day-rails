> 佛世尊皆出人間，非由天而得也。
> *(verbatim contiguous substring from `zh-ekottarika-agama.md#^pT02p0694a0201`. Full context: the Buddha tells the monks that the gods of the Thirty-Three regard the human world as the good destination, since it is here that one goes forth and reaches the higher knowledges — "佛世尊皆出人間，非由天而得也". ## Disambiguated Meaning
Grounded in the Chinese source (^pT02p0694a0201): 佛世尊 — "the Buddhas, the World-Honored Ones"; 皆出人間 — "all arise in / come forth from the human world"; 非由天而得也 — "and are not attained from (a birth among) the gods." Awakening is realized in a human life, not in the heavens; the human state is where the path is walked and a Buddha appears. An encouraging teaching: this very human life is the precious ground of practice.

- Rendering choice: 佛世尊 → "Buddhas" (the World-Honored Ones); keep plain "Buddhas." 人間 → "the human world." 天 → "the heavens / a divine realm." Frame it inclusively and hopefully (the human birth as opportunity), not as a put-down of other realms.
