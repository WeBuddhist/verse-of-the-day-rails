## Disambiguated Meaning
Grounded in the Chinese source (^pT01p0107a1008): one who **practices loving-kindness** (行慈, mettā), when **the body breaks up at death** (身壞命終), is reborn **in the time it takes to shoot an arrow** (如發箭之頃 — i.e. swiftly, without delay) **in the Brahmā heaven** (生梵天上, a radiant/divine realm). A warm, encouraging promise: a heart cultivated in loving-kindness carries one swiftly to a happy state. Buddha-spoken to the brahmin students; points the way.

- Rendering choice: 比丘 ("monk") → "one who lives with loving-kindness" for a lay-inclusive audience (flagged; a reviewer may restore "a monk"). 梵天上 ("the Brahmā heaven") → "a heavenly realm" for accessibility (flagged; the specific term is the Brahmaloka). 行慈 → mettā = "loving-kindness," the standard term, never "love." The arrow simile denotes *speed*, not violence — kept as the image of swiftness.
