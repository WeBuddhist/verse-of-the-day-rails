## Disambiguated Meaning
Grounded in the Chinese source (^pT02p0569c0102): 其有瞻視病者 — "whoever looks after / tends the sick"; 則為瞻視我已 — "has thereby tended me (the Buddha)." Care given to a sick person is care given to the Buddha himself. Spoken after the Buddha found a gravely ill monk whom no one was helping, and washed and cared for him personally. A warm, dignifying teaching: humble acts of care are of the highest worth.

- Rendering choice: 瞻視 → "tend / look after / care for" (nursing a sick person). 我 → "me," the Buddha speaking; keep first person. Render for a lay-inclusive audience without narrowing to monastics.
