> ཀྱེ་མ་འདུ་བྱེད་རྣམས་མི་རྟག །སྐྱེ་ཞིང་འཇིག་པའི་ཆོས་ཅན་ཡིན། །སྐྱེས་ནས་འཇིག་པར་འགྱུར་བ་སྟེ། །དེ་དག་ཉེ་བར་ཞི་བ་བདེ།

## Grounding — Pali parallel
The Udānavarga's most famous verse; exact parallel to the Pali **aniccā vata saṅkhārā, uppādavayadhammino; uppajjitvā nirujjhanti, tesaṁ vūpasamo sukho** (SN 15.20 / DN 16), Sujato (CC0): *"Oh! Conditions are impermanent, their nature is to rise and fall; having arisen, they cease; their stilling is bliss."* Meaning is anchored on this documented parallel; our renderings are made from the Tibetan source, cross-checked against it.

## Disambiguated Meaning
Conditioned things (*saṅkhārā* / འདུ་བྱེད) are impermanent, of the nature to arise and pass; having arisen they cease; the stilling (*vūpasama* / ཞི་བ) of them is bliss. Buddha-spoken (an *udāna* of the Victor, per the Udānavarga framing). Theme = impermanence, ending on peace (not bleakness).
