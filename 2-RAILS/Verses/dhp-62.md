## Disambiguated Meaning
Grounded in the source verse and Sujato (1-SOURCES/Translations/en-dhammapada-sujato.md#^62): thinking "I have sons, I have wealth" (*puttā m'atthi dhanaṁ m'atthi*), the unwise person is worn out with worry (*bālo vihaññati*) over holding them as "mine." But even one's own self is not one's own possession (*attā hi attano natthi*) — so how could sons or wealth be? (*kuto puttā kuto dhanaṁ*). The verse cuts possessive grasping at the root: if not even the self is truly owned, nothing external can be.

- *bāla* = "fool / immature / one who lacks understanding." A modern rendering may soften to "the unwise" or "one who does not understand" rather than the harsher "fool" — flag for each termbase.
- *vihaññati* = "is tormented / frets / is worn out" — keep the sense of anxious striving, not mere folly.
- *attā hi attano natthi* — the not-self / non-ownership pivot; render so the "not even the self is truly mine" force lands.
