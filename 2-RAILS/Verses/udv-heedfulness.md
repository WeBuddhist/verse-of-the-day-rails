## Grounding — Pali parallel
Exact parallel to the Pali **Dhp 21**: *appamādo amatapadaṁ, pamādo maccuno padaṁ; appamattā na mīyanti, ye pamattā yathā matā*, Sujato (CC0): *"Heedfulness is the state free of death; heedlessness is the state of death. The heedful do not die, while the heedless are like the dead."* Meaning is anchored on this documented parallel; the shipped bo is the verbatim Degé source, and the other renderings are made from the Tibetan, cross-checked against the Pali.

## Disambiguated Meaning
Grounded in the Tibetan source (^p426) and the Pali parallel (Dhp 21): *bag yod* (**appamāda** — diligence/heedfulness) is the abode/ground of the deathless (*'chi med* = *amata*); *bag med* (heedlessness) is the abode of death. The heedful do not die; the heedless are perpetually "dying" (as if already dead). The verse contrasts an alert, careful life with a careless one, and calls the reader toward diligence. Buddha-spoken (an *udāna* of the Victor, per the Udānavarga framing).

- Rendering choice: *bag yod* → **heedfulness / diligence** (termbase: *appamāda* = diligence, heedfulness); keep the standard term, not "carefulness." *'chi med* → "the deathless" (*amata*), the standard image, glossable as "what does not die." "The deathless" is figurative for the unconditioned/liberation, not literal immortality — a reviewer may keep it as "the deathless."
