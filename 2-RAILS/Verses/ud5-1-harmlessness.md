## Disambiguated Meaning
Grounded in source + Sujato (^ud5-1-6-1..4): *sabbā disā anuparigamma cetasā* — "having ranged over every direction with the mind"; *nevajjhagā piyataramattanā kvaci* — "one finds nothing anywhere dearer than oneself"; *evaṁ piyo puthu attā paresaṁ* — "just so, each of the many others holds their own self dear"; *tasmā na hiṁse* — "therefore let one not harm" another. The verse grounds non-harming (*ahiṁsā*) in a universal fact everyone can verify: since I hold myself most dear, and everyone else does too, I should harm no one. Spoken by the Buddha after King Pasenadi and Queen Mallikā each admit they love themselves most; the verse needs none of that frame to land.

- Rendering choice: the final word *paramattakāmo* is read two ways — most translators take it as **"one who loves / holds themselves dear" (*attakāma*)**, which fits the verse's logic; Sujato reads it "one who desires self-knowledge / the highest good." Flag for the reviewer; the shipped rendering follows the mainstream "loving oneself → harm no other," and either reading yields the same instruction (harm no one). *na hiṁse* → "should not harm" (*ahiṁsā*, harmlessness — the standard term).
