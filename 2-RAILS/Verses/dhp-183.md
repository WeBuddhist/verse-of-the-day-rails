## Disambiguated Meaning

Grounded in the source verse and Sujato (1-SOURCES/Translations/en-dhammapada-sujato.md#^183):

The refraining from all wrongdoing (*sabbapāpassa akaraṇaṁ*); the undertaking / cultivation of the wholesome (*kusalassa upasampadā*); the purifying of one's own mind (*sacittapariyodapanaṁ*) — **this** is the teaching of the Buddhas (*etaṁ buddhāna sāsanaṁ*), i.e. common to all of them.

- This is the famous summary of the path (the Ovāda-pāṭimokkha verse). Three clauses + the refrain; keep the three-part parallelism in every rendering.
- *sāsana* = "teaching / instruction / dispensation." Sujato: "instruction of the Buddhas." Flag word choice (teaching / instruction / message) for each termbase.

## Theme & Selection Notes

Theme: **the path / the heart of the teaching.** One of the most quoted verses; a complete ethical program in one stanza. Self-contained; ideal verse of the day. (Part of a 183–185 set in the source; stands alone fine.)
