## Disambiguated Meaning
Grounded in the Tibetan source (^p31) and the 84000 reference: **objects (of desire) are like a dream** (*yul ni rmi lam dang 'dra ba*) — they arise only to vanish (*byung nas med par 'gyur ba*); so **what wise person would generate craving (*'dod chags*) for objects that are dreamlike?** The verse is a remedy for desire: seeing the fleeting, insubstantial nature of sense-objects loosens the grip of craving for them. Buddha-spoken (the Blessed One's own words, quoted in the sūtra) — a teaching that points the way, not praise of a buddha.

- Rendering choice: *yul* → "objects" (objects of the senses/desire), following 84000; a reviewer may prefer "sense objects." *'dod chags* → "craving/desire" (rāga), the standard term, not a softer "liking."
- The rhetorical question ("what wise person would…?") is kept as a question, not flattened to a statement — it carries the verse's gentle challenge.
