## Disambiguated Meaning
Oneself is one's own refuge/protector (*mgon*); who else could be one's refuge? For the wise one who has become their own refuge, their aims are attained (`bo-udanavarga.md#^p460`; parallel Dhp 160, `1-SOURCES/Translations/en-dhammapada-sujato.md#^160`). The point is self-responsibility on the path: no one else can walk it for you — but a self that is disciplined and well-tamed becomes a refuge that others cannot give. This is not self-sufficiency-as-isolation; it is the Buddha's insistence that liberation is won by one's own effort.

**Flagged rendering choices.** *nātha* / *mgon* is rendered "lord" by Sujato, but "refuge" or "protector" reads warmer and is standard (cf. *saraṇa*); the card uses "refuge." The Tibetan variant's fourth line is "the wise attain their aims" (*don rnams thob*), where Pali Dhp 160 has "gains a refuge hard to gain" (*nāthaṁ labhati dullabhaṁ*) — the card follows the Tibetan source it ships (aims), noting the Pali variant here.
