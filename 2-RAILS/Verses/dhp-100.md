## Disambiguated Meaning
Grounded in the source verse and Sujato (1-SOURCES/Translations/en-dhammapada-sujato.md#^100): a thousand words (*sahassaṁ vācā*) strung from meaningless lines (*anatthapadasaṁhitā*) are worth less than a single meaningful word (*ekaṁ atthapadaṁ*) which, on being heard, brings peace / stilling (*yaṁ sutvā upasammati*). Quality of speech over quantity: one word that quiets the heart outweighs a thousand empty ones.

- *atthapada* = a "word/line connected with the goal (attha)" — a meaningful, beneficial saying. A modern rendering may use "meaningful word," "word of substance," or "word worth hearing" — flag for each termbase.
- *upasammati* = "is stilled / comes to peace"; keep the sense that hearing it calms, not merely informs.
