## Source Text (CBETA — CC BY-NC-SA)
> 當淨其心；隨其心淨，則佛土淨。
> *(verbatim from `zh-vimalakirti.md#^pT14p0538b2609`, 佛國品 / Buddha-Field chapter)*

## Grounding
Own modern rendering from the Chinese source; meaning cross-checked against standard translations of the Vimalakīrtinirdeśa and the Tibetan parallel (Toh 176). One self-contained sentence, quoted in full. **Mahāyāna.**

## Disambiguated Meaning
The famous Vimalakīrti teaching that the outer world reflects the inner: **purify the mind, and as the mind becomes pure the buddha-field (one's world) becomes pure** (心淨則佛土淨). Not a claim that externals magically change, but that a purified mind meets the world purely.
