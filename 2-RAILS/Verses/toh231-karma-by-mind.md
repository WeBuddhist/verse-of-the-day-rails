## Disambiguated Meaning
Grounded in the Tibetan source (^p165) and the 84000 reference (^F82a): *dge ba'am yang na mi dge ba'i las* — "action, whether virtuous (**kusala**) or unvirtuous (**akusala**)"; *sems kyis bsags pa'o* — "is accumulated / gathered up by the mind (**citta**)." The mind is where our wholesome and unwholesome deeds are stored and take shape — which is why the passage teaches mindfulness of mind. Empowering rather than fatalistic: since action is gathered by the mind, watching the mind is the lever. Buddha-spoken (a teaching that points the way, not praise of a buddha).

- Rendering choice: *las* → **action / deeds** (*kamma*), the standard term. *dge ba / mi dge ba* → **wholesome / unwholesome** (*kusala/akusala*), termbase standard, not "good/bad." *bsags pa* → "accumulated / gathered up" (stored over time), following 84000's "accumulates."
