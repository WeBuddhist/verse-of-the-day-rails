## Disambiguated Meaning
Grounded in the Chinese source (^pT01p0100c0910): the Buddha, speaking to the brahmin Kūṭadanta (who had prepared a great animal sacrifice), describes an ascending ladder of what is truly meritorious; near its summit he says that **giving rise to a joyful heart** (起歡喜心) and **saying aloud** (口自發言) "**I go for refuge to the Buddha, the Dharma, the Sangha**" (我歸依佛，歸依法，歸依僧) is a merit that is **supreme** (此福最勝). The point that lands on its own: going for refuge, done with gladness, is of the highest worth. Buddha-spoken, a teaching that points the way (not praise of the Buddha, not a self-declaration).

- Rendering choice: 歸依 → "go for refuge / take refuge" (the standard formula), not "rely on." 此福最勝 → "this merit is supreme"; a reviewer may prefer "this is the highest merit."
- Quote boundary: begins at 起歡喜心 (dropping the comparative 不如 that ties back to the merit-ladder). This is a contiguous quote, and the standalone superlative 此福最勝 keeps it faithful and self-contained.
