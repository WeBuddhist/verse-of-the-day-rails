## Disambiguated Meaning

Grounded in the source verse and Sujato (1-SOURCES/Translations/en-dhammapada-sujato.md#^328):

If you find a discerning, prudent companion (*nipaka sahāya*) — an attentive
friend of good conduct and steadiness (*sādhuvihāri-dhīra*) to live alongside —
then, having overcome all dangers and hardships (*sabbāni parissayāni*), travel /
live on together with them, glad at heart (*attamana*) and mindful (*satīmā*).

- The verse celebrates **good companionship on the path** (*kalyāṇa-mittatā*):
  the right friend is one who is wise *and* well-conducted, and walking with them
  makes one able to overcome difficulties — joyfully and mindfully.
- It is the positive member of the 328–330 triad (328: travel *with* a good
  companion; 329–330: if none is found, better to go alone than with fools).
  Self-contained on its own.
- *nipaka* ("alert / prudent / discerning") and *sahāya* ("companion") — render
  warmly (friend / companion / kindred spirit); flag for each stream's termbase.

## Theme & Selection Notes

Theme: **friendship** (Connect pillar). Warm, uplifting, self-contained, deeply
relatable — about finding someone to share the journey. Strong verse of the day
and a natural fit for **Sangha Day (Māgha Pūjā)**. Echoes the Buddha's
*"admirable friendship is the whole of the holy life"* (SN 45.2).
