## Disambiguated Meaning
Grounded in source + Sujato (^ud3-4-3-1..4): *yathāpi pabbato selo, acalo suppatiṭṭhito* — "just as a rocky mountain is unmoving, well founded"; *evaṁ mohakkhayā bhikkhu, pabbatova na vedhatī* — "so, with the ending of delusion (**moha**), one does not tremble, like a mountain." Spoken by the Buddha watching Sāriputta seated in deep concentration. The image: a mind cleared of confusion is steady and unshaken by whatever blows against it, like a mountain in the wind.

- Rendering choice: *mohakkhayā* → "when delusion/confusion ends"; keep "delusion" (moha), glossable as "confusion." *bhikkhu* → rendered inclusively (e.g. "one who is free of delusion / the steady one") for a lay audience, rather than narrowing to "a monk" (flag; a reviewer may restore "a monk"). The mountain simile is the heart of the verse and carries with no glossary.
