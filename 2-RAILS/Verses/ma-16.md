## Disambiguated Meaning
MĀ 16 is the Saṁyukta/Madhyama parallel to the Kālāma teaching — but **note the Chinese version differs from the famous Pali AN 3.65 framing**: it does *not* give the "do not go by tradition/report" list. Instead the Buddha tells the Kālāmas to set doubt aside, abandon greed, hatred, and delusion, and dwell with a heart free of enmity and ill-will, pervading all directions with goodwill. Such a disciple attains the **four "peaceful dwelling places" (the four solaces)**: whether or not there is another world and the fruits of action, *here and now I live free of enmity, at peace, with nothing to fear.* The card distils this present-life peace of a blameless, friendly heart. (Do not import the Pali "free inquiry" framing — it is not in this source.)

## Theme & Selection Notes
Theme: **peace-of-a-good-heart** — fresh and distinct from the loving-kindness card (Day 10): the angle here is the *present-moment ease and fearlessness* of a heart without ill-will, not mettā practice itself.
