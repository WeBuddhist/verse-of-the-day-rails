## Disambiguated Meaning
Grounded in source + Sujato (^197): *susukhaṁ vata jīvāma* — "let us live in true happiness indeed"; *verinesu averino* — "free from hatred among those who hate"; *verinesu manussesu, viharāma averino* — "among people filled with hate, let us dwell free of hatred." The point is the happiness of a heart that stays free of hatred even when surrounded by hostility. Buddha-spoken; the first of the "living happily" verses (Dhp 197-200).

- Rendering choice: *averino* is **non-hatred / free of hatred** (from *avera*), the standard term (termbase: *vera/avera* = hatred/non-hatred). Sujato renders it warmly as "loving / with love," but the verse's literal claim is the *absence of hatred*, not the presence of love — keep "free from hatred" so the term is precise (a reviewer may prefer a warmer wording). *susukhaṁ* → "in true happiness / very happily" (intensive).
