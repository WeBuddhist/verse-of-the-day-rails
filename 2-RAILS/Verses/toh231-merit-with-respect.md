## Disambiguated Meaning
Grounded in the Tibetan source (^p32) and the 84000 reference: the Blessed One teaches that the three should each be done **with respect / reverence** (*gus par*): **merit** (*bsod nams*, puṇya) is to be **pursued** with respect, **discipline** (*tshul khrims*, śīla) **guarded** with respect, and **wisdom** (*shes rab*, prajñā) **cultivated** with respect. The teaching is about the *attitude* of practice: not perfunctory but wholehearted and reverent. Buddha-spoken (the Blessed One's own words, quoted in the sūtra) — a teaching that points the way.

- Rendering choice: the vocative *dge slong rnams* ("monks") is kept in the verbatim bo but **not** carried into the other renderings, so the instruction reads as a universal call to all practitioners (same ecumenical-address principle applied on the Toh 26 card, *rigs kyi bu* → "friend"). Flagged for reviewer preference. *gus par* → "with respect / reverence."
