## Disambiguated Meaning
SĀ 33 ("Not Self") is the Saṁyuktāgama parallel to the Anattalakkhaṇa teaching. The card quotes one contiguous sentence-chain from the sutta's closing block (`zh-samyukta-agama.md#^pT02p0007c0704`, within `^sa33-0`), grounded against Patton: "多聞聖弟子於此五受陰非我、非我所，如實觀察。如實觀察已，於諸世間都無所取，無所取故無所著，無所著故自覺涅槃" — the well-learned noble disciple truly sees these five grasped aggregates as **not self, not belonging to self**; seeing this, **they grasp nothing in the world; grasping nothing, they cling to nothing; clinging to nothing, they awaken to nirvana for themselves.**

- This is a single contiguous run of the source text (one clause leading causally into the next via 已…故…故), not a stitch of separate sentences — it stops before the following quoted arahant-declaration formula ("我生已盡…", left out, a legitimate stopping point).
- Aggregates named in full elsewhere in the block (色/受/想/行/識 — form/feeling/perception/volition/consciousness); the card's single sentence refers to them collectively as "the five aggregates" per termbase standard naming.
