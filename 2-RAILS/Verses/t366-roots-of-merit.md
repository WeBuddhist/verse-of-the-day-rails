## Disambiguated Meaning
The Buddha tells Śāriputra that one cannot be reborn in that pure land (彼國, Sukhāvatī) through only *few* roots of goodness (善根, *kuśala-mūla*), merit (福德, *puṇya*), and the conditions they set up (因緣) (`zh-amitabha-sutra.md#^pT12p0347b0903`). The teaching cuts against a shortcut mindset: genuine attainment rests on real, sustained cultivation of wholesome roots and merit, not a token gesture. In the sūtra's own logic this sets up the practice that follows — wholehearted, undistracted mindfulness of the Buddha (一心不亂).

**Flagged rendering choices.** 彼國 ("that land") is Sukhāvatī; render it "that land" / "that pure land," framed as a universal aspiration rather than a school-specific claim (ecumenical). 善根 = "roots of goodness / wholesome roots" (*kuśala-mūla*), not "good deeds"; 福德 = "merit" (*puṇya*) per termbase — keep the standard terms, not paraphrase. 因緣 (causes and conditions) may be folded into "and merit" for a short card, or spelled out.
