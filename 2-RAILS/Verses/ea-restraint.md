## Source Text (CBETA — Ekottarika Āgama T125, CC BY-NC-SA)
> 凡夫之人，若眼見色，便起染著之心，不能捨離；彼以見色，極起愛著，流轉生死，無有解時。

## Disambiguated Meaning
Grounded in the Chinese source (^pT02p0728b0202): **when an ordinary person (*pṛthagjana*, 凡夫) sees a form with the eye, a mind of defiled attachment arises** (便起染著之心) that **cannot let go** (不能捨離); grasping at what is seen, **craving flares up** (極起愛著), and so they **flow on through birth and death with no time of release** (流轉生死，無有解時). The eye is the exemplar for all six senses (the source generalizes next with "六情亦復如是"). The teaching shows the mechanism by which unguarded senses bind — the ground for the practice of sense-restraint (*indriyasaṁvara*). Buddha-spoken to the monks; a teaching, not praise of the Buddha.

- Rendering choice: 染著 → "defiled attachment / clinging"; 愛著 → "craving" (taṇhā-flavoured), the standard term, not a bland "liking." 流轉生死 → "flow/drift on through birth and death" (saṁsāra), kept as the vivid image the sentence turns on.
- Tone note: this is the *problem-side* diagnosis (how craving binds), not an uplifting turn; framed soberly and honestly. Space it per `selection-criteria.md` §3 (tone).
