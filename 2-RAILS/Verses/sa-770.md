## Disambiguated Meaning

Grounded in the Chinese source and Patton (1-SOURCES/Translations/en-samyukta-agama-patton.md#^sa770):

The Buddha teaches that **wrong view (邪見) can be given up and right view (正見) can be cultivated** — and *because* they can, the path is worth undertaking. Abandoning wrong view and cultivating right view (and likewise the other seven factors: intention, speech, action, livelihood, effort, mindfulness, concentration) leads to what is purposeful, beneficial, and to lasting well-being.

- The uplifting core for a daily card: *change is possible* — wrong view isn't fixed; right view can be grown, and well-being follows.
- 正見/邪見 = right view / wrong view; the eight factors are the Noble Eightfold Path — render per the termbase.
