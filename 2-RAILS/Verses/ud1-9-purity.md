## Disambiguated Meaning
Grounded in source + Sujato (^ud1-9-4-1..4): *na udakena sucī hoti* — "one does not become pure (**suci**) by water"; *bahvettha nhāyatī jano* — "though many people bathe here"; *yamhi saccañca dhammo ca* — "the one in whom there is truth (**sacca**) and the Dhamma / principle"; *so sucī so ca brāhmaṇo* — "that one is pure, and that one is a brahmin." Spoken to matted-hair ascetics who bathed ritually for purification: real purity is not washed on from outside but grows from inner truth and right conduct. Buddha-spoken; self-contained and needs no backstory.

- Rendering choice: *suci* → "pure / purity" (inner purity, not physical cleanness). *sacca* → **truth** (termbase standard). *dhamma* here → "the Dhamma / principle / what is right" (Sujato: "principle"); flag for the reviewer whether to say "the Dhamma" or "virtue/principle." *brāhmaṇo* → "a (true) brahmin" in the Buddha's redefined sense — the inwardly pure person, not a birth-caste; frame it as "a truly noble one" if the caste word reads oddly.
