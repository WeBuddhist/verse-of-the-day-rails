## Disambiguated Meaning
Grounded in the Tibetan source (^p508) and the 84000 reference: *ngar 'dzin pa dang nga yir 'dzin pa yod pa las* — "from the presence of grasping at 'I' (I-making) and 'mine' (mine-making)"; *gnod sems dang, tha ba dang, zhe sdang dang, 'chab pa 'byung bar 'gyur ro* — "arise ill will, hostility, hatred, and concealment/resentment." The Buddha names self-grasping as the deep root of our hostile states: where there is clinging to a self and what is "mine," ill will follows. Practical and hopeful in that it points to a cause we can loosen.

- Rendering choice: *ngar 'dzin / nga yir 'dzin* → "grasping at 'I' and 'mine'" (ahaṅkāra / mamaṅkāra), the self-grasping the not-self teaching addresses. The four results: *gnod sems* = ill will/malice, *tha ba* = hostility/roughness, *zhe sdang* = hatred/anger, *'chab pa* = concealment (hiding faults) or resentment — flag for the reviewer which of "concealment / resentment" to keep.
