## Disambiguated Meaning
Asked by a deity how he crossed the flood, the Buddha answers: *appatiṭṭhaṁ anāyūhaṁ oghamatariṁ* — "I crossed the flood neither halting nor straining" (`1-SOURCES/Translations/en-samyutta-nikaya-sujato.md#^sn1-1-1-5`). He then unpacks it: "When I stood still, I sank under; when I strove, I was swept away" (`#^sn1-1-1-7`, `#^sn1-1-1-8`) — so it was by neither that he crossed (`#^sn1-1-1-9`). The **flood** (*ogha*) is the current of craving and existence that carries beings along. *Santiṭṭhāmi* (standing still / halting) images stagnation and indulgence — sinking; *āyūhāmi* (exerting / straining) images grasping and forcing — being swept away. Liberation is the **middle way** between the two: neither pushing nor stalling.

**Flagged rendering choices.** Sujato keeps the water image — *appatiṭṭha* → "standing," *anāyūha* → "swimming" (`#^sn1-1-1-5`); the more literal sense of *appatiṭṭha* is "not halting / taking no stand" and of *anāyūha* "not straining / not exerting." The card renders "standing still" and "straining" to carry the middle-way meaning plainly while staying anchored on Sujato. The closing four-line stanza (`^sn1-1-2-1` → `^sn1-1-2-4`, "After a long time I see a brahmin fully quenched…") is spoken **by the deity, not the Buddha** — so it is outside the buddhavacana gate and is **not** shipped; only the Buddha's own prose answer is used.
