## Disambiguated Meaning
Grounded in the Tibetan source (^p354) and the 84000 reference: the Blessed One tells the bodhisattva Sūryaprabhāsa (addressed as *rigs kyi bu*, "son of a good family" — rendered ecumenically/inclusively as "friend") to train in the understanding that **all phenomena are, by their very nature, already pure** (*rang bzhin gyis rnam par dag pa*). This is the sūtra's emptiness/Prajñāpāramitā teaching in its most accessible form: not that things become pure through effort, but that their nature already is pure — practice is *recognizing* this, not manufacturing it. Buddha-spoken (direct address, "Then the Blessed One said to the bodhisattva Sūryaprabhāsa…"), not homage to him.

- Rendering choice: *rigs kyi bu* ("son of a good family") → "friend" for ecumenical, non-gendered modern address, following the same inclusive-language principle the vault already applies to *bodhicitta* → "the awakening mind." Flagged for reviewer preference (a more literal "child of noble family" is also defensible).
- This single sentence is deliberately isolated from its neighbors (which cover "without conditioning," "empty," "signless," etc.) to keep the card to one clean, self-contained claim rather than stacking multiple technical points.
