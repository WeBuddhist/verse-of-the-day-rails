## Disambiguated Meaning
Grounded in the Tibetan source (^p559) and the 84000 reference: all phenomena arise dependent on **conditions** (*rkyen*), and among those conditions they "rest firmly on the root of **intention**" (*'dun pa'i rtsa la rab tu gnas*) — i.e. the direction of one's motivation/aspiration is decisive. Therefore, **whatever aspiration (*smon lam*, praṇidhāna) one makes, a corresponding fruit (*'bras bu*) is obtained.** The teaching is that outcomes track the intention that shapes them: set the aspiration, and a like result follows. Spoken *by* a Thus-Gone One (a buddha) to a great king within Śākyamuni's discourse — buddhavacana, not a stanza in praise of a buddha.

- Rendering choice: *'dun pa* → "intention" (its base sense of chanda/āśaya, motivation/interest), following 84000's "the quality of one's intention." A reviewer may prefer "aspiration/motivation"; keep it distinct from *smon lam* (the formal "aspiration/prayer" in line 3), which we render "aspiration."
- Quote boundary: the **next** verse in the same passage ("I have now attained results / According to the aspirations I formed…") is the past-buddha's *self-report of his own attainment* — deliberately **not** used, per `selection-criteria.md` §2 (a bare proclamation of attainment is a poor daily card). This verse, by contrast, teaches the principle and points the way.
