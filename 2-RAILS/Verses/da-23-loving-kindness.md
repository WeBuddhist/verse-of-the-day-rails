## Disambiguated Meaning
Grounded in the Chinese source (^pT01p0100c1716): 若能以慈心念一切眾生 — "if one can hold all beings in mind with a heart of loving-kindness (慈心, mettā)"; 如搆牛乳頃 — "even for as long as it takes to milk a cow" (a short while); 其福最勝 — "that merit (福, puñña) is supreme." Even a brief moment of genuine loving-kindness for all beings is the most valuable act of all. Warm and encouraging: small, short acts of kindness are of the highest worth. Buddha-spoken, to the brahmin Kūṭadanta.

- Rendering choice: 慈心 → "a heart of loving-kindness" (mettā = loving-kindness, the standard term, never "love"). 福 → "merit" (puñña), not "luck/blessing." 如搆牛乳頃 → the cow-milking image denotes *a brief span of time*; keep the concrete image (universal and vivid), gloss its sense as "even for a moment."
