## Disambiguated Meaning
"The Single Stanza" (*Ekagāthā* / ཚིགས་སུ་བཅད་པ་གཅིག་པ) — a whole one-stanza sūtra in praise of the Buddha. Grounded in the Tibetan source (^p409): foremost among beings, there is no other great ascetic (*dge sbyong chen po*) like the Buddha — not in the god-realms, this world, Vaiśravaṇa's abode, the palaces of the gods, nor in any direction; nowhere on this vast earth is there another like him. The card renders the stanza faithfully, condensing the enumerated places to "the heavens or this earth" (the list exists only to say *nowhere*).
