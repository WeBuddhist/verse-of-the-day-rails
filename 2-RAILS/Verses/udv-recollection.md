## Source Text (Degé Kangyur — Public Domain)
> གང་ཞིག་ཉིན་དང་མཚན་རྣམས་སུ། །སངས་རྒྱས་རྗེས་སུ་དྲན་བྱེད་ཅིང་། །གང་དག་སངས་རྒྱས་སྐྱབས་སོང་བ། །དེ་དག་མི་ཡི་རྙེད་པ་ཡིན།
> *(verbatim from `bo-udanavarga.md#^p445`, Udānavarga chapter 15 (Recollection); a self-contained quatrain.)*

## Grounding — Pali parallel
Parallel to the Pali **Dhp 296**: *suppabuddhaṁ pabujjhanti, sadā gotamasāvakā; yesaṁ divā ca ratto ca, niccaṁ buddhagatā sati* — the disciples of Gotama, whose mindfulness is directed day and night to the Buddha, wake up well. The Udānavarga verse shares the "day and night, mindful of the Buddha" core and adds the going-for-refuge and "true human gain" clause. The shipped bo is the verbatim Degé source; the renderings are made from the Tibetan, cross-checked against the Pali parallel.

## Disambiguated Meaning
Grounded in the Tibetan source (^p445) and the Dhp 296 parallel: those who, *nyin dang mtshan* — "by day and by night," *sangs rgyas rjes su dran byed* — "keep the Buddha in mind (buddhānussati)," and *sangs rgyas skyabs song ba* — "have gone to the Buddha for refuge (saraṇa)," *de dag mi yi rnyed pa yin* — "theirs is the (true) gain/attainment of a human being." The steadying point: to keep the Buddha in mind and take refuge is to make one's human life truly worthwhile.

- Rendering choice: *rnyed pa* literally "gain/acquisition/attainment"; rendered "the true human reward" so it reads as *the real prize of a human life*, not a transaction (a reviewer may prefer "a true human treasure" or "have truly gained as humans"). *rjes su dran* → "recollection / keeping in mind" (buddhānussati), the standard practice term. *skyabs* → "refuge" (saraṇa).
