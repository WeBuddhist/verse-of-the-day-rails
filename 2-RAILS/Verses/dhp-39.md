## Disambiguated Meaning
Grounded in the source verse and Sujato (1-SOURCES/Translations/en-dhammapada-sujato.md#^39): for one whose mind is not oozing/festering (*anavassuta-citta* — unsoaked by greed), whose heart is unstruck/unbattered (*ananvāhata-cetas* — not damaged by hate), who has let go of both merit and evil (*puññapāpapahīna*), and who is wakeful/alert (*jāgarato*), there is no fear (*natthi bhayaṁ*). A calm, blameless, watchful heart has nothing to fear. Complete four-line verse — rendered in full.

- *puññapāpapahīna* ("having given up both merit and evil") points to the arahant beyond accumulating good-and-bad kamma; a modern rendering may keep "given up right and wrong" (Sujato) or soften to "beyond good and evil" — flag for each termbase, but do not drop it.
- *anavassuta* / *ananvāhata* are vivid metaphors (not leaking, not battered); render the sense (untroubled by craving, unhurt by hate) plainly.
