## Disambiguated Meaning
Grounded in the Tibetan source (^p587) and 84000: Ānanda asks the Blessed One how to view bodhicitta; the Buddha answers with the gold analogy (pure by nature, unchanged however it's shaped), then speaks this closing verse: **the awakening mind (bodhicitta) is itself pure; work for the good of both self and other; see its empty/insubstantial nature; and aim toward what gives rise to wisdom.** Buddha-spoken, explicitly framed as a proclaimed verse ("the Blessed One proclaimed the following verse") answering Ānanda's direct question — not homage to the Buddha.

- Rendering choice: *byang chub sems* → "the awakening mind" (never "Great Vehicle mind"), per the vault's ecumenical-wording rule.
- Rendering choice: 84000's four line-broken clauses are compressed into two sentences without cutting any of the four clauses (pure · work for both · see emptiness · aim at wisdom's cause) — same technique used on `toh122-great-compassion` and `toh330-generosity`.
- Weakest-margin candidate of this batch: the 84000 wording alone runs ~157 chars; the own-translation compression below brings it to ~110, but flag for extra reviewer care that no nuance was lost in compressing.
