## Disambiguated Meaning
Grounded in the Tibetan source (^p519) and the 84000 reference: the Buddha ("the Teacher"), moved by compassion, answers a deva's question about what conduct leads to higher rebirth. This unit: **those who give up taking what isn't given, and always delight in giving — who give up stealing and do not steal — are headed for the higher realms.** Buddha-spoken, addressed to a divine being who asked in good faith (not homage paid to the Buddha).

- Rendering choice: 84000's English runs long (~150 chars) because it preserves the four-line verse structure with line breaks; the shipped rendering compresses the same four clauses into more natural prose without cutting any of them — same technique already used on the existing `toh122-great-compassion` and `toh194-patience` cards for repeating-refrain Kangyur verses.
- "Higher realms" (*mtho ris*) is kept general (not "heaven") to stay ecumenical and avoid asserting a specific cosmology beyond what the verse itself claims.
