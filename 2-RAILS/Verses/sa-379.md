## Disambiguated Meaning
Grounded directly in the Chinese source (`zh-samyukta-agama.md#^sa379-0`): "爾時，世尊告諸比丘：「有四聖諦。何等為四？謂苦聖諦、苦集聖諦、苦滅聖諦、苦滅道跡聖諦。」" — the Blessed One tells the monks: **there are four noble truths — the truth of suffering, the truth of the origin of suffering, the truth of the cessation of suffering, and the truth of the path leading to the cessation of suffering.**

- **Real-quote handling:** the full block continues with "若比丘於此四聖諦未無間等者，當修無間等…應當學" ("if a monk has not yet penetrated these four truths, they should train to do so…"). Per the "opening portion" allowance in `selection-criteria.md` §2, the card quotes the **opening enumeration only and stops there** — a complete, contiguous quote naming the four truths — rather than continuing into the training-clause or the (separate, longer) narrative frame. This is quoting-and-stopping, not distillation: nothing inside the quoted span is cut or reworded.
- 四聖諦 = the four noble truths; 苦/集/滅/道 = suffering / origin / cessation / path — standard termbase renderings (see `termbase.md`, four-noble-truths section, added for this card).
