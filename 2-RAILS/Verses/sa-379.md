## Disambiguated Meaning

Grounded in the Chinese source and Patton (1-SOURCES/Translations/en-samyukta-agama-patton.md#^sa379):

The Buddha proclaims the **four noble truths** in their threefold structure: *this is* suffering, its origin, its cessation, the path; suffering *is to be known*, its origin *abandoned*, its cessation *realized*, the path *cultivated*; and these tasks *have been accomplished*. For a daily card, distil the practical core: **know suffering · let go its cause · realize its ending · cultivate the path.**

- Keep the four-fold parallel and the action verbs (know / abandon / realize / cultivate) — that is the verse's structure.
- 四聖諦 = the four noble truths; 苦/集/滅/道 = suffering / origin / cessation / path — render in each language's standard Buddhist vocabulary (termbase).

## Theme & Selection Notes

Theme: **the Four Noble Truths** — the foundation of the whole teaching, and the natural verse for **Dhamma Day / Āsāḷha Pūjā** (the first sermon). Self-contained as the four-fold formula.
