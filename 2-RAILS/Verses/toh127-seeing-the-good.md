## Disambiguated Meaning
When you see the *byang chub snying po* — the essence/seed of awakening — in others, do not dwell on their faults (*'khrul pa*); do not let anger (*khong khro*) arise toward them, but always keep a loving mind (*byams pa'i sems*) (`bo-toh127.md#^p172`; meaning cross-checked against 84000 Toh 127 v. 40, reference only). The instruction turns attention away from cataloguing others' errors and toward their capacity for awakening, sustaining goodwill instead of resentment. In the sūtra it is spoken to Candraprabha as part of how a bodhisattva relates to others; extracted, it is a universal teaching on not fixating on faults and holding a kind heart.

**Flagged rendering choices.** *byang chub snying po* is literally "essence/heart of awakening" (bodhi); 84000 uses "essence of enlightenment." Render "essence of awakening" (ecumenical, plain). *byams pa* = loving-kindness (*mettā*) per termbase — keep the standard term, not "love." The Tibetan states the condition first ("when you see the essence of awakening [in them], do not look at their faults"); English may lead with "do not look at their faults" for flow, preserving both clauses.
