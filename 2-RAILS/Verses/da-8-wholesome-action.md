## Disambiguated Meaning
Grounded in the Chinese source (^pT01p0049b0401): the Buddha, asked whether he teaches for the sake of offerings/gain, answers that the Dharma he teaches is **subtle and supreme** (微妙第一), and its whole purpose is **to end what is unwholesome and increase what is wholesome** (為滅不善，增益善法) — *akusala* removed, *kusala* grown. It is a compact statement of the teaching's entire aim, and a close cousin of the mission verse Dhp 183 ("do a little less harm, a little more good"). Buddha-spoken, addressed to the wanderer Nigrodha; a teaching that points the way, not a proclamation about the Buddha.

- Rendering choice: 微妙第一 → "subtle and supreme" (subtle/profound + foremost); a reviewer may prefer "profound and foremost." 不善/善法 → the standard "unwholesome/wholesome" (*akusala/kusala*), not "bad/good deeds."
