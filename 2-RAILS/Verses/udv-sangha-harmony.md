## Disambiguated Meaning
Grounded in the Tibetan source (^p484) and its Pali parallel Dhp 194 (Sujato): four things are named as **happy / bringing ease** (*bde*, sukha): the **arising of Buddhas** in the world, the **teaching of the Dharma**, the **harmony of the Sangha** (*dge 'dun rnams ni 'thun pa*, saṅgha-sāmaggī), and the **united striving/practice of those in harmony** (*'thun pa rnams kyi dka' thub*, samaggānaṁ tapo). A wholly affirmative verse celebrating what makes a spiritual life and community joyful. Buddha-spoken (Udānavarga/Dharmapada utterance).

- Rendering choice: *dka' thub* (tapas) → "striving / practice" here (the disciplined effort of the community), following Sujato's "striving," not the literal "austerity"; the four-fold parallel "happy… happy…" structure is kept.
