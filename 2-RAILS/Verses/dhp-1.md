## Disambiguated Meaning

Grounded in the source verse and Sujato (1-SOURCES/Translations/en-dhammapada-sujato.md#^1):

Mind/intention (*mano*) goes before all experiences (*dhammā* — here our mental states / lived experiences); they are led by mind and made by mind. If one speaks or acts with a corrupted, ill-intentioned mind (*paduṭṭhena manasā*), suffering follows that person as the cartwheel follows the hoof of the ox that pulls it.

- *manopubbaṅgamā dhammā*: Sujato renders "intention shapes experiences"; the long-standing alternative is "mind precedes all mental states / all things." A modern rendering may use "mind," "intention," or "the heart" — flag for each stream's termbase.
- *dhammā* here = experiences / mental phenomena, **not** "teachings" — fix this sense in every rendering.

## Theme & Selection Notes

Theme: **the mind / intention.** The opening verse of the Dhammapada and its paired verse (Dhp 2) state the law of mind-made experience. Self-contained; iconic; excellent verse of the day. (Dhp 2 is the bright twin — could pair.)
