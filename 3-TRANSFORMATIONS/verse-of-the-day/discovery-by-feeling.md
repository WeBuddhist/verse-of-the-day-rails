# Discovery by Feeling — "Where are you right now?"

A relatability layer over the verse corpus, adapted from the Bible-app pattern
(YouVersion and similar surface *"verses for when you're feeling anxious /
grieving / grateful…"* — letting people start from their emotional state rather
than from a reference). We adapt the **mechanic**, not the theology.

## The adaptation — the Buddhist difference

The Bible-app version typically offers a verse as **reassurance** ("God is with
you"). Buddhism meets a mind-state differently: it **recognizes** the state,
**understands its nature** (impermanent, not-self, conditioned), and offers a
**skillful response** — the path's way through. So this feature is not "a verse
to feel better"; it is *"here is how the Buddha meets this."*

Each feeling therefore surfaces **two registers**:

1. **Meets you where you are** — a verse that names/honours the experience
   without judgement (so the person feels seen).
2. **Points the way through** — a verse that offers the skillful turn (the
   antidote, the insight, the practice).

This avoids spiritual bypassing and stays **non-manipulative** — it doesn't
exploit the emotional moment, it serves it. (Aligned with WeBuddhist values.)

## Entry points — two doors

Plain words, not Pali. We **don't** copy the Bible-app list wholesale — some of
its entries are Christian-framed and don't map to Buddhist psychology (e.g.
*"tempted"* / *"sinful"* / *"guilty before God"*; the Buddhist equivalent of
temptation is **craving**, already covered below). And we **add** the qualities
Buddhism actively cultivates — the brahmavihāras and other wholesome states —
which a Bible-style "emotions" list omits.

So there are two doors into the corpus:

**A. Difficult states you're *in*** (meet + transform):
> anxious · afraid · angry / irritated · sad / grieving · lonely ·
> restless / can't settle · overwhelmed / stressed · discouraged / low ·
> craving / wanting · doubtful / lost · ashamed / regretful

**B. Qualities you want to *grow*** (nourish + deepen):
> loving-kindness (*mettā*) · compassion (*karuṇā*) · joy in others' good
> (*muditā*) · equanimity (*upekkhā*) · **spiritual friendship / sangha
> (*kalyāṇa-mittatā*)** · **belonging / community** · gratitude · generosity ·
> mindfulness · patience · contentment · calm · aspiration

Door A is the "verses for when you feel…" mechanic. Door B — *"a verse to grow
loving-kindness / friendship / gratitude today"* — is the Buddhist addition, and
fits practice better: these are states one deliberately cultivates, not just
moods that arrive. Both grow as needed.

### Grounded in WeBuddhist's mission & pillars (confirmed against the knowledge base)

Reconciled with `10_ORG/11_Strategy/🌟North_Star.md` (updated 2026-06-22).

**The mission *is* a verse.** WeBuddhist's stated mission — *"help Buddhists do a
little less harm, a little more good, and know their own minds — day after day"*
— is the Buddha's own summary, **Dhp 183** (avoid evil, do good, purify the
mind). So the whole feature points back to one north-star teaching; treat
Dhp 183 as the anchor verse of the corpus.

**Four pillars: Learn · Practice · Connect · Sustain.** The cultivation themes
map onto the three practitioner-facing pillars (Sustain is the funding pillar):

- **Learn** — *Dhamma in their own words*: wisdom, the path, mindfulness, aspiration (Dhp 1, Dhp 183).
- **Practice** — *transformed through daily habits*: the brahmavihāras (mettā, karuṇā, muditā, upekkhā), patience, generosity (*dāna*), contentment, calm.
- **Connect** — *share transformation through communities*: **sangha, spiritual friendship (*kalyāṇa-mittatā*), belonging.** A first-class theme — the Buddha put it at the centre: *"Admirable friendship is the entire holy life"* (SN 45.2).

**This feature serves both halves of the platform.** The North Star frames the
product as **Reliability + Relatability**: *"Without trusted content we are just a
scripture database; without engaging content we are just another wellness app."*
The rails are Reliability (every verse a verified canonical source); discovery-
by-feeling is **Relatability**. And it's built for **Persona F** — "going through
a difficult period, trusts the teachings can help, if she could only find the
right ones."

**Tone — "Sacred, Not Precious."** The audience is *already-Buddhist
practitioners* (it should "feel like home," not like Headspace/Calm). So the
"meets you / points through" framing is right: speak to a practitioner who knows
the Dharma, not a wellness consumer. Keywords: depth, stillness, clarity,
groundedness, contemporary. (YouVersion is an approved reference for *clean
readability at scale* — we adapt its mechanic, in this register.)

## The Buddhist lens (what's underneath)

Each felt-state maps to a Buddhist understanding — usually a **hindrance**
(*nīvaraṇa*) or affliction, paired with its antidote, or a **wholesome state**
(*brahmavihāra* / merit) to encourage. This lens guides which verses fit and how
they're framed — it is **not** shown raw to a Tier 3/4 user.

**Door A — difficult states (meet + transform)**

| anxious / restless | restlessness-worry (*uddhacca-kukkucca*) | mindfulness, settling | Dhp 21, Dhp 1 |
| afraid | fear from clinging | refuge, seeing impermanence | (to tag) |

## How it's wired into the verses

Each verse rail (`2-RAILS/Verses/<slug>.md`) carries a tag:

```yaml
speaks_to: [angry, lonely]      # everyday felt-states this verse addresses
buddhist_lens: "ill-will → non-anger (mettā)"   # one-line framing (curator-facing)
```

The discovery feature queries `speaks_to:`. The curator's job is to tag verses
honestly (which real human moments does this verse actually meet?) and, over the
corpus, to ensure **every common felt-state has at least one "meets you" and one
"points through" verse** across the three canons.

## Wellbeing guardrail

Some entries (despair, severe anxiety, grief, shame) touch real distress. A verse
is **not** therapy or crisis support. For the heaviest states the feature should
pair the verse with a gentle path to help (a "talk to someone" / support-resources
link), never imply a verse alone resolves a crisis, and avoid any verse that could
read as harsh or guilt-inducing in a vulnerable moment. This is a hard rule, not a
nicety — consistent with WeBuddhist's care for practitioner wellbeing.

## Relationship to verse-of-the-day

Same corpus, two doors in: **verse of the day** (a curated daily card) and
**discovery by feeling** (the user starts from their state). The `speaks_to:`
tags serve both — and a future "today I'm feeling…" entry could even personalise
the daily verse.
