**Gate-boundary note (Toh 95) — RESOLVED by Evan, 2026-07-13.** Several candidates above (the newborn declaration, the palace-gate renunciation vow, "I will end old age and death," rejecting Rudraka Rāmaputra's attainment) are the bodhisattva's own **pre-enlightenment** words. **Ruling: these are usable as buddhavacana.** The deciding test Evan set: pre-awakening words count *only when they are from the Buddha's final lifetime — the one in which he became the Buddha — not from a past-life Jātaka.* The Lalitavistara is exactly that: a biography of Gautama's last life (Tuṣita descent → birth as Siddhārtha → renunciation → awakening → first sermon), not a Jātaka, so its pre-enlightenment lines qualify. The gate is unchanged for its normal cases — words of disciples, gods, kings, omens (e.g. the rejected "sound of the cymbals" lead), or the Bodhisattva in a *past* life still fail. Individual candidates covered by this ruling: the newborn declaration, the palace-gate vow ("until I attain unsurpassed awakening, I will not enter Kapilavastu"), "I will put an end to old age and death," rejecting Rudraka Rāmaputra's attainment, the jhāna-memory/middle-way-discovery scene (needs heavy compression), and the Tathāgata's vow not to pass into parinirvāṇa prematurely. All are `unclaimed` and pickable; `verse-rail` still re-verifies each quote verbatim against `bo-toh95.md` at claim time.
