# Candidate Pool — Rejected

Passages considered and disqualified, so a future tagging pass doesn't re-read and re-derive the same rejection. One line of reason is enough — the point is to save the *next* scan the work, not to justify the call at length.

### Toh 323 (The Single Stanza / Ekagāthā)
- link: [[1-SOURCES/Text/bo-toh323.md#^p409]]
- reason: homage to the Buddha (praise stanza — "there is no other ascetic like him"), not spoken *by* him. Gate 1 fail.

### Toh 143 (The Two Stanza Dhāraṇī)
- link: [[1-SOURCES/Text/bo-toh143.md#^p14]]
- reason: dhāraṇī/mantra text (contains untranslatable mantra syllables — "tadyathā oṃ vajra­prakāra…"); no clear "Blessed One said" framing in the passage (homage is to Mañjuśrī, not narrated as Buddha-speech); genre borders on tantra-adjacent even though filed under mdo. Skip — not a confident buddhavacana quote.

### Toh 150 (The Inquiry of Avalokiteśvara on the Seven Qualities) — DEFERRED, not a clean reject
- link: [[1-SOURCES/Text/bo-toh150.md#^p663]]
- reason: good content (Buddha to Avalokiteśvara, seven trainings for a new bodhisattva) and one item ("with a mind like a bird, be without grasping") looks promising as a single extractable clause — but the Tibetan runs the seven items as one continuous list joined by "dang" (and), not as cleanly separated sentences the way the English 84000 numbering (1)-(7) implies. Confirming a genuinely clean, self-contained extraction needs a closer read than this pass budgeted for. Not tagged either way — flagged so the next pass starts here instead of re-discovering this from zero, but doesn't skip the harder read.

### SĀ 259 (Direct Realization)
- link: [[1-SOURCES/Text/zh-samyukta-agama.md#^sa259-0]]
- reason: vivid, memorable simile ("the five acquired aggregates are like illnesses, abscesses, thorns, and killers") and genuinely self-contained — but it's **spoken by Śāriputra**, answering Mahākauṣṭhila's question, not by the Buddha. Gate 1 fail (same category as disciple-spoken verses being out of scope). Worth flagging because the simile is good enough that a future pass might want to reach for it again without checking who's speaking.

### SĀ 784 and SĀ 785 — DEFERRED, translation-mismatch discovered
- link: [[1-SOURCES/Text/zh-samyukta-agama.md#^sa784-0]] and [[1-SOURCES/Text/zh-samyukta-agama.md#^sa785-0]]
- reason: the Patton English at these two anchors is a fully-spelled-out, elaborate exposition (worldly vs. noble right view/speech/action/etc., each redefined at length). The actual Chinese source at both anchors is much more compact and uses elliptical "乃至" ("and so on") compression (784) or a plain list of the ten wholesome/unwholesome actions (785) — genuinely different content than what Patton's wording implies. **Do not use Patton's wording for either of these anchors.** The Chinese itself looks like it could still be a fair candidate (785 in particular is the classic ten-wholesome-actions formula — a possible fit for the "five precepts" / ethics theme gap), but needs its own fresh read directly from the Chinese, not reconstructed from Patton, before tagging — deferred rather than rushed. **This is the second Patton/Chinese misalignment found in this corpus (after SĀ 379)** — worth treating as a standing risk for the rest of the SĀ Patton pairing, not a one-off.

### MĀ 5 (The Wood Pile Parable)
- link: [[1-SOURCES/Text/zh-madhyama-agama.md#^ma5-0]]
- reason: genuinely Buddha-spoken and on-topic (the danger of unrestrained sensual conduct), but the central simile is graphic/harsh — a burning wood pile and molten iron balls burning through the body — the kind of imagery `selection-criteria.md` §3 says to keep off the daily card regardless of buddhavacana/length compliance. Tone/wellbeing gate fail, not a buddhavacana or length fail; a new rejection category worth naming explicitly (see friction-log note in `candidate-tagging/SKILL.md`).

### MĀ 9 (Seven Chariots)
- link: [[1-SOURCES/Text/zh-madhyama-agama.md#^ma9-0]]
- reason: the actual purification-stages teaching (seven chariots relaying a traveler to their destination, mapped to the seven purifications) is spoken by the disciple Pūrṇa Maitrāyaniputra, answering Śāriputra's question — not by the Buddha (who only appears afterward, endorsing what was said). Gate 1 fail — same disciple-spoken pattern as SĀ 259.

### MĀ 31 (Discernment of the Noble Truths)
- link: [[1-SOURCES/Text/zh-madhyama-agama.md#^ma31-0]]
- reason: the Buddha frames the four noble truths and hands the detailed teaching to Śāriputra, who delivers essentially the entire discourse (including the closing verse praising the Buddha's insight — "This is what Venerable Śāriputra said"). Gate 1 fail — same disciple-spoken pattern as SĀ 259 and MĀ 9; the closing verse is additionally praise-of-the-Buddha rather than Buddha-spoken (compounding gate 1 fail).
