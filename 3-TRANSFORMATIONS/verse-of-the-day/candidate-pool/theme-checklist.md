# Theme checklist — core Buddhist teachings to watch for

A reference list `candidate-tagging` checks candidates against when deciding whether to tag `theme:`. This is **not** the `speaks_to` felt-state list (that one's fixed — see `discovery-by-feeling.md`); this is the doctrinal-topic side, and it's deliberately semi-open: it grows when tagging hits a clearly-recognized core teaching that isn't listed yet (see `4-SYSTEM/Skills/candidate-tagging/SKILL.md`).

A candidate only gets a theme tag if it (a) is a genuinely good, self-contained, gate-passing quote on its own terms, and (b) is a clean instance of something on this list. A good quote that doesn't match anything here still goes in the pool untagged for theme (still tagged for `speaks_to` if it fits there).

## Already covered (see `log.md`'s `theme` column for the full running list — don't re-flag these as gaps)

friendship, impermanence, non-hatred, self-mastery, mind, right-view, overcoming-anger, self-reflection, mindfulness-of-breathing, loving-kindness, not-self, speech, generosity, patience, effort, peace-of-a-good-heart, threefold-training, equanimity, contentment, truthfulness, wholesome-action, harmlessness, inner-peace, self-knowledge, wise-priorities, taming-the-mind, small-good-deeds, freedom-from-craving, self-responsibility, gradual-purification, living-lightly, serenity-of-the-wise, words-and-deeds, true-nobility, purity-of-mind, bodhicitta, thought-shapes-mind, great-compassion, concentration, gratitude, buddhas-purpose, gift-of-teaching, non-attached-giving, virtue-fragrance, true-renunciation, middle-way (partial — SN 1.1 only), self-reliance, genuine-cultivation, seeing-the-good, innate-purity (emptiness, accessible framing), four-noble-truths, bodhicitta-is-pure.

Themes may recur (never back-to-back) — a theme already covered is not a reason to skip a good new candidate on the same ground; it's just not a "gap."

## Known gaps — actively look for these

- **skilful means (upāya)** — not yet represented.
- **restraint of the senses (indriyasaṁvara)** — not yet represented. Candidates found: EA ea-a, ea-b, ea-c, three instances all from the Ekottarika shortcut-list pass (`candidate-pool/chinese.md`).
- **dependent origination (paṭiccasamuppāda)** — touched partially by EA ea-q's closing clause ("the darkness of delusion — remove it entirely with the twelve links") but not centered as its own theme there. Strong candidate now found: EA juan 30, the compact "this being, that becomes; this ceasing, that ceases" formula framing the full twelve-link chain (`candidate-pool/chinese.md`).
- **forgiveness** — not yet represented. Candidate found: DA 27, the Buddha accepting King Ajātaśatru's repentance for patricide ("one who can repent within the noble teaching benefits themselves; out of compassion, I accept your repentance") (`candidate-pool/chinese.md`).
- **refuge / confidence (saddhā)** — not yet represented as its own theme (distinct from the self-reliance / self-refuge angle already covered). Candidate found: DA 2-b, "the teaching and discipline are your refuge after I am gone" (`candidate-pool/chinese.md`).
- **karma and its fruit** — touched by `toh330-generosity` and `t366-roots-of-merit`, but no card centers it as the theme itself. Candidate found: EA juan 28, the butcher-who-rides-in-luxury teaching (present fortune traced to past-life merit, not to a harmful livelihood) (`candidate-pool/chinese.md`).
- **renunciation** — touched by `t210-true-renunciation`; a second, fresh angle would still be welcome.
- **middle way** — only SN 1.1 so far; a clearer/different angle would help. Candidate found: EA ea-h, "the middle way of diligence" (over-effort vs. laziness) (`candidate-pool/chinese.md`).
- **sympathetic joy (muditā)** — covered under `speaks_to` on some cards, but no theme/day centers it. Candidate found: EA (juan 6-8, Rāhula) — "cultivate a mind of joy; once joy is cultivated, all envy is entirely removed" (`candidate-pool/chinese.md`).
- **dedication of merit** — not yet represented (Mahāyāna).
- **right speech / right livelihood specifically** — "speech" (Dhp 100) is covered generally; the ethical-conduct angle specifically is not.
- **emptiness (śūnyatā), a Pali/Chinese-side example** — `innate-purity` and `bodhicitta-is-pure` cover it from the Tibetan side; a Pali or Chinese source teaching it directly would round this out. Candidate found: EA (juan 10, to Śakra) — "observed thus, no perception arises; with no perception, there is no fear; with no fear, there is final nirvāṇa" (`candidate-pool/chinese.md`).
- **full liberation / the arahant's self-declaration** ("birth is ended, the holy life fulfilled, the task done, no more becoming") — a classic, recurring canonical formula (found at DA 3, DA 5, DA 6 in the Dīrgha Āgama alone) distinct from any already-covered theme; not yet centered on a card. Candidate found: DA 5 (`candidate-pool/chinese.md`).
- **practice as true offering/homage** ("one who receives the teaching and puts it into practice — this is called true offering to the Tathāgata") — a recognized formula distinct from `non-attached-giving` (which is about the spirit of material giving, not what counts as genuine devotion to the Buddha); prompted in its source context by devas scattering flowers, which the Buddha names as not the real offering. Candidate found: DA 2-a (`candidate-pool/chinese.md`).
- **unstained in the world** (the lotus rising clean from mud, as an image for the Buddha/a practitioner unmarked by worldly conditions) — distinct from `innate-purity`, which covers emptiness/inherent purity from an introspective, Tibetan-side angle; this is an external, image-based instance of the same idea. Candidate found: EA ea-i (`candidate-pool/chinese.md`).
- **three poisons and their remedies** (greed/hatred/delusion, each paired with its specific antidote — impurity-contemplation, loving-kindness, dependent origination) — a recognized, compact formula distinct from `overcoming-anger` (which only covers one of the three). Candidate found: EA ea-q (`candidate-pool/chinese.md`).
- **moderation in eating** — eating only to sustain the body rather than for pleasure; distinct from `contentment` (general) and `living-lightly`. Candidate found: EA juan 12, the cart-axle/greasing simile (`candidate-pool/chinese.md`).
- **heedfulness as the foundation of virtue** (appamāda/不放逸 as the ground all other good qualities grow from, not just "effort" applied to a task) — distinct from `effort` and `self-mastery`. Candidate found: EA juan 18, the earth simile ("all plants depend on the earth to grow; all good qualities depend on heedfulness") (`candidate-pool/chinese.md`).
- **detachment from gain and honor** (not clinging to material support, fame, or veneration — the "gain/loss" pair of the eight worldly winds) — distinct from `freedom-from-craving` (broader) and `non-attached-giving` (about giving, not receiving). Candidate found: EA juan 13 (`candidate-pool/chinese.md`).
- **true kingship / true authority reframed** (the Buddha declining worldly kingship: "I am already a Dharma king") — minor, narrow gap. Candidate found: EA juan 14 (`candidate-pool/chinese.md`).
- **spiritual equality in the sangha** (renunciation erases caste distinction, as rivers lose their names on entering the ocean) — distinct from `wholesome-action`'s conduct-over-birth angle (EA juan 18); this one is specifically about unity/equality within the monastic community itself. Candidate found: EA juan 21, the four-rivers simile (`candidate-pool/chinese.md`).
- **communal harmony among practitioners** (the six conditions of concord — shared bodily/verbal/mental kindness, sharing material gains, holding precepts and right view in common) — distinct from `spiritual-friendship` (dyadic); this is about a whole community's cohesion. Candidate found: EA juan 29 (`candidate-pool/chinese.md`).
- **the seven factors of awakening as medicine** — distinct from `concentration`; the specific framing of the seven awakening-factors (mindfulness, investigation, effort, joy, tranquility, concentration, equanimity) together as a supreme remedy that heals illness itself. Candidate found: EA juan 33, the Junda/均頭 healing episode (`candidate-pool/chinese.md`).
- **precious human life / rarity of encountering the Dharma** — a classic, widely-recognized Buddhist teaching (human birth is hard to obtain; meeting a Buddha's teaching harder still) not yet represented at all. Candidate found: EA juan 33, the wheel-turning-king contrast (`candidate-pool/chinese.md`).
- **ten recollections (anussati)** — a recognized formula (Buddha, Dharma, Sangha, virtue, generosity, the heavens, stillness, breath, body, death) distinct from `mindfulness-of-breathing`, which only covers one item on the list. Candidate found: EA juan 34, taught to the miserly Vilāsin via Ānanda (`candidate-pool/chinese.md`).
- **mindfulness of death (maraṇasati)** — distinct from `impermanence` (general) and `mindfulness-of-breathing` (general); this is specifically contemplating death through the breath rather than as a countdown of days left to live. Candidate found: EA juan 35, the Buddha correcting monks' shallow accounts of death-contemplation (`candidate-pool/chinese.md`).
- **the four dharma-seals as a compact formula** (impermanence / suffering / not-self / nirvana-as-cessation stated together as one fourfold teaching) — distinct from `impermanence` alone. Candidate found: EA juan 36, spoken to the grieving people of Vesālī on the Buddha's last journey (`candidate-pool/chinese.md`).
- **right speech / right livelihood as their own compact formulas** — distinct from general `speech` (Dhp 100/133). Candidates found: Snp 3.3 and Udānavarga ch.8 (the fourfold "well-spoken speech" formula, both Pali and Tibetan instances) for right speech; Udānavarga ch.13 ("not trading in the Dharma like a merchant") for right livelihood (`candidate-pool/pali.md`, `candidate-pool/tibetan.md`).
- **the unconditioned / Nibbāna's ontological status** ("there is an unborn, unbecome, unmade, unconditioned") — distinct from `innate-purity`/`bodhicitta-is-pure` (which are about mind-nature, not nirvana itself). Candidates found: Ud 8.3 (Pali) and Udānavarga ch.26 (Tibetan) — both closely parallel (`candidate-pool/pali.md`, `candidate-pool/tibetan.md`).
- **full liberation / arahant's self-declaration, a first-person Buddha version** — the existing gap note (DA 5) was third-person; now also found as a direct first-person declaration. Candidate found: Udānavarga ch.21, "I am the unsurpassed Worthy One of the world..." (`candidate-pool/tibetan.md`); Pali-side instances also found at Ud 3.10, 7.1, 7.2, 8.9, 8.10 (`candidate-pool/pali.md`).
- **skilful means (upāya), first real candidate** — previously listed as "not yet represented." Candidate found: Ud 8.6, the Buddha crossing the flooded Ganges while others search for boats ("the wise have already crossed") (`candidate-pool/pali.md`).
- **dedication of merit, first real candidate** — previously listed as "not yet represented" (Mahāyāna). Candidate found: Ud 8.6a, the anumodanā verse dedicating merit to local deities at Pāṭaligāma (`candidate-pool/pali.md`).
- **restraint of the senses, a clean Pali/Tibetan-canon instance** — previously only Chinese Ekottarika candidates existed. Candidates found: Dhp 360-361 and Ud 3.5 (`candidate-pool/pali.md`); no clean standalone Tibetan instance found yet in the Udānavarga (recurs but always bundled with other material).
- **detachment from gain and honor (eight worldly winds), Pali/Tibetan instances** — previously only a Chinese EA candidate existed. Candidates found: Snp 2.4 (Maṅgalasutta) and Iti 81 (Pali); Udānavarga ch.13 (Tibetan) (`candidate-pool/pali.md`, `candidate-pool/tibetan.md`).
- **spiritual equality / conduct over birth-caste, Tibetan instance** — previously only Chinese (EA juan 21) and Pali (Snp 1.7, 3.9, Dhp 393-pattern) instances existed. Candidate found: Udānavarga ch.33 (`candidate-pool/tibetan.md`).
- **communal harmony, Pali/Tibetan instances** — previously only a Chinese EA candidate existed. Candidates found: Iti 19 (Pali); Udānavarga ch.30 (Tibetan, Dhp 194 parallel) (`candidate-pool/pali.md`, `candidate-pool/tibetan.md`).
- **three poisons and their remedies, Pali instance** — previously only a Chinese EA candidate existed. Candidates found: Iti 50 and Iti 93 (the latter explicitly pairs each poison with its named antidote) (`candidate-pool/pali.md`).
- **moderation in eating, Pali instance** — previously only a Chinese EA candidate existed. Candidate found: Ud 4.6 (`candidate-pool/pali.md`).
- **heedfulness as the foundation of virtue, Pali/Tibetan instances** — previously only a Chinese EA candidate existed. Candidates found: Dhp 21, Snp 2.10, Iti 23, Iti 47 (Pali); Udānavarga ch.4 (Tibetan, Dhp 21 parallel) (`candidate-pool/pali.md`, `candidate-pool/tibetan.md`).
- **karma and its fruit, Pali/Tibetan instances** — previously only a Chinese EA candidate existed. Candidates found: Snp 3.9, Ud 3.1, Ud 5.4 (Pali); Udānavarga ch.9 (Tibetan) (`candidate-pool/pali.md`, `candidate-pool/tibetan.md`).
- **refuge / confidence (saddhā), Tibetan instances** — previously only a Chinese DA candidate existed. Candidates found: Udānavarga ch.10 and ch.15 (`candidate-pool/tibetan.md`).
- **true kingship / true authority reframed, Pali instance** — previously only a Chinese EA candidate existed. Candidate found: Snp 3.7, the Buddha's reply to the brahmin Sela (`candidate-pool/pali.md`).
- **unstained in the world (the lotus rising clean from mud), Tibetan instance** — previously only a Chinese EA candidate existed, and this is the *literal* lotus-and-mud image the Chinese candidate only alludes to. Candidate found: Udānavarga ch.18 (`candidate-pool/tibetan.md`).
- **dependent origination (paṭiccasamuppāda), Pali instances** — previously only Chinese EA candidates existed. Candidates found: Ud 1.1 (the flagship "first night of enlightenment" verse) and Snp 3.12 (`candidate-pool/pali.md`).
- **a new theme candidate: "root-of-conflict" (attadaṇḍa)** — the psychological origin of violence in self-protective fear, distinct from `harmlessness` (the ethic of not-harming), which names *why* conflict arises rather than just condemning it. Candidate found: Snp 4.15, the Buddha's own account of the saṁvega he felt seeing people quarrel (`candidate-pool/pali.md`). Not yet added to "already covered" — flagging for a human's judgment on whether this merits its own theme line or folds into `harmlessness`.
- **a new theme candidate: "attachment and grief"** — clinging to loved ones specifically, distinct from general `impermanence`. Candidate found: Udānavarga ch.5, Dhp 212 parallel (`candidate-pool/tibetan.md`).
- **a new theme candidate: "the Buddha's hesitation to teach" / "resolve to teach"** — the profundity-of-realization episode (the Dharma seeming too subtle for anyone to understand, followed by the decision to teach anyway), distinct from `buddhas-purpose` (general statement of aim) and `gift-of-teaching` (about receiving/practicing teaching, not the Buddha's own doubt before giving it). A matched pair of candidates found: Toh 95 (Lalitavistara), `toh95-hesitation-to-teach` and `toh95-resolve-to-teach` (`candidate-pool/tibetan.md`).
- **a new theme candidate: "testing a teaching against its true fruits"** — evaluating whether a practice actually leads to dispassion, cessation, and awakening (an MN 26/Ariyapariyesana-style formula), distinct from `middle-way`. Candidate found: Toh 95, the bodhisattva's reply rejecting Rudraka Rāmaputra's attainment as insufficient (`candidate-pool/tibetan.md`).
- **a new theme candidate: "the Buddha's vow to remain for the world's benefit"** — an active refusal to pass into parinirvāṇa prematurely, tied to the Three Jewels being firmly established, distinct from a final acceptance-of-death framing. Candidate found: Toh 95, the Tathāgata's reply to Māra (`candidate-pool/tibetan.md`).
- **middle way, a second clear Tibetan-canon instance** — previously only SN 1.1 (partial). Candidates found: Toh 95, the jhāna-memory/middle-way-discovery scene and the first-sermon eightfold-path passage (`candidate-pool/tibetan.md`).

## How this grows

When `candidate-tagging` finds a passage that's a clean instance of a real, recognized core Buddhist teaching not listed above (and not already in the "already covered" list), add it to "Known gaps" (or move it to "already covered" once a day actually runs on it — that housekeeping can happen whenever `log.md` is next updated, not urgently). Don't add vague or one-off phrasings — this list is for teachings substantial enough that a reader would recognize them as "a thing," not a paraphrase invented for one verse.
