# Day 11 — Not-Self (SĀ 33)

Rail: [[2-RAILS/Verses/sa-33]] · grounded in Patton (CC0); CBETA source CC BY-NC-SA.
**Note (distillation):** SĀ 33 is a prose discourse with a dialectical Q&A (impermanent → painful → not-self). Unlike a gāthā, it has no compact verse form, so the card renders the sutta's **own concluding statement** faithfully rather than a coined summary — but this is still a distillation, flagged per the "prefer short verses over heavy editing" rule. ## Renderings (one short line each)

- **en** — Body and mind are not your self, nor your own. Seeing this, you grasp at nothing, cling to nothing, and the heart is freed.
- **zh** — 色非我，受、想、行、識亦然；如是觀五蘊非我、非我所，則無所取著，心得解脫。
- **bo** — ⚑ གཟུགས་བདག་མ་ཡིན། ཚོར་བ་སོགས་ཀྱང་དེ་བཞིན། ཕུང་པོ་ལྔ་བདག་དང་བདག་གི་མ་ཡིན་པར་མཐོང་ན། གང་ལའང་མི་འཛིན་ཅིང་ཆགས་པ་མེད་པས་སེམས་གྲོལ།
- **hi** — शरीर और मन न आत्मा हैं, न तुम्हारे अपने; यह देखकर तुम किसी को नहीं पकड़ते, किसी से नहीं चिपकते, और मन मुक्त हो जाता है।
- **ne** — शरीर र मन न आत्मा हुन्, न आफ्ना; यो देखेर तिमी केहीलाई पक्रँदैनौ, केहीमा टाँसिँदैनौ, र मन मुक्त हुन्छ।
- **mn** — ⚑ Бие, сэтгэл нь би ч биш, миний ч биш; үүнийг үзвэл юунд ч зуурахгүй, юунд ч наалдахгүй, сэтгэл чөлөөлөгдөнө.

## QA — pre-review (against 2-RAILS/Verses/sa-33.md)

### en
- Back-translation: "Body and mind are not your self, nor your own. Seeing this, you grasp at nothing, cling to nothing, and the heart is freed."
- Findings: none critical. Matches the sutta's conclusion (aggregates not self / not belonging to self → grasp nothing → attach to nothing → freed) ✓. "Body and mind" glosses the five aggregates for a no-glossary audience. No em dash ✓.
- Confidence flags: "body and mind" as a gloss of 五蘊 — reviewer may prefer "all that you are."
- Net: clean for review.

### zh (Traditional)
- Back-translation: "Form is not self; feeling, perception, volition, consciousness are likewise; contemplating the five aggregates as not-self and not-mine, one grasps and clings to nothing, and the mind is liberated."
- Findings: uses the sūtra's own 色/受想行識, 五蘊, 非我非我所, 心解脫 ✓. Maximally faithful. Traditional ✓.
- Net: clean for review.

### hi
- Back-translation: "Body and mind are neither self nor your own; seeing this you grasp no one, cling to nothing, and the mind becomes free."
- Findings: आत्मा (self) / अपना (mine) contrast preserved; मुक्त = liberation. Faithful.
- Net: clean for review.

### ne
- Back-translation: "Body and mind are neither self nor one's own; seeing this you grasp nothing, cling to nothing, and the mind becomes free."
- Confidence flags: पक्रँदैनौ / टाँसिँदैनौ verb register; naturalness.
- Net: needs native review.

### bo ⚑
- Back-translation (approx): "Form is not self; feeling and the rest likewise. Seeing the five aggregates as not self and not mine, one grasps at nothing and, being without attachment, the mind is freed."
- Findings: བདག་མེད (anattā), ཕུང་པོ་ལྔ (five aggregates), སེམས་གྲོལ (liberation) per termbase ✓.
- Confidence flags: བདག་གི (mine) phrasing; verb forms of grasp/cling.
- Net: escalate to native Tibetan dharma reviewer.

### mn ⚑
- Back-translation (approx): "Body and mind are neither 'I' nor 'mine'; seeing this, one clings to nothing, attaches to nothing, and the mind is liberated."
- Findings: би биш / миний биш for not-self/not-mine; termbase anattā row (би үгүй) is ⚑.
- Confidence flags: anattā term; cetovimutti (сэтгэлийн чөлөөлөл ⚑).
- Net: escalate to native Mongolian dharma reviewer.
