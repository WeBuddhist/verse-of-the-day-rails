# Day 11 — Not-Self (Dhp 62)

Rail: [[2-RAILS/Verses/dhp-62]] · grounded in Sujato (CC0).
Replaces the earlier SĀ 33 pick for this slot: a short single verse, **translated in full** (no distillation), per the "prefer short verses over heavy editing" rule. Keeps the not-self theme. Meaning: the unwise fret over "my children, my wealth," but even one's own self is not truly one's own, so how could anything else be?

## Renderings (one short line each)

- **en** — "My children, my wealth," the fool frets. But even your self is not your own, so how could children or wealth be?
- **zh** — 「子屬我，財屬我」，愚者如是憂惱；連自身尚且不屬於己，何況子與財？
- **bo** — ⚑ བུ་ནི་ངའི་ཡིན། ནོར་ནི་ངའི་ཡིན་ཞེས་བླུན་པོ་ཉོན་མོངས། བདག་ཉིད་ཀྱང་བདག་གི་མིན་ན། བུ་དང་ནོར་ལྟ་ཅི་སྨོས།
- **hi** — 'पुत्र मेरे हैं, धन मेरा है,' ऐसा सोचकर मूर्ख व्याकुल होता है। जब अपना आप ही अपना नहीं, तो पुत्र और धन कैसे?
- **ne** — 'छोराछोरी मेरा हुन्, धन मेरो हो,' भन्दै मूर्ख व्याकुल हुन्छ। जब आफै आफ्नो होइन भने, छोराछोरी र धन कसरी?
- **mn** — ⚑ "Хүүхэд минь, эд хөрөнгө минь" хэмээн мунхаг нь зовдог. Өөрөө ч өөрийн биш байтал хүүхэд, эд хөрөнгө хаанаас нь?

## QA — pre-review (against 2-RAILS/Verses/dhp-62.md)

### en
- Back-translation: "'My children, my wealth,' the fool frets. But even your self is not your own, so how could children or wealth be?"
- Findings: none critical. Faithful to Sujato and the rail: possessive worry (*vihaññati*) + the "not even the self is your own" pivot (*attā hi attano natthi*) + a fortiori children/wealth ✓. *bāla* rendered "fool" (Sujato); could soften to "the unwise" (flagged in rail). No em dash ✓.
- Net: clean for review.

### zh (Traditional)
- Back-translation: "'Children belong to me, wealth belongs to me,' the fool worries thus; even one's own body does not belong to oneself, let alone children and wealth?"
- Findings: 愚者 = *bāla*; 憂惱 = *vihaññati*; the non-ownership pivot preserved (連自身尚且不屬於己). Traditional ✓.
- Net: clean for review.
