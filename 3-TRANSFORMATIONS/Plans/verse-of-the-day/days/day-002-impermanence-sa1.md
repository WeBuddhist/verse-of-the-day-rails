# Day 2 — Impermanence (SĀ 1)

Rail: [[2-RAILS/Verses/sa-1]] · grounded in Patton (CC0).
SĀ 1 is prose, not verse — per the rail and `selection-criteria.md` §2, the card distils the single causal chain rather than the whole discourse: *seeing the aggregates as impermanent → disenchantment → fading of delight-and-greed → liberation of the mind.* Sober theme; space it out (§3).

## Renderings (one short line each)

- **en** — See body and mind as impermanent. From that seeing comes disenchantment, greed fades, and the heart is set free.
- **zh** — 觀身心無常；如是觀照便生厭離，貪欲止息，心得解脫。
- **bo** — ⚑ ལུས་སེམས་མི་རྟག་པར་མཐོང་ན། སྐྱོ་ཤས་སྐྱེ་ཞིང་འདོད་ཆགས་ཟད་དེ་སེམས་རྣམ་པར་གྲོལ།
- **hi** — शरीर और मन को अनित्य देखो; ऐसा देखने से विरक्ति जागती है, लोभ शांत होता है, और मन मुक्त हो जाता है।
- **ne** — शरीर र मनलाई अनित्य देख; यसरी देख्दा विरक्ति जाग्छ, लोभ शान्त हुन्छ, र मन मुक्त हुन्छ।
- **mn** — ⚑ Бие, сэтгэлийг хувирамтгай хэмээн үз; ийнхүү үзвэл залхрал төрж, шунал арилж, сэтгэл чөлөөлөгдөнө.

## QA — pre-review (against 2-RAILS/Verses/sa-1.md)

### en
- Back-translation: "See body and mind as impermanent; from that seeing comes disenchantment, greed fades, and the heart is freed."
- Findings: none critical. "Body and mind" glosses the five aggregates (form + the four mental aggregates) for a no-glossary audience — faithful to the chain. *anicca* → "impermanent" ✓; 厭離/disenchantment ✓; 心解脫 → "the heart is set free" (cetovimutti = heart's release, termbase ✓). No em dash ✓.
- Confidence flags: "body and mind" is a deliberate accessibility gloss of 五蘊; reviewer may prefer "all that you are."
- Net: clean for review.

### zh (Traditional)
- Back-translation: "Contemplate body and mind as impermanent; contemplating thus gives rise to disenchantment, greed-desire ceases, and the mind attains liberation."
- Findings: uses the sūtra's own terms 無常 / 厭離 / 心解脫 ✓. Traditional script ✓.
- Net: clean for review.

### hi
- Back-translation: "See body and mind as impermanent; seeing thus, dispassion arises, greed calms, and the mind becomes free."
- Findings: अनित्य = *anicca* ✓; विरक्ति = disenchantment; मुक्त = liberation. Faithful to the chain.
- Net: clean for review.

### ne
- Back-translation: "See body and mind as impermanent; seeing so, dispassion arises, greed calms, and the mind becomes free."
- Findings: parallels Hindi; अनित्य per termbase.
- Confidence flags: naturalness of विरक्ति vs a plainer Nepali term.
- Net: needs native review.

### bo ⚑
- Back-translation (approx): "If you see body and mind as impermanent, weariness arises and craving is exhausted, so the mind is fully liberated."
- Findings: མི་རྟག་པ = *anicca* ✓; སེམས་རྣམ་པར་གྲོལ for 心解脫/cetovimutti (termbase ⚑ སེམས་རྣམ་གྲོལ / རྣམ་གྲོལ). སྐྱོ་ཤས for "disenchantment" is a reasonable default.
- Confidence flags: term for disenchantment (厭離); exact form of cetovimutti.
- Net: escalate to native Tibetan dharma reviewer.

### mn ⚑

- Findings: хувирамтгай = *anicca* ✓; сэтгэлийн чөлөөлөл family for cetovimutti (termbase ⚑). залхрал for disenchantment is proposed.
- Confidence flags: disenchantment and cetovimutti renderings unratified.
- Net: escalate to native Mongolian dharma reviewer.
